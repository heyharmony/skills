# Internal Communications

> Clear, engaging updates that build trust and drive action.

## What It Does

Internal Communications helps you craft messages that land with your team and company. Whether you're sharing a quick progress update (3P format), announcing a major change, responding to an FAQ, or building a recurring newsletter, this skill helps you choose the right format, nail the tone, and structure your message so it's clear and actionable. It works with meeting transcripts and real data, so your comms feel authentic and grounded.

You end with a ready-to-send message that builds transparency and momentum.

## Who It's For

- **Project and Product Leads:** Sharing status updates, launches, and decisions with leadership and cross-functional teams
- **Engineering Managers:** Communicating technical progress, roadmap changes, and incidents clearly to both technical and non-technical audiences
- **CEOs and Executives:** Drafting all-hands announcements, company newsletters, and strategic direction updates
- **Operations and HR:** Creating FAQ responses, policy announcements, and organizational changes that are sensitive and clear
- **Marketing and Communications:** Building narratives around products, milestones, and company updates for internal audiences
- **Team Leads:** Writing weekly or monthly syncs that keep stakeholders informed and aligned

## Use Cases

- **Weekly Status Update:** Turn your standup notes and progress metrics into a crisp 3P update (Progress/Plans/Problems) that takes 30 seconds to read and 2 minutes to scan.
- **Product Launch Announcement:** Transform a product launch meeting into a celebration that explains what shipped, why it matters, and what customers should try next.
- **Company Announcement:** Draft a clear, empathetic announcement about a restructuring, new policy, or major decision that addresses the questions people will ask.
- **Newsletter:** Create a monthly company narrative that celebrates wins, shares context on strategic bets, and builds culture and trust.
- **FAQ Document:** Turn the 20 Slack messages you answer repeatedly into one clear, organized Q&A that you can link people to.
- **Incident Communication:** After a system outage or crisis, craft a message that's honest about what happened, factual about the impact, and clear on what's being done next.

## Setup

Install the skill. To write an internal communication:
1. Clarify your audience, format, and core message
2. Gather source material (meeting notes, transcripts, metrics)
3. Trigger the skill with *"write an update"* or *"draft an announcement"*
4. Pick your format (3P, newsletter, FAQ, status report, etc.)
5. Work through the steps; the skill pulls structure and examples
6. End with a copy-paste-ready message and a suggestion for next action

## What You Get

A polished internal message that's audience-appropriate, supported by real data and quotes, and ends with clear next steps. You'll have multiple format options (3P, newsletter, FAQ, status report, announcement) and real examples so you can adapt to your style. The skill also suggests what to do with your message—whether that's sending via email, posting in Slack, adding to your wiki, or scheduling for an all-hands presentation.
