# Business Model Canvas Builder

> Map your entire business in 9 boxes — and validate every assumption before you run out of money.

## What It Does

Creates a structured, visual business model that shows how your product creates, delivers, and captures value. When customer conversations exist in Harmony — discovery calls, sales recordings, interviews — the canvas is grounded in what customers actually said about their problems, willingness to pay, and how they work today. When it's not, it works from your context and asks the right questions to fill the gaps. The output is a narrative and visual business model ready to test with leadership, investors, or the market.

## Who It's For

- **Founders and CEOs** defining or pivoting their business model before fundraising
- **Product leaders** validating that the go-to-market strategy matches the underlying business assumptions
- **CFOs and finance leaders** stress-testing revenue and cost assumptions before major decisions
- **Board members** evaluating strategic pivots or new markets
- **Operations leads** identifying the key resources and activities that make the model work

## Use Cases

- Building a business model for a new product from scratch → Full 9-box canvas grounded in customer discovery
- Validating pricing assumptions before launch → Revenue section with unit economics (LTV:CAC, gross margin, profitability timeline)
- Pivoting after market feedback → Updated segments, channels, and value props with old assumptions clearly marked
- Stress-testing for board review → Scenario analysis showing what breaks if key assumptions are wrong
- Comparing multiple business models (direct sales vs. freemium) → Side-by-side comparison of segments, channels, and revenue

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with these integrations:

- **HubSpot / Salesforce** — Pulls customer segment data, deal sizes, win/loss records, and account history to validate customer segments and revenue assumptions
- **Gmail** — Surfaces customer language from email threads to inform value proposition and price-sensitivity
- **Slack** — Pulls internal discussions about pricing, go-to-market strategy, and product feedback
- **Google Drive** — References existing strategy docs, competitive analysis, and product roadmaps

## Setup

Install and go. Works best when your customer conversations live in Harmony — the skill pulls exact customer quotes to validate customer segments, value propositions, and pricing assumptions. Without conversation data, provide your research or market insights, and the skill will ask targeted questions to fill gaps.

## What You Get

A complete business model document with: executive summary, customer segments (primary and secondary), value propositions tailored to each segment, channels (discovery, acquisition, retention), key resources and activities, revenue streams with unit economics, fixed and variable cost structure, profitability timeline, and a ranked list of critical assumptions with validation methods. Includes scenario analysis showing how the model changes if key assumptions are wrong, and concrete next steps for validating the riskiest assumptions first.
