# Set OKRs & Goals

> **Strategy without goals is a hope. Goals without strategy are wishes.** Build both, then measure learning, not just grades.

## What It Does

This skill helps you set focused, ambitious goals that actually connect to company strategy and drive execution. Creates 3 strategic goals (not 15—forcing real prioritization), balances different types of metrics (hard numbers, soft measures, financial), builds systems so progress happens automatically, and reviews goals for learning (what did we discover about the market, our estimate, our execution?) instead of just grading (did you hit 100%?). The key insight: goals are a communication tool first, a measurement tool second.

## Who It's For

- **CEOs & Founders** defining the company's annual or quarterly direction and need it to be focused and achievable.
- **Chief Strategy Officers** translating strategy into measurable outcomes that teams can rally around and believe in.
- **VPs & Department Heads** cascading company goals to their teams and creating accountability without command-and-control.
- **Product Leaders** connecting product roadmap to business outcomes instead of just shipping features and hoping it matters.
- **Finance & Operations Leaders** ensuring goals align with financial model, unit economics, and resource allocation.
- **Team Leads** helping your team understand how their work contributes to company strategy; creating clarity.

## Use Cases

- **Annual or quarterly planning:** You need to define what matters and align everyone without micromanaging or creating conflict.
- **Strategy just shifted:** New market, new product, new customer focus. Goals operationalize the shift and signal what changed.
- **Last year's goals didn't work:** Too many (15 goals = no priorities), too vague (improve quality = no measurement), no one tracked them. Reset with discipline.
- **Teams are moving in different directions:** Sales optimizing for bookings, Engineering optimizing for quality, Product optimizing for features. Competing goals destroy alignment.
- **You're about to hire or reallocate budget:** Where does the money go? Goals determine allocation. Otherwise it's politics.
- **Mid-quarter course correction:** Goals aren't aging well. Market shifted, learning changed assumptions. Adjust the target, don't abandon the goal.
- **Learning from misses:** You aimed for 100%, hit 60%. Instead of shame, ask "what does this teach about our market, estimate, or execution?"

## Connectors

Works standalone—you need clarity on strategy and willingness to measure.

Richer with:
- **Notion / Confluence:** Create centralized goal tracker, strategy cascade, narrative updates, searchable archive
- **Salesforce:** Map sales goals to revenue targets, customer segments, expansion metrics; enable forecasting
- **Linear / Jira:** Link work (tickets, epics) to OKRs; measure what actually shipped toward each goal
- **Google Sheets:** Simple goal tracker, metrics updates, progress dashboard, baseline vs target
- **Slack:** Weekly goal update reminders; surface blockers; normalize discussing goals and learnings

## Setup

No special setup needed. Bring:
- **Clear strategy:** One paragraph on what you're optimizing for (growth? profit? product quality?) and what you're willing to trade
- **Baseline metrics:** Where you are now for each strategic priority (revenue, NPS, velocity, etc.)
- **Goal owners:** VPs or heads of function accountable for outcomes (one owner per goal)
- **Willingness to say no:** Limiting to 3 goals forces real prioritization. If you keep 5-10, you haven't prioritized.
- **Commitment to review:** Monthly check-in (20 min) and quarterly deep-dive (90 min) on progress and learning

## What You Get

- **3 focused strategic goals** (not 15) that drive all work and every decision; forced prioritization
- **Absolute targets** (not ratios, not percentages) that can't be gamed via denominator manipulation
- **Mixed metrics** (hardcore numbers + soft measures + financial) so you're measuring what actually matters
- **Systems and practices** embedded alongside goals (so progress happens automatically, not heroically)
- **Monthly review cadence** to stay on track and surface blockers early (not quarter-end surprises)
- **A learning culture** where missing goals is data (what were we wrong about?), not shame
- **Aligned execution:** Teams know how their work contributes to company success; reduced politics and conflict
- **A historical record:** Archive of goals and their outcomes; next year you use this to improve estimation and strategy

## How to Use This Skill

1. **Start with strategy, not goals.** Ask leadership: "What are we optimizing for?" If you don't have a clear answer, your goals will be a conflicting wish list. Fix strategy first.
2. **Limit to 3.** This is hard. But 3 goals means real prioritization; 10 goals means nothing matters. If you "need" more than 3, you haven't prioritized.
3. **Use absolute targets.** "Increase revenue 30%" can be gamed by shrinking the denominator. "Reach $50M revenue" is absolute and real.
4. **Mix metric types.** Hardcore (revenue), soft (NPS), financial (margin). You'll get a full picture of how you're doing.
5. **Review monthly.** 20-minute sync: are we on track? Do we need to adjust? Monthly rhythm prevents quarter-end scrambles.
6. **Learn from misses.** You aimed for 100%, hit 60%. Don't shame. Ask: "What did we learn about the market, our estimate, or our execution?" That's the insight.
