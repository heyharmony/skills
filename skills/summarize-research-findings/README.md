# Summarize Research Findings

> Turn scattered user research into a structured brief with confidence ratings and actionable next steps.

## What It Does

Synthesizes insights from Harmony meeting transcripts (user interviews, customer calls, discovery sessions) and external research documents into a cohesive narrative. Extracts verbatim quotes, clusters findings into themes, rates evidence strength, flags contradictions, and recommends validation or product actions. Converts raw research into a 5–10 minute executive read.

## Who It's For

- **Product Managers:** Consolidate user feedback from sprints into a brief that grounds roadmap decisions in actual user needs, not hunches.
- **Design Researchers:** Run discovery sessions, capture insights in real time from Harmony transcripts, and hand off structured findings to the design team within hours.
- **UX/Product Designers:** Understand what users actually said (via verbatim quotes) before sketching solutions, and track evidence strength so you don't over-weight a single outlier.
- **Customer Success Leaders:** Extract themes from quarterly customer advisory meetings and user feedback to feed back to product, ensuring customer voice shapes the roadmap.
- **Market Researchers:** Combine internal user research with external reports to identify white space and validate market opportunity.
- **Startup Founders:** After a round of founder-led customer interviews, quickly extract patterns to know what to build next and what to validate further.

## Use Cases

**Before:** Ran 6 user research interviews. Notes are scattered in Harmony, Slack, and a shared doc. Team has a 1-hour sync in 2 days. No clear consensus on what users want.
**After:** Skill generates a 2-page research brief with 4 key themes, evidence ratings, contradictions, and 3 recommended next steps. Everyone reads it in 10 minutes. Roadmap conversation is grounded.

**Before:** Customer Advisory Board met last week. 12 customers gave feedback on pricing, features, and roadmap. Capturing insights feels like herding cats; team doesn't know if feedback is signal or noise.
**After:** Pull Harmony transcript, run skill, get structured brief with strong/moderate/weak evidence tags. Know which themes came from 8/12 customers (strong) vs. 1 customer (weak). Confidence boost.

**Before:** Onboarding a new PM. She needs to know what research exists, what users care about, and what was already validated. Takes a week of digging.
**After:** Give her the research brief. She reads it in 20 minutes, sees which findings are validated (5+ sources) and which are still exploratory (1–2 sources). Ramp time cut by 80%.

**Before:** Leading a competitive analysis. Gathered 3 reports on market trends + ran 2 interviews with power users. Data points are everywhere; not sure which insights are most credible.
**After:** Skill rates evidence strength (reports are dated, interviews are fresh) and clusters themes. Know which competitive moves are real threats vs. hype.

**Before:** Quarterly planning time. Customer feedback exists but is buried in support tickets, Slack threads, and old meeting notes. Worried you're missing signal.
**After:** Aggregate Q1 research (interviews, support ticket trends, net-new feature requests) into one brief. See what customers consistently asked for across channels. No surprises at planning time.

## Connectors

**Works standalone.** You provide Harmony transcript links or upload PDFs.

**Richer with:**
- **Notion:** Store research briefs in a portfolio database. Tag by theme, evidence strength, or date. Build a searchable research repository.
- **Google Drive:** Export brief as a shareable doc. Collaborators can comment and vote on recommended next steps. Easy to archive and version.
- **Slack:** Post the executive summary and key themes to a dedicated #research channel. Alert PM and design lead when a new brief is ready.
- **Confluence:** Embed brief in a living research wiki. Link to related briefs, roadmap updates, and follow-up tests so context compounds over time.

## Setup

Install and go. Paste Harmony transcript links or upload research documents. No API keys, no configuration.

## What You Get

A structured research brief in under 5 minutes. Includes: executive summary (the headline), key themes with verbatim quotes and evidence strength ratings, contradictions and gaps you might have missed, a source quality assessment (how much you should trust this brief), and a prioritized list of validation and product actions. Every finding is tied to a specific speaker and session, so debates end with "here's what they actually said" instead of "I think they meant…"
