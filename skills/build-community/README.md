# Build Community

> A great community doesn't scale with size—it compounds with engagement depth. 50 passionate members beat 5,000 inactive ones.

## What It Does

Community is not a marketing channel—it's a product. This skill builds communities that compound by identifying early members, establishing culture fast, creating escalating roles that reward contribution, programming regularly, and moderating relentlessly. It measures engagement depth (not just size), tracks impact on retention and referral, and identifies when a community is thriving vs. declining.

## Who It's For

- **Founders & Heads of Product**: Want to build a moat and increase LTV through community. Pain point: Launched community, got 1k members, but no engagement; unclear if it's working.

- **Community Managers**: Building from scratch or reviving stalled communities. Pain point: Unclear what drives engagement; moderation feels overwhelming; can't show ROI.

- **Growth Leaders**: Want community as referral and retention lever. Pain point: Community feels separate from business metrics; can't prove its impact.

- **Customer Success Teams**: Using community to reduce support load and increase customer success. Pain point: Members ask same questions repeatedly; no knowledge base.

- **Product Managers**: Want to understand user behaviors and feedback at scale. Pain point: Best insights come from community, but unstructured; hard to act on.

## Use Cases

- **You're launching a community and want to avoid the "empty room" problem** → Recruit 50 founding members intentionally; establish culture from day 1

- **Your community has stalled engagement** → Diagnose: is it moderation, programming, or culture? Run targeted interventions

- **You want to measure whether community is actually creating value** → Track engagement depth, referral impact, retention differential, and LTV uplift

- **Different types of members should have different roles** → Design escalating roles (Member → Helper → Moderator → Ambassador) to reward growth

- **You want community members to help each other** → Design programming and mechanics that shift authority from you to members

- **You're deciding whether to even build a community** → Assess community-market fit first; not every product needs one

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with:

- **Slack / Discord**: Your community platform; connectors help measure engagement (message volume, thread participation, reactions)

- **HubSpot**: Segment community members by customer tier; identify power users and advocates for recruitment and programming

- **Google Analytics**: Track community impact: referrals from community, signup rates, member retention vs. non-members, LTV differential

## Setup

No setup required. Assess whether your product naturally creates peer value (if not, community won't compound). Recruit 50 founding members intentionally. Decide on platform (Slack for <2k, Discord for larger). Define 3-5 cultural norms and pin them. Design 2-3 member roles and communicate how to level up. Plan 8 weeks of programming. Start moderating from day 1. Track engagement weekly.

## What You Get

- Community-market fit assessment: does your product naturally support peer learning and connection?

- Founding member recruitment strategy: who to target, how to recruit, expected acceptance rate

- Culture and norms framework: 5 clear guidelines that establish tone from day 1

- Member roles and escalation path: from default Member through Helper, Contributor, Moderator, Ambassador with visible promotion criteria

- 8-week programming plan: events designed to build engagement and shift authority to members

- Moderation strategy: clear policy, team structure, response time targets, and metrics

- Engagement measurement framework: track engagement depth (% monthly active, posts per member, attending events) not just size

- Business impact analysis: how community affects customer retention, referral rates, LTV, and ROI

- Health dashboard: metrics to watch weekly and quarterly to know if community is thriving or declining

- Growth roadmap: how to scale from founding members to 5k+ without losing culture

## Key Insight

Communities compound differently than products. A product with 10k users and 2% active is dying. A community with 500 members and 30% monthly active is thriving. The 1-9-90 rule is real: 1% create, 9% contribute, 90% lurk. Design for all three. Don't force lurkers to participate. Give contributors recognition. Give creators status and leadership. The communities that win are the ones where the top 10% feel so invested that they help you recruit, moderate, and program. That's scale without burnout.
