# Draft Business Proposal

**Role**: Sales & Revenue | **Action**: Create

Generate tailored business proposals that mirror prospect language from discovery calls, demos, and stakeholder meetings. This skill transforms conversation insights into proposals that speak buyer language, address specific objections, and map solutions to stated problems.

## What It Does

Generic proposals fail because they treat every prospect the same. This skill mines Harmony transcripts to extract the prospect's own words—their stated problems, decision criteria, objections, and timelines—then builds a proposal around that language. The result: a proposal that feels written *for them*, not templated *at them*.

### Core Capability

- **Extract buyer language**: Automatically identify stated problems, success metrics, decision criteria, and concerns from discovery transcripts
- **Build problem-solution maps**: Connect each stated problem to your specific capability and the outcome it delivers
- **Structure for their decision framework**: Lead with ROI if that's what they care about; lead with risk mitigation if that's their concern
- **Preempt objections**: For each concern they raised, include evidence or a case study that neutralizes it
- **Tailor social proof**: Surface case studies and customer testimonials from their industry, size, and use case
- **Align timeline**: Commit to implementation dates based on their stated urgency ("before your September audit")

## When to Use

- Post-discovery call or demo (write while their language is fresh)
- RFP responses (map requirements to conversation context)
- Enterprise deals requiring multi-stakeholder buy-in
- Competitive deals (emphasize what they said they value)
- Renewal proposals with expansion opportunities
- Follow-up after a demo when the next step is "send us something"

## Quick Start

1. **Gather inputs**: Bring a discovery call transcript, demo recording notes, or stakeholder meeting summary
2. **Specify context**: What's their main problem? Who are the stakeholders? What's the timeline?
3. **Generate proposal**: The skill mines the transcript for language, builds a problem-solution map, and structures the proposal around their decision criteria
4. **Tailor & send**: Review the draft, customize pricing if needed, and send it the same day the conversation is fresh

## Inputs

- Discovery call, demo, or stakeholder meeting transcript (or detailed notes)
- Prospect company name, industry, size, and role of key stakeholders
- Your solution/product name and core capabilities
- Any pricing or term constraints
- Competitive alternatives they mentioned
- Timeline or urgency signals they shared

## Outputs

- **Executive Summary**: Their problem in their words, your solution, key outcome, timeline
- **Problem-Solution Map**: Visual mapping of each stated problem to your capability
- **Solution Detail**: Organized by their use cases (not your feature list)
- **Objection Handlers**: Each concern they raised, with evidence or case study
- **Pricing Section**: Framed per their decision criteria (ROI, risk, cost-per-unit)
- **Social Proof**: Case studies from similar customers
- **Implementation Timeline**: Phases and milestones aligned to their stated urgency
- **Next Steps**: Clear call to action and contact info

## Tips for Best Results

- **Use exact quotes** from transcripts—the more of their language in the proposal, the higher the resonance
- **Specify the stakeholder**: Different sections for Finance (ROI), IT (integration), Operations (timeline)
- **Lead with their timeline**: If they said "before September," commit to it and make it a headline
- **Address competitive alternatives**: If they mentioned two competitors, explain why you're different in ways they care about
- **Include metric-specific proof**: If they care about labor hours saved, show a case study with labor hour numbers
- **Don't wait for perfection**: Send the proposal the same day as the discovery call while language is fresh; you can refine over multiple iterations
