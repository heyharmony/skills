## Purpose
Brand consistency builds trust and professionalism. But translating brand guidelines into actual presentations, documents, and reports is often overlooked, leaving artifacts looking disconnected or amateurish. This skill helps you apply your company's brand identity consistently—from color palettes and typography to layout and tone of voice. If you don't have formal guidelines yet, this skill also walks you through defining one from scratch.

## When to Use
- Creating presentations (pitch decks, all-hands slides, investor decks, customer pitches)
- Designing reports or dashboards that need visual consistency
- Building templates for recurring artifacts (monthly updates, weekly syncs, project charters)
- Ensuring color, typography, and spacing consistency across documents and slides
- Onboarding a new team member on visual and tonal brand standards
- Auditing existing artifacts for brand compliance and identifying inconsistencies
- Creating brand guidelines from scratch if your company doesn't have formal ones yet
- Adapting brand guidance for new mediums (print, video, interactive formats)

## Instructions

### 1. Audit Your Current Brand (or Start from Scratch)
**If you have existing brand guidelines:**
- Gather your brand document, design system, or style guide (PDF, Figma, wiki, etc.)
- Extract the following elements:
  - Color palette (primary, accent, neutrals, with hex codes)
  - Typography (heading font, body font, sizes, weights, line heights)
  - Logo (primary version, secondary version, clear-space rules, size minimums)
  - Spacing and grid rules (margins, padding, column widths)
  - Imagery style (photography style, illustrations, filters, tone)
  - Voice and tone (formal vs. casual, active voice, key phrases, what to avoid)
  - Component library (buttons, cards, tables, headers, etc., if available)

**If you don't have guidelines:**
- Answer these foundational questions:
  - **What's your industry/category?** (e.g., fintech, SaaS, nonprofit, enterprise)
  - **What's your positioning?** (e.g., "accessible & friendly" vs. "premium & serious" vs. "innovative & cutting-edge")
  - **Who's your audience?** (e.g., executives, engineers, consumers, creators)
  - **What 3 adjectives describe your brand?** (e.g., "trustworthy, innovative, human" or "bold, playful, inclusive")
  - **What colors feel right?** Pick 2-3 colors that resonate with your positioning
  - **What's your tone?** How do you talk? (formal, conversational, technical, approachable, inspiring)

### 2. Map Brand Elements to Artifact Types
Different artifacts need different emphasis:

| Artifact Type | Color Heavy? | Typography Critical? | Logo Placement | Spacing/Grid |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Presentation deck | Yes (consistent background/accents) | Yes (heading hierarchy, readability) | Title slide + footer | Significant (slide margins, section breaks) |
| Document/Report | Moderate (headers, callouts) | Yes (heading/body distinction, scannability) | Header or letterhead only | Moderate (margins, section spacing) |
| Dashboard | Yes (data visualization colors) | Moderate (labels, legends, readability) | Optional (corner or omit) | Significant (card layouts, data density) |
| Social media graphic | Yes (attention-grabbing) | Yes (headline legibility) | Always included | Tight (limited space) |
| Email template | Moderate (header, CTA buttons) | Yes (subject line + body clarity) | Signature or header | Moderate (comfortable reading width) |
| Slide deck for executives | High (polish matters) | High (clear hierarchy, readable at distance) | Title slide + footer | Significant |

Assemble the pieces you'll reuse:

**Color Palette:**

**Typography:**
```

  - H2: [size]pt, [weight], [line-height], [color]
  - H3: [size]pt, [weight], [line-height], [color]

  - Body text: [size]pt, [weight], [line-height], [color]

```

**Logo:**
- Primary version (full logo with text) for general use
- Icon-only version (mark only) for small spaces, favicons, avatars
- Clear-space rule: minimum [Xpx] of breathing room around logo
- Minimum size: [Xpx] wide (below this, use icon version)
- Do not: stretch, rotate, change colors, add effects, use on busy backgrounds

**Voice and Tone:**
- **Formal:** Active voice, avoid contractions, precise language (e.g., "not isn't")
- **Conversational:** Use contractions, second-person "you," personality
- **Technical:** Precise terminology, avoid jargon unless audience is technical, explain concepts clearly
- **Approachable:** Friendly but not cutesy, relatable examples, acknowledge user needs
- **Examples of your voice:** [3-5 sentence examples that show how you talk]
- **Avoid:** [3-5 things you never say or do in brand messaging]

### 4. Apply to Your Artifact: Start with Structure
Choose your artifact type. Create a wireframe or outline first:
- **Presentations:** Define slide template (title, content, section breaks, footer)
- **Documents:** Define layout (headers, margins, column widths, callout styles)
- **Reports:** Define sections (title page, TOC, section headers, metrics/visualization areas)
- **Dashboards:** Define card layouts, metric displays, color-coding for status

Ensure white space and hierarchy are clear before adding content.

### 5. Apply Colors Purposefully
Avoid the "rainbow" trap (using all colors equally). Instead:
- Use primary color for: main CTAs, key headlines, brand emphasis
- Use secondary color for: supporting elements, secondary CTAs, section dividers
- Use accent color sparingly: alerts, important callouts, data highlighting
- Use neutrals for: body text, backgrounds, dividers, supporting text
- Use data colors consistently: same color for same categories across all charts

For charts and data: ensure color-blind friendly palettes (avoid red/green without additional distinction).

### 6. Apply Typography Hierarchically
Don't skip heading levels. Create clear visual distinction:
- **Highest level (H1):** Largest, boldest, most color emphasis (e.g., deck title, document title)
- **Section headers (H2):** Medium size, slightly less emphasis, consistent color (usually primary or neutral dark)
- **Sub-headers (H3):** Smaller, lighter weight, same family, clear distinction from H2
- **Body text:** Most readable size (typically 10-14pt), neutral color (dark gray or black), generous line-height (1.4-1.6)
- **Captions/labels:** Small, lighter weight, secondary color or neutral gray

Test readability: print your artifact at actual size and stand 3 feet away. Can you still read the hierarchy?

### 7. Lock Down Spacing and Layout
Consistent spacing builds visual rhythm. Define your grid:
- **Margins:** All sides [Xpx] or [Xcm] (e.g., 1 inch on all sides for documents, 0.5 inch for slides)
- **Padding inside boxes/cards:** [Xpx] (e.g., 16px inside a callout box)
- **Space between sections:** [Xpx] (e.g., 32px between major sections)
- **Space between elements within a section:** [Xpx] (e.g., 12px between list items)

Use a consistent grid (e.g., 8px or 12px) so all spacing is a multiple of your base unit.

### 8. Apply Logo and Visual Identity
- **Documents:** Logo in header (top left or center), small size (0.5-1 inch wide)
- **Presentations:** Logo on title slide (prominent), footer on all other slides (small, bottom right)
- **Reports:** Logo on cover page and header/footer of each page
- **Dashboards:** Logo in top-left corner (small) or omit if space is constrained

For imagery (if applicable):
- Use consistent photography style (if it's portraits, all portraits; if it's lifestyle, all lifestyle; if it's abstract, all abstract)
- Apply consistent filters or tone (if moody, all moody; if bright, all bright)
- Ensure images support your positioning and tone

### 9. Ensure Tone is Consistent
Review your copy:
- Are headers in active voice and action-oriented?
- Are you using your defined voice (formal, conversational, etc.)?
- Are you avoiding jargon (unless audience is technical)?
- Are you using second-person ("you") when appropriate?
- Do key phrases match your brand voice examples?

Rewrite anything that feels off-brand.

### 10. Audit and Iterate
Before finalizing:
- Print or view at actual size (not just on screen)
- Check all headings for readability and hierarchy
- Ensure color contrast meets accessibility standards (AA or AAA)
- Verify logo placement and sizing
- Confirm spacing is consistent throughout
- Get feedback from someone who knows your brand; do they recognize it?
- Take a screenshot and compare side-by-side with other branded artifacts; does it match?

Fix any inconsistencies before sharing.

### Application Checklist (for any artifact)

```markdown
# Brand Application Checklist

## Colors
- [ ] Primary color used for main CTAs and key emphasis
- [ ] Secondary color used for supporting elements
- [ ] Neutral text is dark enough to meet WCAG AA contrast standards
- [ ] Color palette is consistent with other branded artifacts
- [ ] No unexpected colors introduced (rainbow effect avoided)

## Typography
- [ ] H1 is used for main title only
- [ ] H2 and H3 follow logical hierarchy
- [ ] Body text is in approved font and size (readable at intended size)
- [ ] All fonts are from approved list (no random substitutions)
- [ ] Line heights are generous (1.4-1.6 for body, more for headers)

## Logo
- [ ] Logo is present (and sized appropriately for artifact type)
- [ ] Logo has adequate clear space around it
- [ ] Logo is not stretched, rotated, or color-modified
- [ ] Logo is high resolution (not pixelated)

## Layout and Spacing
- [ ] Margins are consistent (e.g., 1 inch on all sides)
- [ ] White space is adequate (not cramped, not empty)
- [ ] Section spacing is consistent throughout
- [ ] Grid is visible and organized (no random positioning)

## Voice and Tone
- [ ] Copy is in active voice
- [ ] No jargon that audience wouldn't understand
- [ ] Key phrases match brand voice examples
- [ ] Tone is consistent with audience and context
- [ ] Headers are action-oriented and clear

## Overall
- [ ] Artifact is recognizable as part of [Company Name] brand
- [ ] Artifact matches other recent branded work
- [ ] Artifact will be readable when printed or viewed at actual size
- [ ] No accessibility issues (contrast, text size, color-blind friendly)
```

## Edge Cases

- **No brand guidelines exist yet:** Use the "Audit" step to gather brand elements from founders, marketing, or product. If those are vague, start with the positioning questions and color/tone research. Document it as you go; this becomes your first brand toolkit.

- **Brand has changed or evolved:** Update the brand toolkit first. Then audit existing artifacts for outdated elements. You don't need to re-brand everything retroactively; moving forward, use the new guidelines.

- **Different artifact types have competing space constraints:** Prioritize readability. For tight spaces (social media graphics, dashboards), use logo sparingly or use icon-only version. For expansive spaces (reports, presentations), give brand more breathing room.

- **Your artifact needs to work on light AND dark backgrounds:** Test both. If primary color (e.g., bright orange) doesn't work on dark background, use a lighter variant or switch to secondary color. Document the rules for both.

- **You have no in-house design system or Figma file:** Create a simple PDF or Google Doc brand toolkit (template included above). Update it as you go. Share it with anyone creating company artifacts.

- **Color-blind accessibility matters:** Test your palette with a color-blind simulator (e.g., Coblis). Avoid red/green pairs without additional distinction (e.g., shape, pattern, text label). Use WCAG contrast checker for text.
