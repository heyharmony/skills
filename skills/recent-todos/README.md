# My Recent To-Dos

> See everything you've committed to — and everything others owe you — across all your recent conversations, so nothing falls through the cracks.

## What It Does

Scans your recent meetings and conversations to extract every commitment: what you promised, what others promised you, and what's overdue. Deduplicates across meetings, assesses priority from context (customer commitments rank higher than internal nice-to-haves), and presents a single, honest list organized by urgency. When Harmony conversations exist, it catches the commitments people make in passing — the "I'll look into it" and "let me send that over" that never make it into a task tracker.

## Who It's For

- **Anyone** who makes commitments in meetings and occasionally forgets them
- **Product managers** juggling promises across multiple stakeholder groups
- **Account managers** tracking what they owe customers vs. what customers owe them
- **Team leads** who need accountability visibility without micromanaging

## Use Cases

- Start of day: "What do I need to do today?" → Prioritized list of overdue + due-soon items across all recent conversations
- Before a 1:1 with your manager: "What have I committed to?" → Filtered list of open items with context on who's waiting
- End of week accountability check: "Did I miss anything?" → Full scan of the week's commitments with status assessment
- Feeling overwhelmed: "What's actually on my plate?" → Honest view of open items, separated from things others owe you

## Setup

Install and go. Works best when your meetings and conversations live in Harmony — the skill scans transcripts for commitment language ("I'll…", "let me…", "action on me to…") and tracks what was promised vs. what was delivered. Also picks up implicit commitments like unanswered questions and "let's discuss offline" moments.

## What You Get

A prioritized to-do list organized by urgency: overdue items first, then due soon, then open. Separate sections for your commitments vs. things others owe you. Each item includes who you promised, which meeting it came from, the deadline (if any), and priority level inferred from context. Ends with a summary highlighting your biggest risk item and offering to create tasks or send nudge messages.
