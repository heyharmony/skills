# User Research Synthesizer

> Turn scattered research notes into structured, evidence-backed insights your team can actually act on.

## What It Does

User Research Synthesizer transforms raw findings from multiple research sessions—interviews, usability tests, surveys, call recordings—into a clear, prioritized research report. It helps you:

- **Identify cross-session patterns** without losing the nuance of individual insights
- **Distinguish signal from noise** by tracking how many sessions support each finding
- **Separate behaviors from opinions** (what people do vs. what they say they do)
- **Map findings to user segments** so you can tailor solutions
- **Prioritize by evidence** combining frequency, severity, and business impact
- **Generate actionable recommendations** linked directly to research evidence
- **Flag contradictions and gaps** that need follow-up research

Instead of a 50-page transcript dump or scattered sticky notes, you get a structured research report that executives, product managers, and engineers can actually use to make decisions.

## Use Cases

### Redesign & Feature Prioritization
Run 8-10 user interviews about a product redesign. Instead of debating which feedback to trust, get a clear breakdown: "6/8 users struggled with navigation (observed), 7/8 want dashboard templates (stated), 2/8 mentioned mobile (not a blocker)."

### New Market Validation
Interviewed 12 potential customers about entering a new market? Synthesize interviews by segment, identify which problem is most acute, and validate willingness to pay.

### Usability Testing Synthesis
Conducted 5 moderated usability tests? Get a report that separates critical blocking issues from minor UX polish, with clips/quotes tied to each issue.

### Customer Feedback Consolidation
Weekly customer calls, support tickets, and survey responses piling up? Run a synthesis sprint quarterly to identify trends and separate one-off complaints from systemic issues.

### Post-Launch Research
After launching a feature, interviewed 15 users about adoption. Synthesize findings to understand: What worked? What confused people? Who's the real user?

### Competitive Research
Interviewed users at competitors' companies? Map their pain points, compare to your roadmap, and identify white space opportunities.

## Setup

**No configuration required.** Provide:
1. **Research session summaries or transcripts**
   - From interviews: participant role/company/use case + key quotes + observations
   - From usability tests: task success, time spent, confusion points, quoted feedback
   - From surveys: response counts + representative quotes + segment breakdowns
   - From recordings: transcript excerpt + key moments + speaker roles

2. **Session metadata** (optional but helpful)
   - Participant role, company type/size, experience level
   - Date, duration, method (interview vs. test vs. survey)
   - Any context about where they are in the customer journey

3. **Business context** (recommended)
   - What problem are you trying to understand?
   - Who are you designing for?
   - Any hypotheses you're testing?

## What You Get

A production-ready research synthesis report with:

- **Prioritized findings** ranked by frequency + severity + business impact
- **Evidence strength tracking** (how many sessions support each finding)
- **Segment mapping** (which user types have which needs)
- **Actionable recommendations** tied to specific evidence
- **Behavioral vs. preference distinction** (observed vs. stated)
- **Contradictions called out explicitly** with potential explanations
- **Research gaps and limitations** flagged for stakeholders
- **Follow-up research suggestions** with specific questions to validate

Output is structured markdown—copy directly into Confluence, Google Docs, or Notion, or use as input for your roadmap planning.

## Example: Real Research Report

**Input**: 8 in-depth interviews + 2 usability tests + survey of 20+ users about a B2B analytics platform redesign. Participants: Data Analysts, Analytics Managers, C-suite executives.

**Key Findings (auto-prioritized)**:
1. Navigation complexity blocks power users (7/8 interviews + 2/2 usability tests observed)
2. Managers want pre-built dashboards for exec reporting (6/8 interviews, 65% survey)
3. Data freshness expectations vary by role (4/8 interviews—real-time for analysts, accuracy over speed for managers)
4. Mobile is nice-to-have, not critical (2/8 interviews mentioned)

**Top Recommendation**: Reorganize navigation to surface advanced features (High priority—observed in 9/10 sessions, directly impacts efficiency).

**Bonus Insight**: While executives expected mobile to be critical, research showed it's low-priority. This saved the team from a quarter of mobile development.
