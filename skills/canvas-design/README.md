# Canvas Design

> Strategic visual communication that moves people and sells ideas without saying much at all

## What It Does

Create museum-quality visual artifacts that communicate complex ideas purely through design—color, space, form, and composition. This skill combines aesthetic philosophy with meticulous execution to produce pristine PDFs or PNGs that feel inevitable and intentional, not templated or generic. You describe the idea or message, we develop a clear design manifesto, then create the visual artifact that embodies it. Perfect for one-pagers, brand identity pieces, infographics, thought leadership posters, and strategic visuals.

## Use Cases

- **Brand Manifesto One-Pager:** Create a stunning visual statement of "who we are" that communicates company values, aesthetic, and positioning in one glance. Before: boring text-heavy brand guidelines. After: a visual artifact that makes brand real and memorable.

- **Annual Strategy Report:** Design a 2-3 page visual summary of yearly results, strategic initiatives, and forward vision. Before: PowerPoint slides with bullet points. After: a cohesive, publication-quality visual narrative that executives frame and reference.

- **Market Research Infographic:** Transform complex data insights (competitor analysis, market trends, customer segments) into a visual story with charts, icons, and color-coded zones. Before: spreadsheet with 50 columns. After: intuitive visual that sells the insight in 30 seconds.

- **Investor Pitch Deck Cover:** Design a distinctive, memorable cover page for your pitch deck that signals quality and intentionality before a word is spoken. Before: generic template. After: a custom visual that sets the tone for the entire pitch.

- **Product Capability Poster:** Create a visual hierarchy of product features, use cases, and benefits using asymmetrical composition and color coding. Before: feature list on website. After: a printable, shareable visual that makes product come alive.

- **Design System Identity:** Build a visual codification of your design language—color palette, typography rules, spatial principles, and component patterns—as a beautiful reference artifact. Before: scattered design files. After: a canonical visual guide everyone references.

## Setup

1. In Harmony (or chat), describe the visual you need and the idea it should communicate: "I need a one-pager that explains our go-to-market strategy using asymmetrical design"
2. Share any reference aesthetics, color directions, or tone preferences
3. Wait for the design philosophy to be articulated and the visual artifact to be generated
4. Download the PDF or PNG
5. Use in presentations, send to stakeholders, print and frame—it's ready to go

If you need updates, ask for revisions: "Make the headline larger" or "Switch the accent color to teal" and get a new version.

## What You Get

A gallery-ready visual artifact—PDF or PNG—that looks intentional, not templated. Includes custom color palette (locked to your visual identity), distinctive typography (not generic defaults), refined spacing and composition, and strategic use of imagery or illustration. Print-quality (300 DPI for paper) or screen-optimized (144+ DPI for digital). Everything is production-ready: color-managed, properly sized, professionally exported. Use immediately in pitch decks, printed materials, web, or as a standalone statement piece.
