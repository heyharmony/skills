# Blue Ocean Strategy Builder

> Escape the red ocean of brutal competition. Find uncontested market space where you compete on your own terms, not theirs.

## What It Does

Maps the competitive landscape, identifies the industry factors everyone assumes are important, then systematically asks: what should we eliminate, reduce, raise, or create? The result is a strategy canvas showing where white space exists — and how to position your product so you're not fighting competitors head-to-head. When customer conversations exist in Harmony, it grounds the analysis in what customers actually value (vs. what competitors claim matters). Without it, it works from your market research and asks strategic questions.

## Who It's For

- **Founders entering crowded markets** who want differentiation without massive capital spend
- **Product leaders** planning a repositioning or new product line that competes differently
- **Strategic planners** exploring adjacent markets or customer segments with less competition
- **Venture investors** evaluating whether a startup has genuine white space or is just another me-too
- **Executive teams** considering a strategic pivot because current market position is unsustainable

## Use Cases

- Crowded market (CRM, project management, API monitoring) → Strategy canvas showing how to compete on different factors entirely
- New product for adjacent segment → Value landscape comparing how primary and secondary customers value different factors
- Win/loss analysis shows pricing pressure but feature parity with competitors → Four Actions Framework showing what to eliminate/reduce to reduce cost and differentiate
- Considering a pivot; unsure if market is viable → Market size analysis + sustainability assessment to validate blue ocean isn't just untested (too small)

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with these integrations:

- **Salesforce / HubSpot** — Pulls win/loss data, competitor mentions in opportunities, and what customers say about alternatives to map the true competitive landscape
- **Gmail** — Surfaces customer language about what they actually value vs. competitor features they ignore
- **Slack** — Pulls team discussions about competition and customer feedback to inform the value landscape
- **Google Drive** — References existing competitive analyses so you avoid duplicate work and focus on white space

## Setup

Install and go. For strongest analysis, have customer conversations or win/loss data in Harmony — the skill pulls what customers actually value (vs. feature lists). Without conversation data, provide your competitive research and target customer segment, and the skill will help you build the strategy canvas.

## What You Get

A complete blue ocean strategy document with: competitive landscape map, current industry value factors, the Four Actions Framework (eliminate, reduce, raise, create), a strategy canvas showing your positioning, target customer profile, business impact assessment (cost, experience, economics), risk analysis, and the #1 assumption to validate first before major investment.
