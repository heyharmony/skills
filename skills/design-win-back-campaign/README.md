# Design Win-Back Campaign

> You can't keep every customer. But before you let them go, try a 4-email escalation that recovers 10–20% at lower cost than acquiring new customers.

## What It Does

A win-back campaign targets lapsed or churned customers with a carefully timed, escalating sequence designed to either re-engage them or gracefully let them go. Unlike regular email campaigns that assume your audience is interested, win-back campaigns are built for skeptics: people who've already lost trust or momentum with your product. This skill teaches you how to segment lapsed audiences (recently inactive vs. cold), design a 4–5 email escalation that respects declining interest (soft re-engage → social proof → incentive → final warning → sunset), write subject lines that work on cold audiences, set success metrics per email, and build automation rules that know when to stop trying. Done well, you recover 10–20% of lost customers at cost lower than acquisition, generate immediate revenue, and improve overall list health by removing permanently inactive addresses.

## Who It's For

- **eCommerce and DTC founders**: Your list naturally decays as customers buy once and never return. Win-back campaigns recover some of them before you lose them permanently.
- **SaaS product managers**: Churned trial users and canceled paid customers are your most reachable sales opportunities (they already know you). Win them back before acquiring expensive new customers.
- **Marketplace managers (B2C or B2B)**: Seller churn, supply-side dropout, or low-activity users hurt your network. A structured win-back campaign reactivates high-value sellers before sunsetting low-revenue ones.
- **Content platforms and memberships**: Members go dormant seasonally or due to life changes. A win-back campaign re-introduces your value and moves them back to activity.
- **Customer success and retention teams**: Before accepting a customer churn, try one final re-engagement sequence with a concrete offer.
- **List-cleaning and email ops teams**: You need a systematic process to identify inactive contacts, give them one final chance, and remove them from active marketing to improve sender reputation.

## Use Cases

- **DTC brand list decay**: You have 50K email subscribers, but 30K haven't purchased in 120+ days. You run a win-back campaign and recover $150K in revenue while improving engagement metrics for the rest of your list.
- **SaaS product churn**: 20% of your trial users who signed up 6 months ago never converted. You run a win-back campaign showing them new features and offering a $30 off 6-month annual plan. You recover 2–3% as new paying customers.
- **Seasonal re-engagement**: Winter sports brand sees significant summer churn (customers bought gear and left). You run a fall-specific win-back campaign ("Gear for winter hiking and skiing") when demand picks up again.
- **Marketplace seller reactivation**: 2,000 sellers on your platform haven't listed anything in 60+ days. You run a win-back sequence with "here's what's new, here's how to get more visibility, here's an exclusive offer." You reactivate 10% (200 sellers) who re-list and resume selling.
- **Community member re-engagement**: Your membership platform had 5K members pay for access. 1K went dormant after the first month. You run a re-engagement series with "here's what members are discussing," "see what you're missing," and "50% off renewal if you re-commit." You recover 15% (150 members).
- **Product update announcement to lapsed users**: You made a major product improvement. Before acquiring new customers, you win back lapsed users who left due to the old pain point. You send "we fixed the thing you complained about" + free trial extension.

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with:
- **HubSpot**: Pull engagement history and lifecycle stage data to identify lapsed contacts and segment by days-inactive and churn reason
- **Salesforce**: For B2B, pull customer churn data, last purchase dates, and account health scores to prioritize win-back (high LTV accounts first)
- **Slack**: Surface customer success and sales team discussions about why customers are churning to inform win-back messaging
- **Gmail**: Review past re-engagement campaigns to see what worked (subject lines, offers, timing) before you design a new one

## Setup

1. **Define "lapsed" for your business**: Days since last purchase (eCommerce), login (SaaS), or engagement (content). This varies by category. 90 days = typical; adjust based on your purchase cycle.
2. **Segment by recency**: Recently lapsed (30–60 days), mid-lapsed (60–120 days), long-term inactive (120+ days). Each segment has different re-engagement potential.
3. **Identify churn reasons** (if possible): Customer interviews, exit surveys, support tickets, or behavior patterns. Different reasons need different messages.
4. **Design the escalation sequence**: Soft re-engage (Email 1) → social proof + value reminder (Email 2) → incentive offer (Email 3) → final warning (Email 4) → sunset (Email 5).
5. **Write cold-audience subject lines**: Use curiosity ("Sarah, quick question"), social proof ("50K customers are back"), or specific value ("40% off your favorite items").
6. **Craft subject lines that work on skeptics**: "We miss you" doesn't work. "We noticed you haven't X in a while" works. "Why" works. Honest > manipulative.
7. **Set conversion targets per email**: Email 1 = reply rate, Email 3 = offer redemption rate, Email 4 = final action rate. Track actual vs. target.
8. **Define sunset rules**: After Email 4 with no engagement, move to "unengaged" list. Stop marketing emails. Improve list health.
9. **Test and optimize**: A/B test Email 2 and Email 3 (trust-building and offer messaging) in Month 2 based on Month 1 baseline.

## What You Get

- **Complete win-back playbook**: 4–5 emails fully written, with subject lines, body copy, CTAs, and timing. Ready to implement in HubSpot, Klaviyo, or your email platform.
- **Escalation sequence template**: Soft → social proof → incentive → final → sunset. Proven structure that respects declining interest and maximizes response before sunsettiing.
- **Cold-audience subject line frameworks**: Approaches that work when trust is low (personal + curiosity, social proof, honest, direct question).
- **Segmentation strategy**: Recently lapsed vs. long-term inactive. Each segment gets appropriate messaging depth and offer generosity.
- **Churn reason analysis**: If you know why customers left, you can address it directly ("we fixed that" or "we now have X you wanted").
- **Conversion targets**: Clear metrics for each email and the full campaign. You'll know immediately if the campaign is working.
- **Sunset automation rules**: When to stop trying, how to suppress unengaged addresses, and how to preserve list health.
- **Optimization roadmap**: Month-by-month testing plan (subject lines Month 2, offers Month 3, segmentation Month 4).
- **Revenue projection**: Based on segment size and target re-engagement rate, how much revenue should you expect to recover?

## Pro Tips

- Email 1 asks a question or requests feedback ("Tell us what's next for you"). This filters for people willing to engage. Low response rate is OK—you're qualifying, not selling.
- Email 3 with a concrete offer drives highest conversion. Make the offer specific and time-limited ("40% off, expires Friday"). Vague offers ("special deal inside") have low redemption.
- Spacing matters on cold lists. Regular campaigns = 3 days apart. Win-back = 5–7 days apart. Lapsed audiences are resistant; more space = lower unsubscribe rate.
- Subject lines are make-or-break for win-back. "We miss you" doesn't work. "Sarah, quick question" works. Personal, curious, or honest openers beat generic.
- Track unsubscribe rate per email. If Email 4 has >1% unsubscribe, soften the language slightly. Email 4 unsubscribes are expected (you're filtering for people who want to stay), but >1% means you're being too pushy.
- Define sunset rules BEFORE launching the campaign. "After Email 4, move to unengaged list" is clearer than deciding after you see the results.
- Don't offer discounts on every email. It trains customers to expect discounts and damages brand perception. Email 1 = free content. Email 2 = social proof. Email 3 = incentive. Email 4 = final incentive or non-financial CTA.
- Test Email 3 offer first. This email drives the most conversions, so get the offer right. Then test Email 2 subject line (trust-building, social proof).
- Lapsed customers have low open rates. If Email 1 opens at 15% while your regular campaigns get 25%, that's expected. Don't panic; focus on conversion rate among those who do open.

## Key Insight

Your lapsed customers are actually valuable targets—they already know your product and company. Winning back a lapsed customer is cheaper than acquiring a new one. But you have a limited window: after 4 touchpoints over 3–4 weeks, engagement drops sharply. Beyond that, you're wasting effort and damaging sender reputation. Design a clean escalation that moves them from soft re-engage to hard offer to final warning. Then let them go and clean your list. The customers who re-engage during this window will be loyal; the ones who don't won't be worth chasing.
