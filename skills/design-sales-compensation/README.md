# Design Sales Compensation

> Build comp plans that drive revenue growth and customer retention — align incentives, keep plans simple, and lock them in annually so reps trust the math.

## What It Does

This skill walks you through designing compensation that actually works and drives the right behaviors. You'll set base/commission splits that match market rates (50/50 for AE, 60/40 for SDR), calculate realistic quotas where 60-70% of reps can hit targets without superhuman effort, build accelerators that reward top performers without creating toxicity, add clawback provisions that align sales with retention, and use SPIFs (Special Performance Incentive Funds) strategically for specific quarterly behaviors. Most importantly, you'll keep the plan simple enough that reps can calculate their own commission in their head—complexity breeds resentment. Includes real templates (copy-paste ready), market benchmarks from Comp.ly and Radford, and worked examples for SMB, mid-market, and enterprise scenarios so you can adapt to your situation without guessing.

## Who It's For

- **Founders & CEOs** — Hiring first sales reps; need market-rate compensation to attract talent without breaking unit economics
- **VP Sales & Revenue Leaders** — Building or redesigning comp plans; want to align incentives to strategic goals (land, expand, retain)
- **People Leaders & Finance** — Managing comp budgets; need to understand commission mechanics and projected payroll
- **Sales Managers** — Managing reps on comp plans; need to explain how commission works and track earnings accurately
- **Operators** — Planning comp changes; want templates and examples for different scenarios (SMB vs. enterprise, SDR vs. AE)
- **Legal & Compliance** — Reviewing comp plans for clawback provisions, non-compete concerns, and regulatory alignment
- **Sales Reps** — Understanding how their comp plan works, calculating expected earnings, and identifying opportunities to maximize commission

## Use Cases

- **Hiring first AE** → Set market-rate OTE, establish base/commission split (50/50 typical), define quota methodology, lock in plan before offer letter
- **Scaling from 1 to 3+ reps** → Ensure quotas are fair by territory, add accelerators for top performers (1.5-2% above 100%), introduce team bonus for cross-sell
- **Experiencing unintended behaviors** → If reps are discounting excessively, closing small deals, or ignoring retention, adjust comp structure to fix behaviors
- **Launching new product** → Use SPIF (Special Performance Incentive Fund) to drive adoption; make new product adoption worth 25-50% extra bonus short-term
- **Reviewing comp at year-end** → Evaluate which behaviors commission drove, which quotas were realistic, which reps hit 60%+, and what to change for next year
- **Expanding to new geography** → Market rates differ; adjust OTE to stay competitive in new market without breaking unit economics; validate quota assumptions
- **Managing comp budget** → Forecast commission as % of revenue by quota attainment; know your top line before you budget payroll

## Setup

Research market rates for your geography and role using Radford, Comp.ly, or Salary.com (data matters). Decide base/commission split (50/50 for AEs is standard; 60/40 for SDRs). Calculate quota by dividing annual revenue target by number of reps; validate that 60-70% of reps can realistically hit it. Define clear commission tiers (0% below 75% quota, 1% at 100%, 1.5% above 100%; keep it mathematically simple). Document everything in writing with examples (rep hits 90% quota = X earnings). Get legal review on clawback provisions, especially for early-stage companies. Share draft plan with team for feedback; address concerns. Lock in final plan for the full calendar year; don't change mid-year.

## First Steps

1. Research market rates for your geography and role (use Comp.ly, Radford, or salary.com); know your competitive range before you make an offer.
2. Decide base/commission split: 50/50 is standard for AEs (50% base, 50% upside), 60/40 for SDRs (more base, lower upside).
3. Calculate realistic quota: annual revenue target divided by number of reps; validate that 60-70% of reps can hit it without superhuman effort.
4. Define commission tiers: 0% below 75% quota, 1% at 100%, 1.5% above 100%. Simple enough to calculate in your head.
5. Document everything in writing with examples; get legal review on clawbacks; share draft with team for feedback before finalizing.

## Key Insight

Comp plans drive behavior more than speeches or mission statements ever will. If you want reps to close big deals, reward big deals. If you want reps to expand existing customers, pay for expansion. If you want reps to care about retention, clawback commission from churned customers. Compensation is how you align incentives without constant management.

The second insight: keep it simple. The mistake most founders make is either being too vague about compensation (reps don't know what they'll earn) or too complex (commission formulas have five tiers and three bonus pools that nobody understands). Reps should be able to calculate their own commission in their head. If they can't, they won't trust it.

Template: 50% base, 1% commission above 75% of quota, 1.5% above quota, clawback if customer churns in first 90 days. Done. Transparent, simple, aligned. Reps who understand their comp plan will execute harder because they trust the math. Bad comp plans require constant management and create resentment. Good comp plans run themselves.
