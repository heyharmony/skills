# Coach Product Managers

> Develop high-impact PMs systematically through explicit competency frameworks and consistent feedback. Coaching is the primary job of product leadership—help your PMs reach their potential.

## What It Does

This skill teaches you to develop product managers through structured coaching built on explicit standards. You'll define what good looks like in a competency framework, diagnose where each PM actually sits using Bloom's taxonomy, get aligned on a shared vision of growth, create specific development plans with clear activities, and follow up relentlessly so improvement sticks.

Rather than vague feedback ("work on execution") or annual reviews that surprise people, this skill creates a continuous coaching culture where PMs know what excellence looks like and have regular feedback on how they're progressing toward it.

## Who It's For

Product leaders and managers responsible for developing product managers:

- VPs of Product and Directors managing PM teams
- Engineering leaders working with product teams and wanting to understand PM development
- Managers new to the PM management role
- Anyone building product organizations and wanting to systematize development
- Product leaders scaling teams and needing to develop PMs who can scale with you

## Use Cases

- **Developing underperforming PMs:** Diagnose whether they lack skill, understanding, or execution, then coach accordingly
- **Building your first product team:** Create a framework for what good looks like and help new PMs reach it
- **Accelerating PM ramp time:** Move PMs from competent to excellent faster with structured coaching
- **Preparing PMs for promotion:** Help them build skills required for the next level before they're promoted
- **Retaining top talent:** Show high performers they're being invested in and have a clear growth path
- **Building PM bench strength:** Develop junior PMs into directors who can lead other PMs
- **Addressing recurring mistakes:** When PMs make the same error repeatedly, coaching can change the pattern
- **Creating accountability:** Use competency frameworks to create transparency about expectations and progress

## Setup

1. Create an explicit competency framework for PMs in your organization (5-7 core competencies)
2. For each PM, diagnose current state using Bloom's taxonomy
3. Get aligned on what excellence looks like using your competency framework
4. Create a shared vision of growth (specific, observable, time-bound)
5. Build a development plan with coaching conversations, assignments, and feedback cycles
6. Follow up weekly on progress
7. Celebrate improvement publicly and adjust the plan as the PM develops

## What You Get

- A competency framework that makes expectations explicit (not vague)
- PMs who know what excellence looks like and how they're progressing toward it
- Faster ramp time for new PMs because you're coaching intentionally
- Higher retention of strong PMs because they feel developed and supported
- Fewer repeated mistakes because feedback is frequent and specific
- A scalable model for developing PMs as you grow
- Clarity on which gaps are coaching problems vs. hiring/fit problems
- Documentation of PM development that becomes institutional knowledge, not dependent on one leader's memory
