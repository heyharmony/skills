# Design Referral Program

Turn organic customer advocacy into a scalable growth channel.

## Overview

This skill mines advocacy signals from Harmony customer call transcripts and designs a referral program that captures word-of-mouth growth. When customers say "I already told my colleague about you" or "can you give me something to share?", those are organic referral moments waiting to be systematized.

The skill guides you through:
- **Mining transcripts** for spontaneous advocacy mentions and NPS promoters
- **Identifying referrer personas** (who refers, when, and why)
- **Designing incentive structures** (two-sided rewards: referrer + referee)
- **Calculating viral coefficient** (K-factor) and setting growth targets
- **Building sharing mechanics** with minimal friction (email, link, in-app, social)
- **Launching and optimizing** from soft launch to scale

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when you are:
- Looking for scalable growth channels post-product-market fit
- Sitting on high NPS (50+) but no formal referral program
- Hearing informal referrals in customer calls that you want to capture and incentivize
- Launching a referral program for the first time
- Optimizing an existing underperforming referral program
- Planning a seasonal referral push (year-end, renewal cycles, product launches)

## Key Outputs

The skill produces seven deliverables:

1. **Advocacy Signal Report** — Quantified mentions of organic referrals from transcripts, segmented by customer type and timing
2. **Referrer Persona Cards** — 2–3 detailed personas capturing who refers, when, why, and how often
3. **Incentive Design Matrix** — Comparison of 2–3 incentive models (cash vs. credit vs. feature unlock) with cost and appeal analysis
4. **Viral Loop Blueprint** — K-factor calculation, payback period, growth targets, and scaling assumptions
5. **Sharing Mechanics Spec** — Channel design (email, link, in-app, social) with friction reduction and tracking setup
6. **Referral Landing Page Outline** — Headlines, value props, proof points, and CTAs optimized for referee conversion
7. **Launch Plan** — Phased rollout (soft launch → expand → optimize → scale) with metrics and iteration triggers

## Example: BrightPath (Online Tutoring)

**The Problem:** BrightPath has strong NPS (9s and 10s), but only 5% of new customers come from referrals. Parents frequently mention recommending to friends in calls, but there's no program to capture it.

**The Solution:**
- Identified Sarah (Parent Advocate): Refers 2–3 families per year, especially October–December (college prep season)
- Incentive: Referrer gets $25 account credit; referee gets first lesson free
- Viral target: Increase referral source from 5% to 15% of new signups in 90 days; K-factor from 0.1 to 0.5
- Mechanics: In-app widget for email and link sharing; pre-written email templates with friend's name
- Launch: Soft-launched to 25 top advocates, then expanded to all users; now tracking weekly referral volume and conversion rate

## How to Use

1. **Gather transcripts** — Collect recent Harmony customer call transcripts (30–50 calls, past 6–12 weeks)
2. **Run the skill** — Follow the 8-step instructions to mine signals, design incentives, and build your launch plan
3. **Validate with your team** — Review outputs with Marketing, Sales, and Product; test top 2–3 incentive models with a small cohort
4. **Soft-launch** — Invite your top 20–30 advocates to test; iterate based on feedback
5. **Measure and scale** — Track K-factor, conversion rate, and payback period weekly; optimize by referrer segment

## Tips for Success

- **Start with your best customers** — Top NPS advocates are lower friction to recruit and have larger networks
- **Make sharing frictionless** — No forms, no limits, one-click email or link; every additional step kills conversion by 30–50%
- **Avoid discounting** — Referral incentives should feel like bonuses or status, not discounts. Offers like "10% off" cannibalize full-price customers
- **Seasonal targeting** — Tie referral pushes to product launch cycles, renewal periods, or industry seasonality (e.g., back-to-school, year-end budgets)
- **Close the loop** — Use CRM tagging to track referrals from first invite through conversion; connect referrer and referee so you can measure impact on LTV and retention
- **Iterate fast** — Launch with one incentive model; after 2 weeks of data, test a variant; winners get sustained investment

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Too much friction** — Forms, approval processes, or reward delays kill participation. Aim for <2 clicks from invite to share
- **Mismatched incentives** — A $5 credit for a $1,000 enterprise deal won't motivate referrals; test and adjust weekly
- **Self-referrals** — Enforce email domain blocking and IP checks to prevent fraud
- **Ignoring compliance** — GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy laws restrict mass email outreach. Use double opt-in and link-only sharing
- **Cannibalization** — Cash-heavy referral programs convert customers who would have paid full price. Prefer non-monetary incentives and tiered payouts
