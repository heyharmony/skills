# Align Stakeholders

> Build consensus across diverse stakeholders by understanding how they think, surfacing disagreement early, and making deliberate trade-offs. Transform stakeholder chaos into coordinated support for your decisions.

## What It Does

This skill teaches you to build alignment before you need it, not after decisions fall apart. You'll learn to approach stakeholders with genuine curiosity instead of trying to convince them, understand their constraints and mental models, present options with recommendations that make trade-offs visible, distinguish real disagreement from misunderstanding, build trust through consistency, and repeat messages relentlessly until understanding sticks across diverse audiences.

Rather than treating alignment as something you do once (present decision, hope people agree), this skill treats it as ongoing: get pre-alignment on strategy, maintain trust through consistent follow-up, and communicate differently to different audiences based on their needs and role.

## Who It's For

Anyone whose ideas need support from people with different incentives and constraints:

- Product leaders getting buy-in from executives, engineering, and sales
- Engineering leaders aligning across infrastructure, platform, and product teams
- Founders getting alignment from board, investors, and key hires
- Managers making decisions that affect multiple teams
- Individual contributors proposing cross-functional initiatives
- Leaders navigating organizational politics without compromising the work

## Use Cases

- **Launching major initiatives:** Get alignment from multiple teams on strategy and trade-offs before you start
- **Making strategic pivots:** Build consensus when changing direction across a skeptical organization
- **Communicating bad news:** Help stakeholders understand difficult decisions and constraints
- **Resolving disagreement:** Distinguish disagreement from misunderstanding and find real trade-offs
- **Getting executive buy-in:** Present options with recommendations so executives can decide confidently
- **Building trust with stakeholders:** Show you understand their constraints and are designing around them
- **Scaling decisions:** Communicate the same decision to diverse audiences (executives, team leads, individual contributors)
- **Managing organizational transitions:** Build support when restructuring teams, changing vendors, or shifting company strategy

## Setup

1. Identify key stakeholders and understand how they think (not just what they think)
2. Get pre-alignment on strategic frame before proposing specific tactics
3. Present multiple options with recommendation to make trade-offs visible
4. Approach disagreements with curiosity, not conviction
5. Build trust through consistent follow-up and honest communication
6. Map concentric circles of communication for different audiences
7. Create repetition plan so messages stick across diverse groups
8. Frame requests as trade-offs to show opportunity costs

## What You Get

- Faster decisions because stakeholders understand and support the direction
- Reduced rework because misalignment is caught before execution
- Fewer surprises mid-project because expectations were set upfront
- Stronger relationships with stakeholders who feel heard and respected
- More durable decisions because consensus is real, not performative
- A reusable alignment process that scales as your organization grows
- The ability to navigate organizational politics without sacrificing your credibility or integrity
- Stakeholders who become advocates for your decisions, not reluctant participants
