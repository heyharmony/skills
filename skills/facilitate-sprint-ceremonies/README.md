# Facilitate Sprint Ceremonies

## Overview

Sprint ceremonies are the heartbeat of agile delivery, but they often become ritual without feedback loops. This skill transforms your standups, planning, reviews, and retrospectives from status meetings into continuous improvement engines by analyzing patterns across ceremony recordings and transcripts.

## What This Skill Does

Analyzes 2–3 sprints of ceremony data to surface:

- **Standup effectiveness**: Duration, participation balance, blocker resolution velocity
- **Planning quality**: Committed vs. delivered scope, story definition gaps
- **Review engagement**: Stakeholder attendance, feedback actionability, demo rehearsal
- **Retro follow-through**: Action item creation and resolution tracking
- **Recurring blockers**: Issues raised 3+ times without resolution
- **Ceremony health score**: 1–10 diagnostic across all four ceremonies

## When to Run This Skill

- At sprint retrospectives to close the improvement loop
- During sprint planning to reference prior-sprint insights
- When auditing standup effectiveness after team complaints
- After a failed sprint to diagnose what broke
- When onboarding a new scrum master
- Quarterly for trend analysis and structural assessment

## Key Outputs

- **Ceremony Health Dashboard** with scores and radar charts
- **Standup Analysis**: time, participation, blockers, resolution
- **Retro Action Item Tracker**: status of every action created
- **Recurring Theme Report**: ranked blockers and debates
- **Sprint-over-Sprint Trends**: 3–5 sprint historical view
- **Prioritized Recommendations**: with rationale and next steps

## How to Use

1. Gather transcripts or notes from standups, planning, review, and retro for 2–3 recent sprints
2. Trigger this skill with the sprint data (or list of meeting links/dates)
3. Skill analyzes patterns and generates a diagnostic dashboard
4. Use recommendations to adjust ceremony format, timing, or facilitation
5. Re-run quarterly to track improvements

## Who Should Use This

- Scrum masters and agile coaches
- Engineering leads wanting to optimize team velocity
- Teams struggling with ceremony effectiveness
- Organizations scaling beyond 1–2 squads (where ceremonies break)
