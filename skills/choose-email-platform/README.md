# Choose Email Platform

> Evaluate and recommend an email marketing platform tailored to your business model, list size, and growth trajectory — cut through vendor hype with a weighted comparison, TCO analysis, and clear recommendation.

## What It Does

Choose Email Platform systematically evaluates leading email service providers (Klaviyo, Mailchimp, Kit, beehiiv, ActiveCampaign, Sendlane, HubSpot Email, and others) against your specific needs. It builds a weighted comparison matrix scoring each platform on automation depth, deliverability reputation, pricing at your current and projected list size, ease of use, integrations, and analytics. You get a ranked recommendation (e.g., "Klaviyo at 87/100 is the best fit for your DTC Shopify brand") with ROI analysis, migration timeline, and total cost of ownership over 3 years.

No more second-guessing whether Mailchimp Free is "good enough" or whether Klaviyo's premium pricing justifies itself. This skill gives you data-driven confidence.

## Who It's For

- **Marketing leaders and CMOs**: Choose the right platform before the team commits time and training.
- **Email marketers**: Understand why your manager chose platform X and what to expect from platform migrations.
- **Operations and RevOps teams**: Ensure the platform integrates with your existing stack (Shopify, Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.).
- **Founders and CFOs**: Assess email platform costs and ROI before scaling email team.
- **Growth and product teams**: Evaluate platforms for transactional email, user engagement, and lifecycle campaigns.

## Use Cases

- **Starting a new email program**: You have no ESP yet and need to choose one from scratch based on business model.
- **Outgrowing your current platform**: Your current tool's limits (pricing, features, automation) are becoming a bottleneck; time to evaluate alternatives.
- **Vendor proposal evaluation**: An ESP sales team is pitching hard; you want to know how they compare objectively.
- **Budget pressure**: Your current platform costs are rising; you want to evaluate cheaper alternatives without sacrificing capability.
- **New channels or use cases**: You want to add SMS, landing pages, or omnichannel automation and need to evaluate one platform vs. multiple.
- **Post-acquisition consolidation**: You've acquired a company with a different ESP; need to pick which platform both teams should use.
- **Team growth and collaboration**: Your current platform's UI or collaboration features don't scale with your team; need something more mature.

## Setup

1. Gather information about your business model (DTC, B2B SaaS, newsletter, agency, etc.).
2. Document current list size, projected growth in 12 and 24 months, and current pain points with existing platform.
3. Identify must-have features (e.g., Shopify integration, SMS bundling, API access).
4. Confirm annual budget available for email platform and add-ons.
5. Share current tech stack (CRM, ecommerce platform, analytics, etc.) so integration requirements are clear.

## What You Get

- **Platform evaluation report** (2–4 pages) with executive summary, weighted scores, and ranked recommendations.
- **Comparison matrix**: Each platform scored on automation (20%), deliverability (20%), pricing (20%), ease of use (15%), integrations (15%), analytics (10%).
- **Total cost of ownership**: 3-year breakdown (platform fees, migration, training, add-ons) for top 3 options.
- **Migration timeline and complexity**: Week-by-week roadmap, effort estimates, risk assessment.
- **ROI case**: Quantified incremental revenue (if data available) or efficiency gains from switching.
- **Platform-specific sweet spots**: Why each platform wins for certain business models (e.g., "Klaviyo for DTC, ActiveCampaign for B2B SaaS").
