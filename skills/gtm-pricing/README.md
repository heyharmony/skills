# GTM Pricing Model Builder

> Find the price that balances customer willingness-to-pay, competitive positioning, and your margin needs — all in one framework.

## What It Does

Designs a pricing strategy grounded in customer feedback from discovery calls (willingness-to-pay, value drivers, budget ranges), competitive positioning, and your cost structure. Evaluates multiple models (value-based, cost-plus, competitive, freemium) and recommends the best fit. Designs tiered packaging (Starter, Professional, Enterprise) with pricing, features, and target customers. Includes economics validation so you know if the model works before you launch.

## Who It's For

- **Founders** launching a product and need pricing from day one
- **Product Managers** scaling a manual pricing model into packaged tiers
- **Go-to-Market leads** repositioning after market shift or moving into new ICP
- **Finance/Revenue Operations** optimizing pricing mix and margins
- **Sales leaders** explaining pricing to prospects and handling objections

## Use Cases

- New product launch, no pricing yet → Complete pricing strategy with 3 tiers, positioning, and economics
- Competitor pressure, "why are we so expensive?" → Value-based strategy that justifies pricing
- Moving upmarket from SMB to mid-market → Rebuild tiers to capture higher willingness-to-pay
- International expansion → Adjust pricing for new regions and competitive landscape
- Revenue optimization, "our LTV is too low" → Re-examine tier mix and packaging to improve ARPU

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with these integrations:

- **HubSpot / Salesforce** — Pull closed deal sizes and win/loss data to validate pricing assumptions
- **Gmail** — Surface price negotiation history and budget feedback from customer emails
- **Harmony** — Extract willingness-to-pay and price sensitivity from discovery call transcripts
- **Slack** — Share pricing framework and recommendation with leadership for collaborative input
- **Notion** — Pull competitor pricing research and strategic docs for positioning context

## Setup

Install and go. For strongest output, gather: cost structure (COGS, support costs), target gross margin, and discovery call feedback on budget/value. If you have Harmony, upload relevant discovery calls to extract willingness-to-pay signals.

## What You Get

A complete pricing strategy document: market analysis (customer willingness-to-pay from conversations), competitor pricing benchmarks, evaluation of 3–4 pricing models with trade-offs, recommended model with rationale, detailed tier structure (Starter/Professional/Enterprise) with pricing and feature matrix, pricing narrative and competitive positioning, economics projection with CAC/LTV/payback period, discount policy, and price increase roadmap for future.
