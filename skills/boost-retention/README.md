# Boost Retention & Engagement

> Every user you acquire is a leak in your bucket. Fix retention first, then optimize acquisition.

## What It Does

Retention is the metric that separates sustainable businesses from growth theater. This skill diagnoses why users leave by analyzing retention curves, identifying the single behavior that predicts long-term engagement, and pinpointing exact moments where users drop off (dead zones). It then builds targeted re-engagement campaigns and habit-formation interventions to keep users coming back, with a specific focus on at-risk segments before they churn.

## Who It's For

- **Product Managers**: Need to understand what behavior in week 1 actually predicts month 6 retention. Pain point: High CAC relative to lifetime value; DAU/MAU ratio stuck below industry benchmark.

- **Founders**: Discovering whether you have product-market fit. Pain point: Unclear if poor retention is a problem with the product or a problem with how users are being acquired/activated.

- **Growth Leaders**: Building sustainable growth. Pain point: Acquisition is working but churn is killing unit economics; don't know where to spend energy on retention.

- **Data Analysts**: Measuring engagement and predicting churn. Pain point: Lack clarity on which metrics actually move retention vs. vanity metrics.

- **Customer Success Teams**: Keeping high-value customers engaged. Pain point: No early warning system for churn risk; don't know which interventions actually work.

- **Support Leads**: Understanding churn reasons at scale. Pain point: Hear complaints one-off; need to identify patterns and feed them back to product.

## Use Cases

- **Your DAU/MAU is below 25%** → Analyze cohort retention curves, find your activation metric, and fix the exact moment users drop off

- **You're about to raise Series A** → Show investors that you understand retention; prove you've measured activation metrics and designed fixes

- **CAC is rising but LTV is flat** → Demonstrate that retention is your priority; show which product changes improved retention and why

- **Competitor has higher retention** → Reverse-engineer their user activation flow and identify what your product is missing

- **Your trial-to-paid conversion is low** → Map trial user retention; identify which behaviors predict paid conversion; design trial experience around those behaviors

- **You're losing power users faster than casual users** → Segment retention by engagement level; design survival strategies for each cohort

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with:

- **Google Analytics / Mixpanel**: Track cohort retention curves, DAU/MAU trends, measure exactly where users drop off in each flow

- **Slack**: Pull support tickets and user feedback to identify churn reasons and common frustration points in specific features

- **HubSpot**: Measure lifecycle stage transitions and design win-back campaigns for at-risk or churned accounts by plan type

- **Intercom**: Segment users for targeted re-engagement messaging based on engagement depth, last-seen data, and behavior patterns

## Setup

No setup required. Bring your product, your user data, and—ideally—some user research about why people leave. If you have access to analytics (Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or internal dashboards), you'll be able to measure retention curves by cohort. If not, this skill will help you design the metrics framework to track going forward.

## What You Get

- Baseline retention health: DAU/MAU, week-1 and month-1 retention, comparison to industry benchmarks and competitors

- Activation metric: the single week-1 behavior most predictive of 60-day retention, with correlation strength and segmentation by persona

- Dead zone identification: the exact moments in your product where users drop off most (onboarding, first feature use, first collaboration), with root causes from user research

- Engagement segmentation: your users divided into healthy, at-risk, soft-churned, and hard-churned buckets with clear retention targets

- Re-engagement playbook: 4 message variants for at-risk users, A/B tested to identify the hook that actually brings them back

- Habit-formation interventions: push notifications, email digests, and in-app nudges designed to build daily/weekly usage patterns without spamming users

- 90-day roadmap: prioritized experiments to fix dead zones, increase activation metric achievement, and reduce churn

## Key Insight

You can't grow your way out of poor retention. A product with 40% month-1 retention and $100 CAC will fail. A product with 60% month-1 retention and $30 CAC will compound. The single most important product question is not "How do we acquire more users?" It's "What behavior in week 1 predicts whether a user sticks around?" Once you answer that, you build everything toward that metric. The companies with the strongest retention don't blast users with notifications—they've designed their core experience so engaging that users naturally form the habit of returning.
