# Meeting Outcome Observer

> Stop watching commitments disappear. Every promise made in a meeting should show up in reality—or get called out as a miss you can fix.

## What It Does

Meetings create accountability, but follow-up is chaos. Someone said "I'll have the proposal by Friday," but Friday comes and the proposal is silent. Another team promised to ship a feature in Q2, but nobody's tracking whether they will. This skill automatically watches commitments made across meetings—promises to customers, internal deliverables, metrics targets—and compares them against follow-up conversations, status updates, and actual execution. It surfaces when promises drift: timeline shifted, scope changed, or silence swallowed it whole. Works from Harmony's multi-meeting context to flag accountability gaps before they become missed deadlines.

## Who It's For

- **Engineering Managers & Tech Leads** — Sprint commitments slip every other sprint. You need visibility: What did we promise? Are we tracking it? What's actually slipping and why?
- **Product Managers** — You're coordinating across teams and talking to customers. Promises get made (to customers, to stakeholders, to other teams). Track them.
- **Sales Leaders & Account Executives** — You promised the customer a feature, integration, or support level. Is it being delivered on time? Track it so you can alert the customer before they discover the miss.
- **Ops & Project Managers** — Your job is making sure commitments don't fall through cracks. This gives you a system to watch them.
- **Scrum Masters & Agile Coaches** — Sprint planning creates commitments. This shows you which teams consistently deliver vs. over-commit, so you can coach on estimation.

## Use Cases

- **Sprint planning vs. sprint review** — Made 10 commitments in Monday planning. It's Friday review. How many are shipped? How many slipped? Use this to improve team estimation.
- **Roadmap accountability** — You told stakeholders "We'll ship Feature X in Q2." Halfway through Q2, is it on pace? Is timeline at risk? Track it.
- **Customer success tracking** — You promised a customer "We'll onboard you by March 15 and have your integration live by April 1." Are those commitments tracking? Alert before they miss.
- **Executive commitment tracking** — CEO told the board "We'll reduce churn by 10% this year." Is the team making progress toward that metric? Track the underlying work commitments that ladder up to it.
- **Repeat miss pattern** — Same team keeps promising to do X, but X slips every sprint. Use this skill to make the pattern visible so you can coach on it.

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with these integrations:

- **Google Calendar** — Maps when each commitment was made and when it's due. Tracks attendees and owners across the meeting timeline.
- **Slack** — Cross-references meeting promises against standup updates and team discussions to catch when teams say they're "on track" but Slack shows they're stuck.
- **Linear / Jira** — Compares promised deliverables to actual tickets, due dates, and completion status. Surfaces when promises aren't even created as tasks yet.
- **Notion** — Tracks roadmap commitments, sprint goals, and strategic promises against documented progress.
- **HubSpot** — Monitors sales commitments (deal close dates, customer deliverables) vs. actual pipeline progression.

## Setup

Install and go. Works best when your meetings are recorded in Harmony and your team uses status-update channels (Slack standups, Linear updates, Notion boards) that the skill can cross-reference.

## What You Get

A commitment tracking report: catalog of every promise made with owner and due date, status of each (on track, at risk, missed), drift analysis showing what changed and why, accountability health scorecard (% delivered on time vs. late vs. missed), spotlights on critical misses, team accountability notes, and specific actions to unblock at-risk items or course-correct on missed ones. Designed to surface patterns so you can improve how your team makes and tracks promises.
