# Weekly Digest

> Your personal intelligence briefing. Everything that happened this week, organized and actionable.

## What It Does
**Weekly Digest** scans everything you've been part of—meetings, conversations, email threads, messages—and generates a single, comprehensive summary. It organizes what happened by impact (key decisions, action items, risks, wins), highlights what changed from last week, surfaces things you might have missed, tracks team sentiment, and projects what's coming next.

Instead of Monday morning spent scrolling through Slack, rereading emails, and asking "What did I miss?"—you get a structured briefing that's readable in 30 seconds, explorable in 5 minutes.

The digest comes as a clean, scannable markdown document you can read now, share with your manager, or reference throughout the week.

---

## Who It's For
**Everyone.** This skill works for:

- **Individual Contributors** – Catch up fast, stay informed, track your action items
- **Managers** – Understand team momentum, identify emerging risks, spot morale shifts
- **Executives** – Strategic overview of what's happening across your org
- **Remote Teams** – Async context without live meetings
- **People with packed calendars** – Efficiency when time is your scarcest resource
- **New team members** – Quick immersion into what's actually happening (not just what's planned)
- **Returning from PTO** – Jump back in without re-reading everything

## Setup

**Trigger Weekly Digest:**
- Say: *"Generate my weekly digest"* or *"What happened this week?"*
- Schedule: Set it to run every Monday morning at 8am
- On-demand: Anytime during the week when you want a refresh

**What It Reads:**
- Your meeting calendar and transcripts (if available)
- Conversations and email threads you're part of
- Decisions, commitments, and mentions that affect you
- Team communications and slack conversations
- Any Harmony-enriched data (sentiment, action items, risks identified in conversations)

**How Often:**
- Weekly (default): Runs every Monday morning
- Ad-hoc: Run anytime you need a catch-up
- After PTO: Run it when you return

## What You Get

### The TL;DR (30 Seconds)
2-3 bullet points capturing the most important things that happened. Is your project on track? Did something break? Did something win?

### Key Decisions
Everything that was decided this week. Who decided it. Why. How it affects you.

### Action Items
What you committed to (and when it's due). What others committed to that affects you. What got completed.

### Risks & Escalations
What could go wrong. What's already going wrong. Severity. Who's owning it.

### Wins & Progress
What's working. What shipped. What exceeded expectations. Momentum indicators.

### Upcoming Deadlines
Critical dates in the next two weeks. Tied to the decisions and action items above.

### This Week Ahead
Your critical meetings. Anticipated decisions coming. Team mood and energy.

### What Changed
How this week differs from last week. New risks that emerged. New opportunities. Scope changes.

### What You Might Have Missed
Decisions made in meetings you weren't in that affect you. Mentions of your work in conversations you weren't part of. Context you need to know.

## How It Handles Edge Cases

**Quiet Week?** The digest is honest: "Few decisions, minimal activity. Good week to focus on technical work."

**Overwhelmingly Busy?** The digest prioritizes ruthlessly: "40+ items this week. Here are your top 12. Full breakdown available."

**After Time Off?** The digest frontloads what you missed: "You were out March 5-9. Here's what changed in your absence."

**New to the Team?** The digest adds context: "Key players," "Team norms," "Why this matters."
