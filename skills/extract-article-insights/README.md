# Extract Article Insights

> Transform any web article into a structured knowledge card in minutes.

## What It Does

Read an article URL or pasted text, and get back a clean, structured insight card. The skill extracts the core thesis, maps supporting arguments with evidence, pulls meaningful quotes, and surfaces action items you can actually use. Perfect for research, competitive analysis, or sharing findings with your team.

## Who It's For

- **Researchers & Analysts** who need to rapidly digest articles and build comparable knowledge cards for a research database without losing nuance
- **Product Teams** tracking competitor moves, market trends, and customer insights from industry articles — and need structured output they can act on
- **Marketing Leaders** monitoring thought leadership and industry coverage to inform positioning and content strategy
- **Knowledge Workers** who want to turn external research into actionable, referenceable insights instead of bookmarking articles they'll never re-read
- **Content Curators** building a tagged library of insights from multiple sources without manually rewriting each one

## Use Cases

- Extract a competitor's AI strategy from a TechCrunch article and flag risks/opportunities.
- Read a detailed industry report and pull the top 5 findings with timestamps and sources.
- Share an article's key takeaways with your team via Slack without forcing them to click the link.
- Build a searchable wiki of research articles with consistent structure and tagging.
- Assess a vendor's market positioning based on what they're publishing and what journalists are saying about them.

## Connectors

- **Notion**: Save insight cards directly to a research database with tags, categories, and related links.
- **Slack**: Share summaries in team channels so people stay informed without reading 15-minute articles.
- **Google Drive**: Store cards in shared research folders for easy team discovery and reference.

## Setup

Install and go. Provide any article URL or paste article text directly. Works with public articles, Medium posts, industry publications, newsletters, and blog posts. When Harmony conversation data exists — such as a meeting where someone referenced an article — the skill can connect extracted insights to the discussion context, showing how the article's claims relate to what your team has already talked about. Respects paywalls and notes partial content when access is limited.

## What You Get

A structured knowledge card with every section you need to make the article useful beyond the first read. The card opens with a one-sentence thesis, followed by the article's supporting arguments ranked by strength of evidence. Key quotes are pulled verbatim with paragraph references so you can trace them back. A credibility assessment flags potential bias, conflicts of interest, and source authority. Action items translate the article's insights into concrete next steps for your team. The card ends with relevance tags and suggested connections to other research in your library.

Every card follows the same format, so your research database stays consistent whether you're extracting from a 500-word blog post or a 5,000-word industry report. Cards can be saved directly to Notion, shared in Slack, or stored in Google Drive — making it easy to build a searchable library of insights that your entire team can reference.

## Tips for Best Results

Provide the full URL rather than pasted text when possible — the skill can extract metadata (author, publication date, source credibility) that enriches the knowledge card. For paywalled articles, paste the text directly. When extracting for a specific project, mention the project context so the skill can score relevance and connect findings to your existing work.
