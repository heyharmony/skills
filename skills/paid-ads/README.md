# Plan Paid Ad Campaigns

## Overview

This skill helps marketing teams build, launch, and optimize paid advertising campaigns. It covers strategy (defining goals, audience, budget), platform selection (Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn, Twitter/X), account structure, creative testing, and performance optimization.

## Key Features

- **Campaign Strategy Framework** — Define goals (awareness, consideration, conversion), select platforms based on audience fit, and allocate budget across proven and testing efforts
- **Platform Guidance** — Understand when to use Google Ads (high-intent search), Meta (demand generation), LinkedIn (B2B), and Twitter/X (tech/opinion leaders)
- **Account Structure** — Naming conventions and organizational best practices for faster learning and budget control
- **Budget Allocation** — 70/30 split: 70% to proven winners, 30% to new tests
- **Creative Testing** — Prioritize concept and angle over design details; rotate and iterate based on performance
- **Performance Optimization** — Monitor CPA, ROAS, CTR, and Conversion Rate; move budget toward winning audiences
- **Reporting Integration** — Connects with Google Drive, Slack, Salesforce, HubSpot, and Gmail for insights and team alignment

## When to Use

Use this skill when you're:
- Starting a new paid campaign from scratch
- Planning campaign strategy and budget allocation
- Launching on a new platform (e.g., LinkedIn after Google Ads success)
- Optimizing underperforming campaigns
- Running creative tests and A/B experiments
- Building audience segments and lookalikes
- Reporting campaign performance to leadership

## Example Scenario

Gridline, a workflow automation SaaS, uses this skill to plan a $15K/month campaign across Google Ads (search, $7K) and LinkedIn (awareness, $5K), plus retargeting ($3K). By testing messaging hooks, targeting high-intent keywords, and leveraging lookalike audiences, they achieve a $200 CPA on search and scale winning audiences within 4 weeks.

## Connectors

- **HubSpot** — Lead scoring, CRM data for audience building
- **Slack** — Team discussions and weekly performance updates
- **Gmail** — Competitor ad research and industry newsletters
- **Google Drive** — Campaign briefs, creative assets, performance dashboards
- **Salesforce** — Opportunity data and ad attribution to pipeline
