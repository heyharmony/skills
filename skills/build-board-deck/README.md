# Build Board Deck

**Assemble a board-ready presentation from executive conversations in 48 hours—no scramble, no slides at midnight.**

## What It Does

Board decks shouldn't be a last-minute push. This skill mines your executive team meetings, pipeline reviews, product updates, customer calls, and finance syncs to auto-assemble a 7-slide narrative structure. Instead of cobbling together disconnected status updates, you get a cohesive story with clear ownership, real metrics, customer evidence, and explicit asks. Each slide answers "so what?"—why this matters to the company.

The skill extracts:
- **Financial & operational health** from finance syncs and pipeline reviews
- **Product progress and risks** from engineering standups and customer feedback
- **Sales context** from pipeline reviews, deal reviews, and lost-deal RCAs
- **Customer signals** from sales calls (win/loss reasons), customer success meetings (NPS, expansion, churn)
- **Team capacity and culture** from people-focused meetings

Output is a structured 7-slide outline (not PowerPoint) ready for your design/template layer:

1. Executive Summary (CEO story)
2. Key Metrics (financial + operational health)
3. Product & Engineering (shipped, learned, roadmap)
4. Sales & Revenue (pipeline, expansion, churn)
5. Customer Health (NPS, retention, case studies)
6. Team & People (hiring, retention, culture)
7. Asks & Discussion Topics (decisions needed from the board)

## Who It's For

- **CEOs & Founders** preparing quarterly board meetings, investor updates, or annual strategic reviews
- **CFOs** building financial narratives into board decks with confidence
- **Board-level executives** who need deck support but lack the time for a three-day drafting sprint
- **First-time board presenters** (new CEOs, first board deck) who need a template and narrative framework
- **Crisis communicators** preparing emergency board updates when bad news breaks

## Use Cases

- **Quarterly board meeting**: Synthesize three months of exec conversations into a cohesive narrative within 48 hours
- **Monthly investor update**: Build lightweight decks for angels or board observers between formal meetings
- **Series A/B/C fundraising prep**: Generate deck foundation with investor-facing metrics and narrative arc
- **Annual strategic review**: Create multi-quarter narrative showing company evolution and strategic pivots
- **Emergency board communication**: Rapidly assemble crisis decks with transparent problem-solution-timeline structure
- **First board deck for new CEO**: Establish board communication baseline when new leadership takes over

## Connectors

This skill integrates with:
- **Slack**: Extracts strategic themes and decisions from executive team channels
- **Google Drive**: Reads existing meeting notes and financial dashboards
- **Notion**: Pulls meeting notes, pipeline snapshots, and OKR tracking
- **HubSpot / Salesforce**: Extracts pipeline, customer data, and expansion metrics
- **Your internal data warehouse**: Fetches financial metrics, cohort analysis, and customer health KPIs

## Setup

1. **Grant access to your meeting notes**: Slack channels (exec team, strategy), Google Drive (finance folder, meeting notes), or Notion workspace where you track executive discussions
2. **Identify metric sources**: Point the skill to your finance dashboard, data warehouse, or Salesforce instance for real-time ARR, pipeline, churn, and NPS data
3. **Provide context on board composition**: Tell the skill if your board includes technical members, investors from specific industries, or has specific hot-button areas (e.g., burn rate, hiring)
4. **Set a timeline**: Specify when the deck is due (e.g., "board meeting March 22") so the skill pulls the right time window of conversations

## What You Get

**7-slide outline** with:
- Slide title, owner (role presenting), and narrative (2–3 sentences)
- 3–5 key data points with context
- "So what" statement (business implication: growth driver, risk, decision)
- Supporting evidence quoted from meetings or data sources

**Bonus**:
- Anticipated board questions and talking points
- Ownership roadmap (who presents which slides)
- Notes on bad news delivery (if needed)
- Appendix with detailed financials or technical context

**Time saved**: 16–20 hours of drafting, synthesizing, and wrangling data. Go from scattered notes to board-ready outline in one skill run.
