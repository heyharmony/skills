# Content Idea Generator

> Turn customer conversations, market trends, and team expertise into a prioritized content pipeline your team can execute on.

## What It Does

The Content Idea Generator transforms customer conversations, support questions, sales objections, and market signals into a structured, prioritized pipeline of high-impact content ideas. Instead of brainstorming in a vacuum, you ground content strategy in real customer language and business outcomes.

The skill helps you:

- **Extract themes** from customer conversations: What problems do they mention repeatedly? What language do they use?
- **Generate ideas across formats**: Blog posts, videos, webinars, case studies, interactive tools, social series—tailored to your audience and goals
- **Score and prioritize**: Effort, impact, uniqueness, and alignment metrics help you focus on quick wins and high-leverage content
- **Map to funnel stages**: Place awareness-stage educational content, consideration-stage comparisons, and decision-stage guides where they matter most
- **Build a timeline**: Create a content calendar that clusters complementary pieces and shows what to ship when
- **Handle edge cases**: Know what to do when you lack data, competitors move fast, or customer needs shift mid-quarter

## Who It's For

- **Content Managers & Strategists**: You own the roadmap and need to move from ideas to a clear plan
- **Marketing Leaders**: You want content strategy grounded in customer reality, not assumptions
- **Product Teams**: You're launching a feature or product and need educational content that resonates
- **Sales Enablement Teams**: You want content that addresses real objections and buyer questions
- **Demand Gen Managers**: You're funding content and need ROI clarity (effort vs. impact scoring)
- **Growth Teams**: You're using content to drive awareness, consideration, or conversion at scale

## Use Cases

### Quarterly or Monthly Content Planning
You have a mandate to build a content roadmap for Q2, but you're starting with a blank page. The skill helps you systematize ideation: gather customer conversations, surface themes, generate 8–10 ideas, score them, and build a month-by-month calendar.

### Turning Customer Conversations into Content
Your sales team reported that three recent prospects asked, "How long does implementation take?" and "Will this break our existing system?" Rather than guessing, you extract those questions from Harmony conversation data, validate them across support tickets, and turn them into a "Compliance Implementation Playbook" blog post + checklist + webinar.

### Competitive Response
A competitor publishes a whitepaper on "AI in Compliance." Instead of panicking, you use the skill to reframe your angle: "We tested their framework with 50 customers—here's what works." You prioritize a comparative blog post and co-host a panel with your CTO.

### Filling Content Gaps in Your Funnel
You analyze your pipeline and realize awareness-stage content is sparse—prospects don't know you exist. You use the skill to generate 5–6 educational, trend-spotting pieces that build authority before they even consider you.

### Product Launch Education
Your CTO built a new fraud detection model. It's technically brilliant, but go-to-market requires content that explains it to compliance officers and IT directors—different angles for different buyers. The skill helps you brainstorm launch content: a technical deep dive, a business case comparison, a customer success story, and a 3-post social series.

### Prioritizing Amid Constraints
You have a small content team and quarterly budget to allocate. The effort/impact matrix helps you identify quick wins (low effort, high impact) to ship fast, while sequencing more ambitious pieces (high effort, high impact) over the quarter.

## Setup

**Prerequisites**:
- Customer conversations (optional but powerful): Use Harmony conversation data, support tickets, or recent customer interviews
- Sales and customer feedback: What questions do prospects ask? What objections come up repeatedly?
- Business context: What are your content goals (thought leadership, demand gen, product education)? Who are you trying to reach?
- Industry knowledge: What trends, regulatory changes, or competitive moves matter to your market?

**How to Use**:
1. Gather your inputs: conversations, customer questions, trends, internal expertise
2. Run through the Instructions section of the skill: extract themes, generate ideas, score them, build a calendar
3. Review the output structure (a prioritized table, quick wins, content calendar, and complementary clusters)
4. Socialize with your team, adjust based on capacity, and start shipping

**Optional Enrichment**:
- If you have Harmony conversation data, pull exact quotes and customer language—this makes content more authentic and SEO-friendly
- If you're working on a specific initiative (product launch, new market entry), filter ideas to that context
- If you want an even more granular roadmap, map each idea to specific campaigns, personas, or revenue metrics

## What You Get

### 1. Prioritized Content Ideas Table
A ranked list of 8–12 content ideas with:
- **Title and angle**: The specific content angle and how it differs from competitor content
- **Format**: Blog, video, webinar, case study, social series, interactive tool, podcast, etc.
- **Funnel stage**: Awareness, consideration, or decision—where it lands in the buyer journey
- **Scoring**: Effort (time + resources), impact (reach + influence), uniqueness (how distinctive), and alignment (how well it fits your goals)
- **Notes**: Why it ranks high or why it's a quick win

### 2. Quick Wins Checklist
3–5 ideas that are easy to ship (low effort) but valuable (high impact). Perfect for building momentum or filling gaps while you work on bigger pieces.

### 3. Content Calendar
A month-by-month breakdown of which pieces to publish when across Q1–Q4. Includes dependencies (e.g., "blog first, video follows, then social repurposing") and strategic windows (e.g., "publish regulatory update before the deadline").

### 4. Complementary Content Clusters
Groups of related content that reinforce each other: blog + video + email series + social + webinar all exploring the same theme. These clusters multiply impact and give you multiple touchpoints with prospects.

### 5. Edge Case Playbook
Real-world scenarios and how to handle them:
- No customer conversation data? Use support tickets and sales feedback instead.
- Niche audience with small reach? Broaden distribution and create micro-content variations.
- Competitor published first? Reframe your angle and lean on proprietary data or customer validation.
- New customer objection discovered mid-quarter? Fast-track a response or queue for next quarter.
- Customer wants to co-create? Fast-track, negotiate scope, maximize mutual value.

## Why It Works

Most content strategies start with assumptions: "We think we should write about X." This skill starts with reality: your customers' actual questions, pain points, language, and behavior. When you listen to what customers ask and then create content that answers it, you build authority, earn trust, and drive conversion.

The prioritization framework prevents the common trap of producing beautiful content that doesn't matter. By scoring effort and impact, you identify high-leverage ideas and quick wins—then allocate team resources wisely.

The edge case playbook acknowledges that strategy is not linear. Markets move, competitors act, customer needs shift. The skill gives you a playbook to adapt without starting from scratch.
