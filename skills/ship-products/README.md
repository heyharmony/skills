# Ship Products

> The main thing is to keep the main thing the main thing. Shipping is a muscle—the more you ship, the better you get.

## What It Does

This skill transforms a launch from vague intentions into concrete action. It surfaces the real shipping blockers (usually cross-team misalignment, not technical problems), establishes clear quality gates so you know what "ready" means, and creates rituals that make shipping repeatable. If your team debates whether you're ready two days before launch, or if your post-launch communication is scrambled, this skill builds the discipline and visibility to ship confidently and frequently.

## Who It's For

- **Product Managers**: Own the shipping timeline but can't see why launches slip. This skill identifies cross-team blockers, creates weekly visibility, and gives you a framework to force decisions before they become problems.

- **Engineering Leads**: Shipping feels chaotic because quality gates are undefined. You can't tell if you're 2 weeks away or 2 months away. This skill clarifies what "done" means and detects timeline risk early.

- **Program/Project Managers**: Managing cross-functional shipping. You need visibility into engineering, design, QA, and support—and a way to escalate when one team's slip affects everyone else's timeline.

- **Design Leaders**: You build designs but don't see how they integrate into the launch process. This skill shows where design decisions impact engineering timelines and when design approval becomes a critical path blocker.

- **Customer Success/Support Leaders**: You're always scrambled on launch day because you didn't know a new feature was launching. This skill includes support onboarding and post-launch protocols so you're never blindsided.

- **Operations/Leadership**: Overseeing multiple product initiatives and want to know why some ship fast and others slip. This skill is the operating system for shipping discipline.

## Use Cases

**Case 1: Quarterly Feature Launch**
Your team has 8 weeks to ship 5 features. Run this skill → Define quality gates → Create week-by-week milestones → Identify the critical path → Run weekly shipping syncs → Surface blockers before they derail you → Launch on time with confidence.

**Case 2: Launch Slipped, Second Attempt**
Your last launch was supposed to be March 1; it shipped March 15. Now you have another launch in April. Use this skill to identify what caused the slip (was it scope creep? QA bottleneck? Cross-team misalignment?), fix the process, and build a shipping cadence that sticks.

**Case 3: Cross-Team Misalignment**
Design finished 3 weeks ago, Engineering started late because design spec was unclear, QA doesn't start until code freeze (and it's already late). Use this skill to build a dependency map upfront, so overlaps are planned, not discovered.

**Case 4: Quality Gates Are Vague**
You keep shipping bugs to production. "It's ready when it feels ready" is your current standard. This skill forces you to define what "ready" is (crash rate, security, data integrity, documentation completeness) and agree on it before you start building.

**Case 5: Launch Day Panic**
Everything feels fine until day-of, then 10 people discover something they should have known weeks earlier. Use the pre-launch ritual (checklist, confidence votes, rollback planning) to surface risks 24 hours early when you can actually fix them.

**Case 6: Support Is Always Scrambled**
Support finds out about launches from users, not from your team. This skill includes a support onboarding step so they understand the feature, edge cases, and gotchas before customers call.

## Connectors

Works standalone with a shipping plan and timeline. But richer with:

- **Linear**: Tracks feature completeness, bug status, and deployment readiness. Shows whether engineering is actually on track or overstating readiness.

- **Slack**: Surfaces shipping blockers from real team discussions. Shows communication patterns that predict launch delays (e.g., tons of back-and-forth about design means design is the blocker).

- **GitHub**: Monitors PR merge rate, build health, and deployment frequency. Reveals whether the team's shipping muscle is strong or atrophied.

- **Zoom**: References shipping meetings to understand team confidence and alignment. Meetings where people sound uncertain are early warning signals.

## Setup

Start with a target launch date and a list of what you're shipping. No special tools needed—the skill walks you through defining quality gates, creating milestones, surfacing dependencies, and running a weekly shipping sync. If you connect Linear/Slack/GitHub, you get real-time status signals and can identify blockers faster. The key is committing to weekly syncs and locking scope 2 weeks out.

## What You Get

- **Ship-ready quality gates**: Non-negotiable standards (zero data loss, no security vulns, core workflows stable) that everyone agrees on before you start
- **Week-by-week milestone plan**: What must happen each week; who owns it; status at a glance
- **Critical path identification**: What can't be late without delaying the entire launch; everything else can be parallelized
- **Cross-team dependency map**: Shows where Product depends on Design depends on Engineering; reveals overlaps and conflicts
- **Weekly shipping sync**: 30-minute meeting where the real decisions happen (blockers, risks, tradeoffs) instead of surprise-launches in final days
- **Pre-launch ritual**: Checklist, confidence votes, and rollback planning that catches issues 24 hours early, not in production
- **Post-launch retrospective**: What made this launch smooth? What nearly broke it? How do we improve?

## Key Insight

Shipping isn't a one-time event—it's a capability that builds over time. Teams that ship weekly get better at shipping than teams that ship quarterly, not because they're smarter, but because they practice. This skill removes the chaos by creating a repeatable process: lock scope, identify dependencies, run weekly syncs, define quality gates, surface risks early. The team that can ship on schedule isn't more resourced; they're more disciplined. Your first launch using this skill might feel slow. Your fifth launch will be boring—because you know you're ready, and you just execute.
