# Generate Startup Ideas

> The best ideas come from problems you've lived with, not brainstorming. Find your unfair advantage and commit.

## What It Does

Instead of throwing hundreds of ideas against the wall, this skill helps you rigorously validate startup ideas by grounding them in real problems you've experienced, talking to 15+ potential customers about how they live with the problem today, and honestly assessing your unfair advantages. The output is not a list of ideas; it's a deep understanding of 1-3 problems worth your next 5 years, and evidence that you're uniquely positioned to solve them. Ideas that sound boring but are actually urgent (compliance, regulatory, operational efficiency) often have stronger unit economics than sexy-sounding ideas in crowded markets.

## Who It's For

- **Founders Between Ideas** transitioning from employment or a previous venture, looking for their next focus
- **Startup Founders** who picked an idea hastily and want to validate deeper before scaling
- **Product Leaders** considering a leap into founding; need confidence that the problem is real
- **Angel Investors** evaluating founder conviction and whether ideas are grounded in evidence
- **Researchers & Strategists** studying markets before making a bet; want a validation framework
- **Career Transitioners** leaving corporate roles with domain expertise, looking to apply it as founder

## Use Cases

- **Leaving Fintech to Start Compliance Company** → Experience the pain of regulatory drift → Talk to 20 founders about how they handle it → Realize it's $70M+ market → Build compliance alert platform

- **Domain Expert Pivoting to Founder** → 10-year construction veteran notices contractors use broken workflows → Interview 30 contractors on how they schedule, bid, and manage jobs → Identify gap in field scheduling → Found construction operations platform

- **Career Switch with Unfair Network** → Left agency world with relationships in 50 brands → See brands struggling with freelancer management → Talk to 15 brand managers → Understand freelancer onboarding bottleneck → Build brand-to-freelancer platform

- **AI Timing Opportunity** → Realize AI has unlocked new use case (summarization, categorization) → Research which workflows would benefit most → Interview users of those workflows → Validate stronger with AI than without → Build AI-first product

- **Watching Regulatory Shift** → New regulations passed in your industry → Founders scrambling to comply → Interview 25 founders on compliance burden → Market suddenly real and urgent → Start compliance consulting or tools company

- **Noticing a Workaround Pattern** → Repeatedly see teams building same spreadsheet solution → Interview them on why no tool exists → Learn: existing tools don't fit their workflow → Build workflow-specific tool that does

## Setup

List 10-15 problems you've personally experienced in the past 3 years. Pick the top 3 that felt "surprisingly hard" (simple on surface, painful in reality). For each, interview 15+ people who experience the problem. Use the Mom Test: ask about their life and workflows, not your idea. Listen for workarounds, complaints, and willingness to change. Document quotes. After interviews, evaluate: do others share this problem? Do you have an unfair advantage (domain expertise, network, timing)? Is the market big enough? Most importantly: do you genuinely believe you should spend 5 years on this? Pick one problem and commit.

## What You Get

- **Grounded Ideas** — Problems validated by 15+ customer interviews, not brainstorming sessions
- **Unfair Advantage Clarity** — Honest assessment of why you're positioned to win vs. competitors
- **Market Confidence** — TAM research, competitive landscape, and growth signals so you know what you're entering
- **Customer Understanding** — Quotes and stories from early conversations that inform your first product decisions
- **Reduced Risk** — Problem validation before you resign from your job or raise funding; lower chance of false starts
- **Founder-Problem Fit** — Clarity on whether you genuinely care about the problem for 5+ years

## Validation Rigor

Great startup ideas aren't brainstormed; they're discovered through disciplined customer research. Here's what rigor looks like:

- **15+ Conversations Minimum** — Talk to 15-20 potential customers about the problem. Patterns emerge. If only 1-2 people relate, it's not a business.

- **Ask How, Not Would** — "How do you solve this today?" beats "would you use this?" The first uncovers real workflows; the second triggers social desirability bias.

- **Listen for Pain Language** — "That's annoying" is not the same as "I'm losing revenue because of this." Urgent problems convert faster than inconvenient ones.

- **Watch for Workarounds** — When people build spreadsheets, manual processes, or duct-tape solutions to solve something, that's a signal. They've already validated the problem for you.

- **Document Quotes** — Write down exact quotes from customers. They're more convincing to future co-founders and investors than your paraphrase.

## Key Insight

The biggest risk in startups isn't execution or competition — it's solving a problem nobody has, or solving it badly because you never really understood the customer. Most founders skip the validation step and rush to solutions. Ideas that come from your personal experience and are validated by 15+ customer conversations have 3x higher success rates than ideas born in brainstorming sessions. The second risk is founder-problem fit: you're uniquely positioned to win only if you have unfair advantages (domain expertise, distribution, technical skill) that competitors can't easily replicate. Pick a problem you're legitimately excited about, where customers show real pain (not just "that would be nice"), and where you have edges others don't have. Everything else is execution — which is hard, but solvable.

Your unfair advantage is the reason you'll win. Without it, you're competing on features, pricing, or execution with founders who have the same advantages. With it, you're playing a different game.
