## Purpose

Founders are drowning in decisions spread across dozens of conversations each week. This skill acts as a strategic mirror — synthesizing recurring themes, contradictions, and patterns across your investor calls, team meetings, customer conversations, and board discussions. It surfaces what you keep saying but haven't acted on, where your stated priorities diverge from your time allocation, and which decisions are overdue. Stage-aware coaching (pre-seed through growth) ensures the advice fits where you actually are, not where you think you should be.

## When to Use

- Monthly strategic reflection to connect conversation patterns into actionable insights
- Post-fundraise priority reset to realign team and strategy after capital infusion
- Before or after a potential pivot to validate whether you're solving the right problem
- When feeling overwhelmed by competing priorities and losing sight of core focus
- Quarterly planning to ensure decisions made in previous weeks ladder up to quarterly goals
- Co-founder alignment check when you sense misalignment on strategy or priorities
- After a tough board meeting to process feedback and identify which concerns are recurring

## Instructions

1. **Scan recent conversations for recurring themes** — Review your last 10–15 conversations (investor calls, team syncs, customer discovery, board meetings, 1-on-1s). Identify topics that appear 3+ times across different meeting types. Note the context each time it surfaces.

2. **Map stated priorities vs. time allocation** — What does the founder say matters most? Then measure: across all recent meetings, what percentage of time was actually spent on each priority? Flag large gaps (e.g., "product-market fit is #1 priority" but only 15% of meeting time goes to customer discovery).

3. **Identify unresolved decisions** — Look for topics that were debated, discussed, or brought up across multiple meetings but never reached a clear conclusion or decision. These are often the biggest blind spots.

4. **Assess company stage and calibrate coaching framework** — Determine if the company is pre-seed, seed, Series A, Series B, or growth stage. Adjust coaching emphasis: pre-seed founders need PMF validation urgency; seed-stage founders need repeatable growth systems; Series A+ founders need scaling organizational structures.

5. **Surface contradictions** — Highlight instances where the founder or team says one thing in one meeting and another thing in a different meeting. Example: "We need to focus and say no" in a strategy meeting, then immediately discussing three new product initiatives in the next day's standup.

6. **Evaluate decision velocity** — Are decisions getting made in a timely way, or do conversations loop without resolution? Identify bottlenecks: missing information, stakeholder misalignment, unclear decision rights, or founder indecision.

7. **Build a strategic priorities dashboard** — Synthesize findings into a clear list: top 3 priorities for the next 30 days, evidence that each is being actively pursued, and risk flags for each (e.g., "hiring is top priority but we haven't posted the job").

8. **Generate coaching questions (not answers)** — Prompt the founder to think through their own blind spots rather than prescribing solutions. Questions like: "You've mentioned product-led growth three times, but your calendar shows 60% sales outreach. What would it look like to align those?"

## Output Format

Deliver a **Strategic Mirror Report** containing:

- **Recurring Themes** — List themes that appeared 3+ times, with brief context from each occurrence
- **Priority-Action Gap Analysis** — Stated vs. actual time allocation for each stated priority
- **Unresolved Decisions List** — Decisions discussed but not concluded, plus suggested decision dates
- **Stage Assessment** — Current company stage and recommended coaching framework for this stage
- **Contradiction Flags** — Specific examples where messaging or focus shifted between meetings
- **Decision Velocity Diagnosis** — Are decisions moving at the right pace? Where are the bottlenecks?
- **Coaching Questions** — 5–7 open-ended questions to help the founder reflect on blind spots
- **Suggested 30-Day Focus Areas** — Top 3 areas to focus on, ranked by impact and urgency

## Example: Luma (AI Writing Assistant)

**Company:** Luma, 18 months old, 12 employees, seed stage, $1.5M ARR, recently closed seed round

**Recent Conversations Reviewed:** Investor sync, 2 customer discovery calls, product standup, co-founder sync, board meeting, head of sales interview debrief

**Recurring Themes:**
- "Product-led growth is our strategy" (mentioned in investor call, strategy doc, and board meeting)
- "We need a head of product" (mentioned in product standup, co-founder sync, and board meeting)
- "Enterprise customers are asking about integrations" (2 customer discovery calls, sales debrief)

**Priority-Action Gap:**
- Stated Priority #1: Product-led growth. Actual time allocation: 15% (1 customer discovery call about onboarding UX, rest is sales-focused)
- Stated Priority #2: Product maturity. Actual time allocation: 25% (weekly product standups, but founder rarely attends)
- Stated Priority #3: Fundraising follow-up. Actual time allocation: 45% (investor syncs, reference calls, compliance)

**Unresolved Decisions:**
- Head of product hire: debated in 3 meetings, no job posted, unclear on seniority level or responsibilities
- Self-serve vs. land-and-expand GTM: discussed in investor sync and board meeting, no clear decision made
- Integration roadmap: customers asking, not on product roadmap, no decision on build vs. partner approach

**Contradiction Flags:**
- "We're product-led, not sales-heavy" (strategy meeting) vs. 60% of founder's meeting time in sales contexts (customer calls, partnership exploration with integrations partners)
- "Product is the focus" (board presentation) vs. product standup attendance rate 20% (skipped 4 of 5 weekly standups to investor meetings)

**Decision Velocity Diagnosis:**
- Decision bottleneck: founder is overcommitted post-fundraise. Every strategic decision is waiting on founder input. Co-founder hasn't stepped into strategic role yet.
- Sales momentum is strong (good sign), but it's pulling focus from the product strategy the founder said mattered.

**Coaching Questions:**
1. Your product-led growth strategy assumes customers self-discover Luma. But right now you're spending 45% of your time on sales. Is product-led the right strategy, or should you be building a hybrid GTM that leverages sales momentum?
2. You've mentioned needing a head of product three times in the last month. What's stopping the hire? What would it take to move this decision forward?
3. Your board heard "product is the focus," but you skipped most product standups. What message does that send to your team about your priorities?
4. Enterprise customers are asking for integrations. You have a choice: build integrations in-house, partner with an integration partner, or tell customers "not yet." Which aligns with product-led growth?
5. Post-fundraise, you're stretched thin. What's one thing you should stop doing to make room for the strategic work only you can do?

**Suggested 30-Day Focus Areas:**
1. **Resolve head of product hire** (Impact: high | Urgency: high) — This is blocking product strategy clarity. Decision: hire level, job description, timeline. Suggested decision date: end of week.
2. **Align GTM strategy** (Impact: high | Urgency: high) — Decide: are you product-led, sales-led, or hybrid? This unblocks integration roadmap, sales hiring, and marketing spend. Suggested decision date: before next board meeting.
3. **Build co-founder ops capacity** (Impact: medium | Urgency: high) — Offload non-founder investor relations to co-founder or operations person. Founder should attend product standups again and own GTM strategy clarity.

## Edge Cases

**Solo founder, no team meetings to mine:** Focus on your customer calls and investor conversations. Add a self-reflection exercise: record yourself explaining your strategy in three different contexts (investor pitch, customer call, internal thought) and identify the gaps in your own narrative.

**Co-founder disagreement pattern:** If conversations show recurring tension between co-founders on priorities, surface this explicitly. Coaching question: "I'm noticing your priorities diverge on [X]. What does alignment look like? What's the real disagreement beneath the surface?"

**Founder in denial about a problem:** If the data clearly shows a blind spot (e.g., "we're customer-centric" but zero customer conversation time), frame coaching as curiosity, not critique. Lead with: "I'm noticing something that might be useful to explore together..."

**Pre-revenue company with no customer calls:** For very early stage, pivot the analysis to investor conversations, co-founder syncs, and internal team discussions. Look for: are you debating the problem space? Are you aligned on what success looks like? Are you testing hypotheses systematically?

**Founder who rejects coaching framing:** Some founders don't want "coaching." Reframe as strategic analysis instead: "Here's what I'm seeing in the pattern of your conversations. What do you notice?"
