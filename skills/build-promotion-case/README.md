# Build a Promotion Case

> Promotion is what happens after you've already done the job. Build the evidence.

## What It Does

This skill helps you create a data-driven narrative demonstrating that you're already performing at the next level. Instead of asking for a promotion, you document the work that speaks for itself — bridging the gap between what you've accomplished and how leadership perceives it. You'll extract concrete metrics, map situations where you operated at higher scope, diagnose potential blockers, and prepare talking points for the conversation.

When Harmony conversation data exists — recorded 1:1s, team meetings, skip-levels — the skill surfaces moments where you demonstrated next-level behavior and pulls direct quotes from managers and peers that validate your impact.

## Who It's For

- **Individual Contributors** wanting to move to Senior or Staff roles without waiting indefinitely
- **Managers** seeking promotion to Director; need to show you can handle broader scope and complexity
- **Career changers** moving across functions; need to establish credibility at new level
- **High performers in stalled companies** where politics or slow growth blocks advancement; visibility becomes your leverage
- **People negotiating compensation** who need to back requests with impact data
- **Soon-to-be parents or sabbatical takers** who want promotion clarity before taking time off
- **Technical leaders** becoming architects or principal engineers who need to demonstrate systems-level impact
- **People returning from leave** wanting to establish current value and momentum before discussing next-level moves
- **Underrepresented groups in leadership** building documented cases to counter potential bias in promotion decisions

## Use Cases

- **Before performance review season** → Document your work so your manager has ammunition to fight for you
- **After shipping something major** → Capture momentum immediately; it's evidence that you operate at higher level
- **Competing for an open role** → Show you're already doing that job informally
- **Facing "not yet" feedback** → Understand the actual blocker (advocacy, visibility, competence gap) and build a plan to remove it
- **Preparing for a skip-level conversation** → Give your skip-level something concrete to advocate with
- **Transitioning to a growth-stage company** → Show you can scale alongside rapid change
- **Building a case across tenures** → Compile impact from multiple roles/companies to establish a clear trajectory
- **Promotion timing uncertainty** → Clarify what you actually need to do to get to the next level (not just waiting)

## Connectors

**Works standalone**, but richer with:
- **Slack** — Extract wins, team feedback, and accomplishments from conversation history
- **Google Drive** — Reference impact reports, project documentation, and quarterly goals
- **Google Calendar** — Timeline major projects and create a narrative arc of sustained impact
- **Gmail** — Pull feedback from performance reviews and recognition messages

## Setup

No setup required. You can build your promotion case with just reflection and documentation. If you connect data sources, the skill can auto-extract wins and timeline your accomplishments.

When Harmony conversation data exists — 1:1 recordings, skip-levels, performance discussions — the skill pulls direct quotes where your manager or peers acknowledged your impact. This grounds your case in evidence others already validated, not just your own self-assessment.

## What You Get

- A structured evidence map of your impact across business, team, and technical dimensions (financial, retention, quality, scale)
- A narrative showing you've already operated at the next level (4–6 concrete situations with outcomes, scoped to match next-level expectations)
- Diagnosis of promotion blockers (visibility, role existence, competence gap, advocacy) and removal strategies for each
- Talking points and story templates for promotion conversations (what to say, how to handle pushback)
- A visibility and momentum plan for the next 30–90 days (who needs to know, when to surface wins)
- An optional counteroffer strategy if you encounter internal reluctance or budget constraints
- Timeline for next conversation and escalation path if initial ask is declined

## Key Insight

Most promotion conversations fail because the candidate frames it as "I want this" instead of "here's what changes for the company." This skill flips the frame — you'll walk in with evidence that you're already doing the job, a clear articulation of the business problem your promotion solves, and a plan that makes your manager's advocacy easy. The best promotion cases don't feel like asks — they feel like recognition of what's already true.
