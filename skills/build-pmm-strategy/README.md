# Build PMM Strategy

**Create product marketing strategy grounded in customer voice — positioning, messaging, competitive differentiation, and sales enablement.**

PMMs are the bridge between product, sales, and marketing. The best ones know that strategy should be rooted in what customers and prospects actually say, not what internal teams assume. This skill helps you mine customer conversations (discovery calls, product feedback, win/loss interviews captured in Harmony) and translate them into coherent positioning, messaging hierarchies, competitive narratives, and sales enablement materials.

## What It Does

- **Extracts customer voice** from Harmony transcripts to identify how customers really describe their problems, your product, and alternatives
- **Builds positioning canvas** (target segment, category, differentiator, proof points) grounded in evidence
- **Creates messaging hierarchy** (headline → value pillars → proof points → objection handlers)
- **Maps competitive narrative** for each major competitor with differentiated messaging angles
- **Designs sales enablement assets** (one-pagers, battlecards, email templates, talk tracks)
- **Defines buyer personas** with conversation evidence, not demographic guesses
- **Builds analyst/press narrative** for external stakeholders
- **Sets measurement framework** to track message pull-through and competitive win rate

## Who It's For

- **Product Marketing Managers**: Creating or refreshing core positioning, launching new products, responding to competitive threats
- **Go-to-Market Leaders**: Building GTM strategies grounded in customer feedback, not assumptions
- **Sales Leaders**: Equipping sales teams with messaging, battlecards, and objection handlers backed by customer evidence
- **Product Managers**: Validating product-market fit and positioning before major launches
- **Marketing Teams**: Ensuring messaging consistency across channels and sales

## Use Cases

- **New product launch**: Define positioning and messaging for a product or major feature launch
- **Competitive response**: Articulate differentiation when a new competitor enters your market
- **Sales rep enablement**: Sales struggling with messaging? Build coherent talking points from wins and losses
- **Analyst briefing prep**: Prepare a clear, defensible narrative for analyst and press conversations
- **Quarterly refresh**: Update positioning based on recent customer feedback and competitive moves
- **Product-market fit validation**: Test core value proposition against customer objections and win/loss data

## Connectors

This skill integrates with:
- **Harmony**: Mine transcripts from discovery calls, product feedback sessions, win/loss interviews
- **Gong**: Analyze sales call recordings and transcripts for messaging patterns
- **HubSpot / Salesforce**: Pull CRM data on wins, losses, deal velocity by message
- **Slack**: Share positioning and messaging frameworks with cross-functional teams
- **Notion / Google Drive**: Store and version messaging assets, positioning canvas, competitive matrix
- **Google Docs**: Collaborate on messaging documentation with product, sales, and marketing teams

## Setup

1. **Gather Harmony transcripts** (or Gong recordings) from:
   - Customer discovery calls (new prospects, expansion conversations)
   - Product feedback sessions (customer advisory board, feature feedback)
   - Win/loss interviews (sales wins and competitive losses)

2. **Have customer data ready**:
   - CRM win/loss data by deal or competitor
   - Sales rep feedback on messaging effectiveness
   - Customer objections and questions

3. **Define your scope**:
   - Which segment or product are you building strategy for?
   - Who are your main competitors?
   - What's the time horizon (quarter, year)?

## What You Get

- **Positioning Canvas**: One-page summary of target segment, category, key differentiator, and proof points
- **Messaging House**: Headline, 3–4 value pillars with proof points and objection handlers
- **Competitive Messaging Matrix**: Side-by-side comparison and differentiation angles for each competitor
- **Persona Cards**: 3–5 buyer personas with challenges, success metrics, and key proof points
- **Sales Enablement Brief**: One-pagers, battlecards, email templates, and talk tracks ready for sales team
- **Analyst/Press Narrative**: 500-word positioning brief for external stakeholders
- **Measurement Framework**: KPIs to track message pull-through, win rate by messaging, competitive win/loss ratio

All assets are grounded in customer evidence from your Harmony transcripts — not guesswork.
