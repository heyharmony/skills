## Purpose

Design the first 90 days to establish belonging, build relationships, deliver visible wins, and clarify success criteria. Prevent the isolation and disengagement that comes from leaving a new hire to drown in tasks. Use structured listening, early pairing, and relationship design to help people ramp faster and feel genuinely integrated into the team.

## When to Use

- **Before start date** — send context, philosophy, and prep materials so they ramp faster
- **First day & first week** — focus on belonging and relationships, not drowning in tasks
- **First 30 days** — conduct listening tour, align on 90-day success, create early wins
- **Month 2-3** — increase ownership while maintaining relationship depth
- **90-day review** — assess learning, define long-term trajectory, calibrate role clarity
- **When new hires struggle** — go back to onboarding; often the problem started day one
- **For critical hires or leaders** — invest more time in listening tour and relationship design

## Instructions

1. **Send philosophy and context before start date**
   - Create a "welcome to the company" document explaining your values, decision-making approach, how you communicate
   - Share 3-5 key company documents (strategy, values, team structure, market positioning)
   - Send link to architecture/codebase (technical roles) with "don't memorize, just orient"
   - Include: time zone of team members, Slack norms (response time expectations), calendar setup
   - Signal: "We want you ready on day one; here's what you should know going in"

2. **Design immediate belonging through pairing**
   - Don't let first day be sitting alone reading docs
   - Assign an onboarding buddy (peer level, ideally someone they'll work closely with)
   - First 5 days: 2-4 hours daily pairing where buddy explains system, answers questions, normalizes asking
   - Pairing is not about productivity; it's about belonging and accelerated learning
   - Buddy should say: "Here's how I do this. Here's how others do it. What feels right for you?"

3. **Teach golden rituals by first Friday**
   - Show how you actually work: daily standup format, how you write PRs or share updates
   - Introduce them to your async communication style (Slack threads? Email? Notion docs?)
   - Explain your decision-making process: who talks to whom before deciding?
   - Show the calendar rhythm: all-hands cadence, 1:1 schedule, planning cycles
   - Golden rituals = how we work together; teaching them early prevents later friction

4. **Conduct a listening tour in first 30 days**
   - Schedule 30-minute 1:1s with 8-12 key people across company
   - Frame: "I want to understand the company from your perspective"
   - Ask: What's working? What frustrates you? What do you wish someone would fix?
   - Listen 80% of the time; don't pitch or defend
   - Take notes; synthesize into "state of the union" they present back to leadership
   - Shows: We value your perspective; your voice matters here

5. **Define success at 90 days, 1 year, and 2 years**
   - Week 1: Draft 90-day success vision together (what does "ramped" look like?)
   - Month 1: Define what good looks like in their role after 90 days
   - Be specific: "You've shipped 3 features" not "you're productive"
   - Include relationship metrics: "You've worked with sales team; they know you"
   - 1-year vision: Where do they grow into? What new skills?
   - 2-year vision: Where are they on trajectory? Leadership, depth, scope?

6. **Engineer early wins for visibility**
   - In first 30 days, identify 1-2 projects where they can ship something visible
   - Early wins should be: achievable (don't set them up to fail), visible (team sees them), legitimate (not make-work)
   - Examples: bug fix they understand, small feature, documentation improvement, process tweak
   - After first win, suggest: "You should present this to leadership next week"
   - Visibility + early win = they feel like they're contributing, not learning

7. **Have "relationship design" conversation early**
   - Ask: What kind of communication do you prefer? Slack or calls? Sync or async?
   - Ask: How often do you want 1:1s? What should we talk about?
   - Ask: What do you need from me to feel supported?
   - Ask: What feedback do you want? How do you like to receive it?
   - Document their preferences; revisit monthly
   - Signals: We care about how you work, not just what you produce

8. **Schedule regular 1:1s and hold them sacred**
   - 30 minutes, weekly, first 90 days (no cancellations)
   - Month 1: Listening mode (how's the ramp? any blockers? how's belonging?)
   - Month 2: Early work review (shipping things? what's hard? what skills to build?)
   - Month 3: Ownership conversation (what are you owning now? where do you want to grow?)
   - Beyond 90 days: Shift to career development, not logistics

9. **Create working agreements together**
   - How do we make decisions? (consensus, trust your judgment, ask first?)
   - What does "done" look like? (code review standards, quality bar, testing expectations)
   - Communication norms: response time, escalation path, when to DM vs. public Slack
   - Conflict resolution: how do we disagree? Who do we ask if we're stuck?
   - Document it; refer back when friction appears
   - Collaborative working agreements prevent later resentment

10. **Avoid over-promising during hiring**
    - If you said "you'll lead this initiative," have that conversation month 1
    - If you promised flexibility you can't deliver, acknowledge the gap now
    - If you oversold stability and reality is chaos, reset expectations
    - Broken promises early destroy trust; honesty early builds it
    - Course-correct in 1:1 before they feel misled

```

Role: [Position Title]
Start Date: [Date]

Onboarding Buddy: [Buddy Name]

  - Company values and working philosophy

□ Set up:
  - Laptop, desk setup, access credentials
  - Slack, email, calendar
  - Code/project access (technical roles)
  - GitHub, tools, documentation wiki

□ Send "light reading":
  - Architecture overview (technical roles)

  - Team member bios (so they know who's who)

    • Overview of first week
  - 10am-12pm: Pair with onboarding buddy
    • Tour systems, Slack, communication style
    • Answer questions, normalize asking
    • Show where things live
  - 1-3pm: Lunch with team (informal, not work talk)
  - 3pm: Meet other team leads (quick 15-min intros)

Tuesday - Friday:
  - Daily 9am standup (show how we work together)
  - Daily 2-4 hours pairing with buddy
  - 1:1 with manager (30 min) — check in on belonging, any blockers

  - End-of-week: Manager 1:1 debrief

FIRST 30 DAYS — LISTENING & EARLY WINS
───────────────────────────────────────
Week 2-3: Listening Tour
  - Schedule 8-12 interviews (30 min each) with key people:
    • [Name 1, Role] — company culture/values perspective
    • [Name 2, Role] — cross-functional collaboration
    • [Name 3, Role] — technical/strategic context
    • [And so on...]
  - Questions:
    • What's working well? What would you change?
    • What should I know about our [department]?
    • How can I be most helpful?
  - Take notes; synthesize into "state of the union"

Week 2: First Assignment
  - Assign 1-2 small wins (achievable in 1-2 weeks)
    • Project: [First Win Description]
    • Success criteria: [Specific, measurable outcome]
    • Buddy/manager available for questions
  - Goal: Ship something visible by week 3

Week 3-4: Relationship Design & Success Alignment
  - "Relationship Design" conversation (1:1 with manager, 1 hour)
    • How do you prefer to communicate?
    • How often 1:1s? What should we talk about?
    • How do you like feedback?
    • What support do you need?
  - Align on 90-day success vision
    • What does "ramped" look like in 90 days?
    • What skills should you sharpen?
    • What relationships matter?
    • Document the vision together

  - Present first win to leadership/team
    • Quick show & tell (15 min)
    • Visibility signal: they're contributing

MONTH 2-3 — OWNERSHIP & GROWTH
───────────────────────────────
Week 5-8:
  - Increase ownership: bigger projects, less pairing
  - Continue 1:1s (weekly, focus on: work progress, blockers, relationship health)
  - Introduce to customers (relevant roles)
  - Have "how's it going?" conversations with team members
  - Manager input: Are they ramping as expected? Any gaps emerging?

Week 9-12: 90-Day Preparation
  - Review 90-day success criteria (are they there?)
  - Prepare 90-day formal check-in
  - Gather feedback from: manager, buddy, cross-functional team members
  - New hire self-reflection: How do you feel? What's been good? What's hard?

90-DAY REVIEW — CALIBRATION & NEXT PHASE

Manager 1:1 (60 min):

✓ What's gone well?
  - Specific examples of their contributions
  - Where they've exceeded expectations
  - Skills they've developed

⚠ Where are they still ramping?
  - What's harder than expected?
  - What skills need more development?
  - Blockers or gaps to address?

→ What's next? (1-year vision)
  - Where are they growing into?
  - New projects or responsibilities?
  - Leadership/mentoring opportunities?
  - Timeline for increased autonomy?

  - Performance rating: [On track / Exceeds / Below]
  - Feedback from cross-functional team: [Summary]
  - New hire's self-assessment: [What they're proud of, what they're struggling with]

  - Shift 1:1s to career development focus (less logistics)
  - Assign leadership opportunity: lead small project, mentor junior person
  - Check in on: are they genuinely connected to team? Do they feel they belong?
  - 6-month calibration: are they on track for 1-year vision?

FIRST YEAR — LONG-TERM THRIVING

  - Regular 1:1s on growth, career, development
  - 360 feedback if available

  - 2-year vision conversation: where do they want to go?
```

## Edge Cases

**New hire isolated despite planning** — Check 1:1 frequency. Are they actually talking about belonging, or just work logistics? Isolation often happens when manager skips the relationship design conversation.

**Early wins feel like busywork** — This signals unclear role or over-planning. Align on what actually matters in month 1. Real work, even small, beats make-work every time.

**Listening tour reveals company problems** — That's the point. New hires see patterns old-timers miss. Create a "state of the union" conversation where they share observations without judgment. Use it to surface blind spots.

**Manager over-schedules new hire** — Protect their ramp time. If calendar is full of meetings, they can't do the work to ramp. Push back on non-essential meetings; protect 20 hours/week for actual work.

**New hire thriving but team culture doesn't match** — Onboarding went well but they don't fit. Early conversation: "How are you really feeling about the team?" Sometimes onboarding surfaces misalignment. Better to know in month 1 than month 6.

**New hire delivered early wins but then hits wall in month 2** — This is common. Month 2 is when the "real job" starts. Projects get harder. Increase 1:1 frequency. Reassess support needs. This is where learning kicks in.

**90-day review shows they're below track** — Have honest conversation about: role fit, pace match, support level. Either adjust role/support, or acknowledge it's not working. Waiting to month 6 makes it worse.
