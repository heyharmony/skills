# Sales Question Generator

> Replace generic sales scripts with personalized discovery questions and outreach messaging that show prospects you've done your homework.

## What It Does

Sales Question Generator creates targeted discovery questions, objection-handling responses, and personalized outreach messaging tailored to your specific prospect. Instead of relying on one-size-fits-all sales scripts, it generates questions organized by proven sales methodologies (SPIN, Challenger, MEDDPICC) and customized to:

- **Prospect role & industry**: A CFO gets asked about budget and ROI. A VP Engineering gets asked about technical integration and scalability. A VP Operations in fintech gets asked about compliance and real-time visibility.
- **Deal stage**: Cold outreach needs different messaging than mid-deal advancement. Reactivating a dead opportunity needs a fresh angle.
- **Company context**: Series A companies have different priorities than enterprise accounts. Recent funding, hiring patterns, and funding announcements signal readiness and urgency.
- **Previous interactions**: If you have prior call notes or Harmony conversation data, the skill references specific things the prospect said to build credibility and continuity.

The output includes:

- **Discovery questions** organized by priority and follow-up probes
- **Objection responses** for common "noes" ("we're happy with our current solution," "we don't have budget," "we're not ready yet")
- **Outreach sequences** with email subject lines, opening hooks, value propositions, and multi-touch follow-ups for different scenarios
- **Multi-threaded selling**: Different question and messaging sets for different stakeholders (CFO vs. VP Operations vs. CTO)
- **Edge case guidance**: Cold outreach, deal reactivation, competitor displacement, and more

## Who It's For

- **Sales Development Representatives (SDRs)**: Generate compelling cold outreach and discovery questions that increase meeting acceptance rates
- **Account Executives (AEs)**: Prepare for calls with questions that uncover pain, budget, timeline, and decision-making criteria
- **Sales Leaders & Managers**: Coach teams on methodology-driven selling and provide templates that ensure consistent, high-quality conversations
- **Sales Enablement Teams**: Build sales playbooks with role-specific and industry-specific question frameworks
- **Founders & Early-Stage Sales**: Get professional-quality sales messaging without expensive sales training or consulting

## Use Cases

### 1. Cold Outreach That Gets Response
Generate personalized cold email and LinkedIn messaging that demonstrates specific knowledge of the prospect's company, recent news, and role-based priorities. Stand out from generic outreach with subject lines and opening hooks backed by research.

**Example**: Outreach to a VP Operations at a Series B fintech company mentions their recent hiring, their focus on scalability, and a specific operational challenge relevant to their industry—not a generic "our platform helps companies like yours."

### 2. Pre-Call Preparation for Deal Advancement
Before a call or meeting, generate discovery questions organized by sales methodology that uncover budget, timeline, pain points, and decision-making criteria. Prepare follow-up probes and objection responses so you're ready for any direction the conversation takes.

**Example**: Before a call with a CFO, get questions about budget availability, ROI expectations, and financial impact. Before the same call with a CTO, get questions about technical architecture, integration capabilities, and security requirements.

### 3. Multi-Threaded Selling
When you're building relationships with multiple stakeholders in the same deal, get tailored messaging and questions for each role. Ensure each conversation ladder toward the same outcome but speaks to that stakeholder's specific priorities and KPIs.

**Example**: In a mid-market deal, you might be working with a CFO (budget, ROI), a VP Operations (workflow disruption, team adoption), and a CTO (data security, API integrations). Generate distinct conversation frameworks for each.

### 4. Objection Handling & Deal Rescue
Prepare responses to common objections like "we're happy with our current solution," "we don't have budget," or "we're not ready yet." Each response includes an acknowledgment (so you don't sound defensive), a bridge that validates their concern, and a redirect with proof points or a reframe.

**Example**: Instead of discounting when a prospect says "we don't have budget," reframe the conversation around the cost of inaction or inefficiency in their current process, and ask them to calculate ROI impact.

### 5. Reactivating Dead or Stalled Deals
When you want to re-engage a prospect who said "not now" or went cold, get messaging that acknowledges the time that's passed, references your previous conversation specifically, and presents a fresh angle or new reason to engage.

**Example**: Six months ago, a VP Operations said their timing wasn't right. Now that you know they've scaled their team from 8 to 20 people, reference that specific context and show how your solution now fits their new reality.

### 6. Competitive Displacement
When a prospect is using a competitor, get questions and messaging that highlight gaps in their current solution without directly attacking the tool. Uncover what they wish they could do, and position your solution as a complementary or superior alternative.

**Example**: Instead of "our platform is better than Tableau," ask "what can Tableau not do that you wish it could?" Then position your solution as purpose-built for the use case Tableau can't solve.

## Setup

### What You Need to Provide

1. **Prospect Context**
   - Company name and industry
   - Prospect's title, role, and function
   - Company stage (early stage, mid-market, enterprise)
   - Current solution or tools they use (if applicable)

2. **Deal Context**
   - Where you are in the sales process (cold outreach, early exploration, active evaluation, deal reactivation)
   - Your objective for the conversation (uncover pain, explore budget, find decision-making criteria, handle objection)

3. **Optional: Harmony or Prior Interaction Data**
   - Notes from prior calls with this prospect
   - Email exchanges or LinkedIn messages
   - Relevant context about their company (recent funding, hiring, public announcements)

### How to Use

1. Gather the information above
2. Input prospect and deal context
3. Specify your sales objective and methodology preference (SPIN, Challenger, MEDDPICC, or balanced)
4. Optionally provide prior interaction data or Harmony conversation notes
5. Run the skill and get back discovery questions, objection responses, and complete outreach sequences
6. Customize the output based on your knowledge and tone—these are starting points, not scripts

## What You Get

**Immediate outputs**:
- 4-6 priority discovery questions tailored to their role and company context
- 3-5 follow-up probe questions
- Role-specific or industry-specific question variations
- 2-3 objection response frameworks covering common "noes"
- Cold outreach email subject lines (3 options)
- Personalized opening hooks and value propositions
- Multi-touch follow-up sequences for different response scenarios (no response, initial interest, objection)

**Ongoing benefits**:
- Shorter deal cycles because discovery questions uncover criteria earlier
- Higher objection handling success because you're prepared with credible reframes
- Improved outreach response rates from personalized, research-backed messaging
- Scalable sales playbooks for your team to follow consistent methodology
- Multi-threaded selling that moves deals forward across multiple stakeholders
