# Craft Winning Presentations

> Transform ideas into persuasive narratives that move audiences from skepticism to action. Design and deliver presentations that stick—where people remember the one thing that matters and do something about it.

## What It Does

This skill teaches you to structure presentations around a single insight (your "arrow") and deliver them with the confidence that comes from mastery. It walks you through defining what your audience needs to hear, building narrative tension between current reality and future possibility, sequencing your message for maximum impact, and mastering your material so thoroughly that you can focus entirely on connecting with the room.

Rather than fixating on slide design or speaking anxiety, this skill builds presentations from first principles: clarity about your core message, deep understanding of your audience as the hero, and delivery that sounds like thinking rather than reciting.

## Who It's For

Anyone presenting to decision-makers, stakeholders, or audiences who need to understand something new and act on it:

- Product managers pitching roadmaps or strategy
- Founders and executives pitching to boards or investors
- Leaders communicating organizational change
- Individual contributors presenting solutions or research findings
- Sales and customer success teams presenting customer-focused narratives
- Engineering leaders presenting architectural decisions or technical roadmaps
- Design leaders showcasing design systems or research to non-design audiences
- Anyone building thought leadership or speaking publicly

## Use Cases

- **Board meetings & investor pitches:** Structure your narrative to move capital allocation decisions
- **Product launches:** Sequence launch messaging to drive customer adoption and word-of-mouth
- **Company updates:** Frame organizational changes to build alignment and reduce resistance
- **Customer presentations:** Design demos that speak to customer outcomes, not product features
- **Internal business reviews:** Present quarterly results or performance in a way that drives accountability and momentum
- **Conference talks & webinars:** Build talks that audiences find memorable and actionable
- **Sales & pitch meetings:** Present solutions that position the customer as the hero and your approach as the path to victory
- **Town halls & all-hands meetings:** Communicate company direction in ways that land across diverse roles and levels

## Setup

1. Clarify your core message—the one thing your audience should remember
2. Identify your specific audience and what success looks like for them
3. Outline your narrative structure using the "what is vs. what could be" contrast
4. Draft your presentation following the 10-step framework
5. Practice delivery using the accordion method (expand, then compress to essence)
6. Conduct pre-meetings with key decision-makers to surface concerns early
7. Refine based on feedback and deliver

## What You Get

- A presentation structured for maximum audience comprehension and retention
- Clear narrative flow that builds tension and resolution
- Confidence in delivery because your material is truly mastered
- Decision-maker buy-in because you've surfaced and addressed concerns early
- A reusable template for future presentations to similar audiences
- A single memorable insight that your audience can repeat to others
- The ability to adapt your core message to different audiences without losing coherence
