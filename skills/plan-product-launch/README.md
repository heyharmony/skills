# Plan Product Launch

**Coordinate phased product launches across teams—from pre-launch buzz through 30-day momentum—by mining cross-functional meeting discussions to catch dropped commitments and misaligned timelines.**

## What It Does

Most product launches fail not from bad products, but from coordination gaps. While your product team preps a Tuesday launch, marketing is planning for Wednesday, sales is pitching without messaging guidelines, and timelines are scattered across Slack and email. This skill fixes that.

**Plan Product Launch** mines launch-related discussions from product reviews, marketing syncs, sales enablement calls, engineering standups, and leadership updates to extract commitments, deadlines, and ownership. It then builds a single coordinated plan using the Owned/Rented/Borrowed (ORB) channel framework—mapping every channel where you'll reach customers. The output: a 3-phase timeline (pre-launch, launch week, momentum), hour-by-hour day-of execution checklist, success metrics dashboard, 30-day momentum plan, and risk register flagging dropped commitments or conflicting timelines before they become problems.

## Who It's For

- **Product leaders** coordinating cross-functional launches (marketing, sales, engineering, ops)
- **Marketing teams** running feature releases, pricing changes, or market expansion launches
- **Founders** and startup teams launching with limited resources
- **Growth leaders** orchestrating go-to-market execution
- **Heads of sales** enabling teams with messaging and timing
- **Operations / program managers** tracking cross-team dependencies

## Use Cases

- Rolling out a new product line with full campaign (press, paid ads, email, influencers)
- Launching a major feature that requires sales enablement and customer communication
- Coordinating a Product Hunt launch across multiple functional areas
- Transitioning a beta product to general availability (GA)
- Executing a pricing or positioning change launch
- Expanding into a new market or geography
- Launching a rebrand or visual identity refresh

## Connectors

- **Slack** — Extract meeting notes, launch discussions, and team announcements
- **Notion** — Store and organize launch plan outputs, timelines, and checklists
- **Asana** — Assign tasks, set deadlines, track deliverables across teams
- **Linear** — Log product launch dependencies, feature flags, and deployment tracking
- **Google Calendar** — Sync launch phases and key milestone dates
- **HubSpot** — Track customer communication timing and campaign metrics

## Setup

1. Gather launch context: new product name, target audience size, existing customer base size, planned launch date(s), cross-functional team members
2. Share recent meeting transcripts or notes from product, marketing, sales, and engineering teams (last 2–4 weeks)
3. Confirm launch scope: is this a full-campaign launch (Tier 1), targeted feature push (Tier 2), or changelog + email (Tier 3)?
4. Provide any existing launch materials: blog drafts, press lists, Product Hunt account, ad campaigns, sales battlecards

## What You Get

- **Launch tier classification** — Tier 1 (full campaign), Tier 2 (targeted push), or Tier 3 (changelog + email)
- **ORB channel map** — Owned, Rented, and Borrowed channels with owners, messaging, and activation dates
- **3-phase timeline** — Pre-launch, launch week, and 30-day momentum phases with milestones
- **Deliverables checklist** — Every task, owner, deadline, status, and dependency tracked
- **Day-of execution plan** — Hour-by-hour checklist from 8 AM to 5 PM with actions, owners, and escalation paths
- **Success metrics dashboard** — Phase-specific targets (signups, activation, press, reach) and owners
- **30-day momentum plan** — Weekly content, campaigns, and iteration roadmap
- **Risk register** — Flagged risks, probability, impact, mitigation, and owners
