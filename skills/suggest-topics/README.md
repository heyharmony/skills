# Meeting Topic Suggester

> Never show up unprepared. Never drop a follow-up.

## What It Does

Meeting Topic Suggester analyzes your conversation history to automatically recommend what you should discuss in your next meeting. It scans your previous meetings with specific attendees for unresolved issues, overdue action items, and pending decisions—then serves up a prioritized agenda ranked by what actually matters.

No more walking into a meeting and discovering mid-way through that you forgot about a critical blocker. No more realizing after the fact that you dropped a thread from three meetings ago.

## Who It's For

**Anyone who has recurring meetings.** Product teams, leadership syncs, client check-ins, board meetings, cross-functional standups—if you meet with the same people more than once, this skill is for you. It's especially valuable if you:

- Manage multiple meetings across different teams or stakeholders
- Lead recurring syncs where decisions compound across weeks
- Work in fast-moving environments where priorities shift between meetings
- Want to show up as the most prepared person in the room

## Use Cases

**Weekly Product Sync**: You're running a 60-minute meeting with your PM, engineering lead, and design lead. Three weeks of meetings have piled up: a critical bug, a design decision that's been deferred twice, a pricing call that Sarah was supposed to finalize. Topic Suggester pulls all of this into a prioritized agenda—blockers first, ideas last—so you allocate the 60 minutes wisely.

**Recurring 1-on-1**: You meet with your direct report every two weeks. You want to bring up their Q2 goals, follow up on a project they were struggling with last meeting, and address a recent customer escalation they handled. Instead of relying on memory (or hastily written notes), Topic Suggester surfaces all the threads from your conversation history.

**Client Check-In**: You're meeting with a long-term client monthly. There's a contract renewal coming up (mentioned 4 weeks ago), a technical integration they wanted to explore, and feedback on your service they shared last month. Topic Suggester reminds you of everything and ranks it so you use the meeting time strategically.

**Board Meeting**: You're updating your board on quarterly progress. You have Q1 metrics to review, a personnel decision to flag, a market opportunity to discuss, and an investor follow-up from last quarter. Topic Suggester pulls these together and surfaces which items need the most time and which can be brief updates.

**Standup That's Losing Momentum**: Your team standup has been the same 30 minutes every day for 6 months. Nothing changes; no decisions happen. Topic Suggester flags that the meeting may have gone stale and recommends either refreshing the agenda or adjusting the cadence.

## Setup

Topic Suggester works with your Harmony meeting data. Simply:

1. **Specify the upcoming meeting**: Who's attending, when is it, how long is it?
2. **Let Harmony pull your history**: The skill automatically scans the last 3–5 meetings with those attendees.
3. **Trigger the skill**: Ask "What should we discuss?" or use a pre-meeting reminder 24 hours before.
4. **Review and refine**: The skill surfaces a prioritized agenda. Add your own context if needed, then share with attendees or use it for personal prep.

No complex configuration. It works with whatever meeting history you already have in Harmony.

## What You Get

**A prioritized agenda** organized into three tiers:
- **Must-Discuss**: Blockers, overdue commitments, decisions that unblock the team
- **Should-Discuss**: Key updates, pending decisions, items that came up multiple times
- **Could-Discuss**: Ideas, explorations, nice-to-have discussions (if time allows)

**Context for every topic**: Why it matters, where it came from, what decision or action is needed, and suggested time allocation.

**Smart flags**: The skill alerts you to:
- Items that have been deferred multiple meetings in a row (time to address them)
- Sensitive topics that need careful framing
- Meetings that have gone stale and may need a refresh
- When you're overbooked and need to either extend the meeting or defer lower-priority items

**A prepared team**: Share the agenda with attendees before the meeting so everyone arrives ready, not scrambling to remember what you discussed last time.
