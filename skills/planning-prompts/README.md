# Planning Prompts for AI Models

> Stop getting mediocre AI output and start getting exactly what you need — structure, context, constraints, examples, reasoning.

## What It Does

Teaches you to write prompts that consistently produce high-quality AI output. Good prompts are structured: they provide context (what's this for?), state constraints (in/out of scope), show examples (what does good look like?), and request reasoning (how should you think?). Grounds prompt design in what actually works for your use cases. When you have team discussions about prompt iterations, user feedback on output quality, or past A/B tests, the skill extracts patterns. Works standalone from your task description and quality criteria.

## Who It's For

- **Product teams** using AI to analyze data, draft documents, or power features; need consistent output quality
- **Writers and researchers** getting AI to produce blog posts, analyses, or summaries and frustrated with poor drafts
- **Engineers and data teams** writing prompts to process information at scale (extract fields, classify data, generate code)
- **Managers** coaching teams on prompt engineering and wanting to share best practices
- **Non-technical users** who feel like they're "bad at asking AI" and want to learn the skill

## Use Cases

- Your AI output is mediocre and you don't know why → Learn how to diagnose and fix the prompt
- You want to automate a task but AI keeps producing the wrong thing → Structure your prompt to define exactly what you need
- You're building a product with AI in the loop and need consistent quality → Framework for testing, iterating, and documenting prompts
- Your team writes prompts differently and gets different results → Template and best practices to standardize approach
- You're A/B testing different versions of a prompt → Version, test, and iterate framework

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with these integrations:

- **Slack** — Pull team discussions about what makes AI output good vs. bad, user feedback on recent prompts, patterns of iterations that worked
- **Notion** — Access prompt templates your team has already tested, documented best practices, and examples of good/bad output
- **GitHub** — Reference how your team has embedded AI prompts in code, what worked in PRs, what got flagged for poor quality
- **Linear** — Track issues where users complained about AI output, A/B tests of different prompts, feature requests for AI improvements

## Setup

Install and go. Works best when you have a specific task in mind and know what quality looks like. If you have examples of AI output (good and bad) from past attempts, bring those too.

## What You Get

A complete prompt design template covering: task definition and purpose, role/perspective (who the AI should think like), explicit scope boundaries (in-scope and out-of-scope items), measurable quality criteria (accuracy targets, required sections, format), exact output format specification with examples, three-tier example bank (GOOD, ACCEPTABLE, POOR) that teach the model your boundaries, constraints and trade-offs, and explicit reasoning request so the model shows its work. Testing and iteration framework with 5-sample validation process, root-cause diagnosis if outputs miss the bar, and version tracking to document what changed between iterations and why. Includes real-world example showing v1→v2→v3 prompt iterations toward 90%+ accuracy.
