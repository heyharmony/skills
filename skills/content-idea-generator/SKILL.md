## Purpose

The Content Idea Generator transforms raw inputs—customer pain points, market signals, industry discussions, and internal expertise—into a structured, prioritized pipeline of content ideas. It helps content teams, marketers, and leaders move from "what should we create?" to a clear roadmap of high-impact content that drives awareness, consideration, or decision-stage outcomes.

## When to Use

- **Quarterly or monthly content planning**: You have goals but lack a structured ideation process
- **Enriching customer conversations**: You've captured customer questions or pain points in conversations and want to turn them into content
- **Competitive moments**: A competitor just published on your space, or a trend is heating up
- **Content gaps**: You know your funnel is weak at a particular stage (awareness, consideration, decision)
- **Team brainstorms**: You need to organize disparate ideas and prioritize them systematically
- **Product launches or pivots**: You're introducing new offerings and need educational content

## Instructions

1. **Gather inputs**
   - Identify your content goals (thought leadership, demand gen, SEO, social authority, product education, etc.)
   - Collect source material: customer conversations, support tickets, common questions, sales objections, industry trends, internal expertise
   - Define your target personas and key decision-makers

2. **Extract themes and pain points**
   - Scan customer conversations for recurring questions, frustrations, and aha moments
   - Note language customers use to describe problems (use their words as content angles)
   - Identify industry trends, regulatory changes, or competitive moves that matter
   - Flag internal expertise or unique points of view your team can articulate

3. **Generate ideas across formats**
   - For each theme, brainstorm 2–3 content ideas in different formats: blog post, video/demo, podcast episode, social series, webinar, case study, infographic, interactive tool, email series
   - Vary depth: quick tips, deep dives, frameworks, comparisons, how-to guides

4. **Score each idea**
   - **Effort** (1-5): Time and resources to produce
   - **Impact** (1-5): Expected reach, engagement, or conversion influence
   - **Uniqueness** (1-5): How distinct from competitor content
   - **Alignment** (1-5): How well it maps to your goals and personas
   - Calculate a composite score or prioritize manually based on your strategy

5. **Map to funnel stage**
   - Awareness: Educational, trend-spotting, "how the world is changing" content
   - Consideration: Comparison frameworks, ROI calculators, webinars, case studies
   - Decision: Implementation guides, integration articles, customer stories

6. **Build a timeline**
   - Assign estimated publish dates across Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4
   - Group complementary content (e.g., blog + video + social series that reinforce each other)
   - Identify quick wins (low effort, high impact) to execute first

## Edge Case Mitigation

**If we lack rich customer conversation data**:
- Fallback to support ticket analysis (questions logged + common objections)
- Conduct quick 30-minute interviews with 2–3 recent customers or prospects to surface language and pain points
- Leverage sales team feedback on objections and deal motions

**If our niche audience (mid-market financial services) is smaller than anticipated**:
- Broaden reach with adjacent audiences: RegTech companies, insurance firms, healthcare compliance officers
- Create micro-content: 60-second social clips, email tips, webinar snippets for broader distribution
- Expand distribution: sponsorships, guest posts on industry platforms (e.g., RegTech Today, Compliance Officers Magazine)

**If a competitor publishes on "AI in Compliance" before we do**:
- Publish our piece as a "Building on [Competitor]: Why Their Framework Is Just the Start" comparative angle
- Emphasize our proprietary risk-scoring model and customer-validated approach
- Accelerate webinar or podcast with CTO to claim thought leadership in implementation, not just concepts

**If we discover new customer objection mid-quarter** (e.g., "legacy system replacement is too costly"):
- Quickly produce a "Cost of inaction vs. transition" blog post or ROI case study
- Add it to the calendar in place of a lower-priority item, or queue for Q3 if timing is tight

**If a key customer (Acme Bank, TechFinance) becomes a reference and wants joint marketing**:
- Fast-track their case study and co-host a webinar or lunch-and-learn
- This can bump up effort for that item but increases impact and credibility

## Edge Cases

### "We don't have customer conversation data"
- Use support tickets, sales feedback, and customer research sprints (quick 30-minute interviews) as proxy signals
- Prioritize formats that help you gather insights as you go (webinars, community forums, podcasts with customers)
- Start with internal expertise and industry trends; build in customer voice over time

### "Our audience is niche and reach is hard to predict"
- Create micro-content variations: blog + email series + social clips + video snippet
- Lean into quality over quantity; one deeply researched piece for a niche audience often outperforms generic content
- Expand distribution through industry associations, partner channels, and sponsorships
- Consider interactive tools or frameworks that appeal to adjacent personas (e.g., a compliance calculator appeals to financial services + legal + risk teams)

### "A competitor just published on one of our planned topics"
- Reframe your angle: "Building on [Competitor]'s insights—here's what they missed" or "We tested their approach with 50+ customers; here's what actually works"
- Publish faster and emphasize unique data, proprietary research, or customer validation
- If the competitor hit it well, pivot to a complementary topic that strengthens your ecosystem

### "Halfway through Q2, we discover a major new objection or question from customers"
- Assess effort and impact quickly; prioritize if high-impact
- Either insert into the current quarter (deprioritizing a lower-ranked item) or queue for Q3
- Use it to inform the Q3 plan and adjust your ideation process to catch earlier signals

### "Key customer asks to co-create content or become a case study"
- Fast-track that idea if it's already on the roadmap; invest in the relationship
- If not on the roadmap, consider adding it mid-quarter if impact justifies the effort
- Ensure mutual value: the customer gets visibility; you get authenticity and proof point
