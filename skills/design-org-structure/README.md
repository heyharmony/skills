# Design Org Structure

> **Structure is your operating system for execution.** Get it wrong and you're fighting friction on every strategic move.

## What It Does

This skill analyzes your organizational model and reveals where structure is creating bottlenecks or preventing the outcomes you need. It maps your current structure against your strategy, identifies approval gates and single points of failure, and recommends specific moves to unblock execution—whether you need to flatten layers, merge teams with frequent dependencies, or centralize standards. The fundamental tension is speed vs coherence: structures optimized for parallel execution (decentralized, flat) move fast but risk fragmentation; structures optimized for coherent experience (centralized, coordinated) are slower but more unified. This skill helps you pick the right trade-off for your strategy.

## Who It's For

- **CEOs & Founders** struggling with execution speed or product quality gaps, unsure if the problem is people or structure.
- **VPs of Engineering** managing growing teams and wondering whether to organize by product, platform, or geography.
- **Chief People Officers** preparing for a reorganization and needing a framework to evaluate options.
- **Product Leaders** frustrated by slow feature prioritization, unclear ownership, or dependency chains.
- **Heads of Sales** facing gaps between what Engineering will commit to and what customers expect.
- **Operations & Finance Leaders** reviewing headcount efficiency and questioning whether layers are justified.

## Use Cases

- **You launched a feature and it failed** → Ask "did structure slow us down enough to miss the market?" Diagnose speed vs coherence and fix the bottleneck.
- **Expanding into a new market** → Decide: centralized control for consistency or distributed teams for local responsiveness? Structure the expansion accordingly.
- **Reorganization failed** → You copied Amazon's matrix or Stripe's product pods without understanding your own strategy. Align structure to *your* priorities instead.
- **Approval gates everywhere** → Three levels of sign-off for every decision is a red flag. Flatten and clarify authority.
- **Managers who don't know the work** → Engineering reports to finance, design reports to operations. Misaligned reporting creates invisible drag. Fix ownership.
- **Quality is slipping as you scale** → Decentralized teams moving fast but breaking consistency. Centralize design/architecture standards, keep execution distributed.
- **Your org chart has layers of people who don't contribute directly** → Too many middle managers amplifying decisions instead of enabling them. Each manager should have clear decision authority.

## Connectors

Works standalone—just bring your org chart and strategy priorities.

Richer with:
- **Slack:** Analyzes communication patterns to show where actual work gets done vs formal org chart; reveals shadow hierarchies
- **Linear / Jira:** Maps feature ownership and reveals cross-team dependencies slowing things down; shows work distribution
- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Shows how team structure aligns (or misaligns) with customer segments and revenue opportunities
- **Google Calendar:** Reveals hidden coordination overhead and meeting loads by role; identifies over-coordinated teams
- **Notion / Confluence:** Finds strategy docs to validate that structure matches declared priorities; documents decision authority

## Setup

No special setup needed. Bring:
- Your current org chart (who reports to whom, team sizes, titles)
- Your strategic priorities for the next 12 months (what must succeed?)
- Known pain points (slow decisions, quality gaps, team conflicts, bottleneck people)
- Optional: transcript data from board meetings or all-hands (Harmony will pull themes)
- Optional: communication data from Slack (if available) to validate formal vs actual structure

## What You Get

- **Structural diagnosis** showing where you gain speed vs lose coherence, with concrete evidence (approval gates, decision times, team dependencies)
- **Concrete bottlenecks** identified with impact on your specific strategic goals (which priority does this delay? by how much?)
- **A recommended structure** with clear trade-offs explained (what you gain, what you lose, why you're making that choice)
- **A phased transition plan** (3 phases, 6 months) with specific people changes, role transitions, and communication strategy
- **A decision framework** for when to restructure again (metrics to watch, review cadence, go/no-go criteria)
- **Success metrics** to track if the new structure actually works (deployment frequency, decision cycle time, quality, engagement)
- **Validation**: Your structure now maps directly to strategy, not to ego or precedent
- **Obstacle anticipation**: Identify which current managers will struggle with the new model and what support they need to adapt

## How to Use This Skill

1. **Come with your org chart and your strategy.** You don't need perfect documentation; rough notes work.
2. **Be honest about pain.** Where are decisions getting stuck? Which teams fight? Where's quality slipping?
3. **Expect discomfort.** The skill will surface structural issues that feel political (why does that VP have so much power?). Lean into it.
4. **Plan for resistance.** Some people will benefit from the current structure and will resist change. The skill helps you manage that.
5. **Implement gradually.** The skill gives you a 3-phase plan so you can pause, learn, and adjust without destabilizing.
