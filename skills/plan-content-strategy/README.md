# Plan Content Strategy

**Build a content strategy grounded in real customer conversations.**

## What It Does

This skill transforms Harmony conversation data into a complete content strategy. Mine your sales calls, support tickets, discovery meetings, and CS conversations to extract recurring customer questions, objections, and language. Then systematically organize that voice-of-customer insight into:

- **Content Pillars** (3–5) aligned to your buyer journey
- **Topic Clusters** with 10–15 topics per pillar drawn from actual customer language
- **Scored Topic Backlog** ranking topics by search signal, buyer intent, competitive gap, and internal expertise
- **Editorial Calendar** with weekly/monthly publishing cadence
- **Content Briefs** with customer quotes and actionable angles for your writing team

The unique Harmony advantage: you're not guessing what customers care about—you're mining what they actually say in real conversations.

## Who It's For

- **Content strategists** building or refreshing quarterly content plans
- **Marketing leaders** launching a new blog, content hub, or SEO initiative
- **Product marketers** entering new markets or customer segments
- **Content teams** looking for a structured way to prioritize what to write next
- **Sales enablement** teams wanting to turn customer objections into content assets

## Use Cases

- **Quarterly planning**: Refresh your strategy with fresh customer language each quarter
- **Blog launch**: Start with customer-backed pillars instead of generic categories
- **Content audit**: Identify gaps between what customers ask and what you've published
- **Market expansion**: Understand a new segment's unique pain points via their conversation language
- **SEO strategy**: Find high-intent topics customers mention that competitors rank for—but you don't
- **Competitive differentiation**: Surface angles competitors miss by analyzing customer conversation themes
- **Repurposing calls**: Turn sales objections, success moments, and support questions into a content roadmap

## Connectors

Integrate with:
- **HubSpot**: Pull call transcripts, track content performance against pipeline stage
- **Google Drive**: Export content briefs, calendars, and scorecards for collaborative editing
- **Slack**: Post weekly editorial calendar updates and gap alerts to your content channel
- **Notion**: Build a collaborative database of topics, briefs, and publishing status

## Setup

1. Ensure you have at least 20–30 recent Harmony call transcripts available (sales, support, discovery, or CS calls)
2. Have your sales and marketing team identify current content pillars or buyer journey stages
3. Optional: Pull competitor content to assess gaps; use SEO tools to understand search volume for potential topics

The skill will guide you through mining conversations, building pillars and clusters, scoring topics, and creating an editorial calendar.

## What You Get

After running this skill, you'll have:

- **Content Pillars Table**: 3–5 pillars with stage, theme, anchor customer quote, and topic count
- **Topic Cluster Map**: Hierarchical organization of topics under each pillar
- **Scored Topic Backlog**: Prioritized list of topics ranked by search signal, buyer intent, competitive gap, expertise, and total score
- **Editorial Calendar**: Weekly or monthly publishing plan for the next quarter, mapped to pillars and content types
- **Content Brief Templates**: 2–3 detailed briefs for your highest-priority topics, ready for your writing team
- **Gap Analysis**: Topics customers ask about that you haven't published yet, ranked by opportunity

All outputs are designed to be shareable with your team via Google Drive, Notion, or Slack for ongoing collaboration and tracking.
