# Tear Down Competitor

> Turn scattered competitive mentions from your sales calls into a data-driven positioning strategy.

## What It Does

When your sales team mentions a competitor during a demo, a lost deal, or a feature conversation, Harmony captures that moment and surfaces it. This skill transforms those mentions into a structured 12-dimension competitive teardown that includes a scoring rubric (Features, Pricing, UX, Performance, Docs, Support, Integrations, Security, Scalability, Brand, Community, Innovation), feature-by-feature comparison matrix, SWOT analysis, positioning map, and prioritized action items. The output is a playbook for product, design, sales, and leadership—grounded in what your customers are actually saying.

## Who It's For

- **Product Leaders**: Prioritize roadmap features by identifying gaps your sales team hears most frequently on calls.
- **Sales Enablement**: Create battle cards and positioning frameworks to arm your team with messaging and objection handlers.
- **Design & UX**: Understand competitor positioning on usability, mobile, and experience to inform your own roadmap.
- **Go-to-Market Teams**: Build differentiation vectors and develop messaging campaigns around competitive wins.
- **Executive Leadership**: Present competitive landscape to the board with data-backed insights from real customer conversations, not analyst reports.
- **Win/Loss Analysts**: Aggregate competitor mentions across deal stages to pinpoint where you're losing and why.

## Use Cases

**Scenario 1: Lost Deal Analysis**
*Before*: Sales VP sees "Lost to TalentSync" in CRM, assumes price sensitivity, moves on.
*After*: Harmony pulls all TalentSync mentions from the past 3 months, surfaces that the real objection is "SOC 2 compliance" (mentioned 4 times), "mobile app" (2 times), and "brand trust" (3 times). Product roadmap shifts to prioritize SOC 2 certification. Win rate improves 15% in regulated verticals.

**Scenario 2: Feature Prioritization**
*Before*: Product team debates whether to build Looker integration or AI anomaly detection. No data on customer preference.
*After*: Skill reveals that in the past quarter, customers asked for Looker integration 8 times (competitive comparison, often mentioned by that competitor); AI was mentioned twice. Roadmap decision made in 10 minutes with confidence.

**Scenario 3: Positioning Refresh**
*Before*: Marketing messaging is generic ("enterprise-grade, user-friendly"). Competitors are similar.
*After*: Teardown shows your unique advantage is real-time collaboration (competitor lacks this), superior documentation (3 mentions of their knowledge base gap), and compliance roadmap. New messaging: "Collaborative hiring platform for compliant, modern HR teams."

**Scenario 4: Sales Enablement**
*Before*: BDR team doesn't know how to respond when a prospect says, "Why should I pick you over [Competitor]?"
*After*: Skill generates a 1-page battle card with head-to-head scoring, quick wins to highlight, and proven objection handlers from your own win calls. BDR uses it in next demo.

**Scenario 5: Board Preparation**
*Before*: CEO rebuilds competitive positioning deck from scratch every quarter, pulls analyst reports, makes assumptions.
*After*: Skill delivers a 6-slide market positioning deck (summary, 12-dimension scorecard, feature matrix, SWOT, positioning map, action items) backed by sales call data. CEO has conviction in narrative.

**Scenario 6: Quarterly Planning**
*Before*: OKRs are set in a vacuum; product bets don't align with market reality.
*After*: Teardown reveals that 70% of lost deals cite compliance, 20% cite mobile, 10% cite price. Q2 OKR #1 is "Ship SOC 2 Type II certification"; Q3 is "Launch mobile app beta." Directly tied to competitive wins.

## Connectors

**Works standalone**, but richer with:

- **Harmony transcripts** (your secret weapon): Named competitor mentions, speaker attribution, deal stage context, customer objections, and quotes. This is the foundation of the skill.
- **HubSpot CRM**: Deal stage, close reason, win/loss data, customer account attributes (industry, size, region) to segment competitive wins/losses by segment.
- **Salesforce**: Same as HubSpot; integrates close reasons and opportunity data to validate objection patterns.
- **Slack**: Reach out to sales team in real-time to surface missing data, confirm objections, or validate insights ("Did we hear 'bulk import' again this week?").
- **Notion**: Store the teardown, create a competitive intelligence wiki, version competitor scores over time to track progress.
- **Google Workspace**: Embed the output in Google Docs for real-time collaboration with stakeholders; link to Slides for board presentations.

**Without connectors**: Skill works by asking you to manually paste in competitor names and sales call excerpts; output is a structured teardown you can copy into a doc or share via email.

## Setup

Install the skill and go. No configuration required. If you have Harmony transcripts enabled, competitor mentions will surface automatically. Optionally connect a CRM (HubSpot/Salesforce) to add deal stage and win/loss data to your teardown.

## What You Get

A production-ready competitive teardown deck that includes:
- **Executive summary** anchored in your sales call data and lost deal patterns.
- **12-dimension scorecard** (Features, Pricing, UX, Performance, Docs, Support, Integrations, Security, Scalability, Brand, Community, Innovation) with specific quotes and sources for each score.
- **Feature comparison matrix** (15+ key features) with priority tiers and gaps you own.
- **SWOT analysis** synthesized from app store reviews, job postings, community feedback, and your sales call themes.
- **Positioning map** (2x2) showing where you and 2–3 competitors sit in the market (e.g., "Ease of Use vs. Enterprise Features").
- **Action items** (Quick Wins | Medium-term | Strategic) tied directly to competitive gaps and customer frequency.
- **Sales call insights** section with direct quotes from prospects and customers explaining why they chose the competitor or what they love about you.

Deliver as Markdown, Google Slides, or PDF. Share with product, design, sales, and leadership. Use to inform OKRs, roadmap, and go-to-market strategy.
