## Purpose

Your best growth channel is hiding in your customer calls. When a customer says "I mentioned you to my network" or "do you have something I can share?", that's an organic referral waiting to be captured. This skill mines advocacy signals from Harmony transcripts — spontaneous recommendations, NPS promoters, power users — and designs a referral program with the right incentives, sharing mechanics, and tracking to turn casual advocacy into a predictable growth loop.

## When to Use

- **Post-PMF growth hunting**: You've found product-market fit but need scalable channels beyond paid acquisition.
- **High NPS, no referral channel**: Customers love you (NPS 50+) but you haven't captured their word-of-mouth potential.
- **Customers already referring informally**: You hear "I told my colleague about you" in calls — time to systematize and incentivize.
- **First-time referral program launch**: Building your referral infrastructure from scratch with data-driven design.
- **Optimizing underperforming program**: Current referral program has low engagement or conversion; time to redesign based on customer signals.
- **Seasonal referral push**: Year-end budgets, renewal cycles, or product launches that create referral momentum.

## Instructions

### 1. Mine Transcripts for Advocacy Signals
Review Harmony call transcripts for organic advocacy mentions. Flag instances where customers:
- Spontaneously mention recommending to others ("I told X about this")
- Ask for shareable collateral ("Can you give me something to send to my team?")
- Compare you favorably to competitors in word-of-mouth context
- Describe how they discovered you through a peer referral
- Volunteer to introduce you to specific people/networks

Create a simple tally: count advocacy mentions, categorize by type, note the customer segment and call context.

### 2. Identify Referrer Personas
Build 2–3 referrer personas from call patterns. For each, capture:
- **Who**: Segment (company size, role, industry, tenure), NPS score, engagement level
- **When**: Timing of referrals (post-onboarding? after a win? renewal period?)
- **Why**: Motivation (solving peer's problem, gaining team credibility, professional growth)
- **How often**: Single referral vs. serial advocate (how many peers do they mention?)

Example: "Miranda, Enterprise power user (NPS 9), refers 2–3 peers per quarter because solving her team's workflow problem makes her look good internally."

### 3. Design Incentive Structure
Define a two-sided incentive model:
- **Referrer incentive**: What does the advocate get? (cash credit, subscription discount, feature unlock, exclusive content, recognition)
- **Referee incentive**: What does the new customer get? (first purchase discount, free trial extension, onboarding bonus, feature access)
- **Matching or tiering**: Does referrer incentive scale with referee conversion? (e.g., $25 if referee signs up, $50 if they spend $500)

Consider psychology: non-monetary incentives (status, access, community) often outperform cash for advocates. Test 2–3 combinations.

### 4. Calculate Viral Coefficient and Set Targets
Define your viral loop metrics:
- **K-factor (viral coefficient)**: invites sent × conversion rate × revenue impact
  - Example: Each customer sends 4 invites, 10% convert = K of 0.4
  - K > 1 = exponential growth; K = 0.5 = sustainable word-of-mouth channel
- **Payback period**: How long until referral incentive cost is recovered by customer LTV
- **Target growth rate**: What % of new customers should come from referrals? (SaaS benchmark: 5–20%)

Set 90-day goals (e.g., "Increase K-factor from 0.2 to 0.35; reach 10% of new ARR from referrals").

### 5. Design Sharing Mechanics
Eliminate friction between intent and action. Build mechanics for each channel:
- **Email**: One-click referral email with pre-written subject, personalization tokens, trackable links
- **Unique link/code**: Referrer gets branded referral link or code to share; track clicks and conversions
- **In-app widget**: "Refer a friend" button in dashboard; modal with copy-paste link and social share options
- **Social sharing**: Pre-populated tweets, LinkedIn posts for advocates
- **Direct invitation**: CSV upload or integration (Slack, email lists) for bulk referral campaigns

Principle: No more than 2 clicks to share; no form friction (pre-populate names if possible).

### 6. Build Referral Landing Page Messaging
Use language and proof points from customer calls. The landing page should:
- **Lead with customer voice**: "Miranda recommended this to her team because..." (real or fictionalized)
- **Emphasize the referee incentive**: Free trial, discount, exclusive early access
- **Social proof**: Customer logos, testimonials, referral-source badges
- **Trust signals**: Security, compliance, integration ease
- **Single CTA**: "Claim your bonus" or "Start free" (don't dilute with multiple options)

A/B test messaging from different referrer personas; mirror language they use in calls.

### 7. Set Tracking and Attribution
Define the data layer:
- **Unique referral codes/links**: Each referrer gets a code (REF-[CUSTOMER-ID]) or UTM-tagged URL
- **CRM tagging**: Tag referred customers and referrers in your system; link both contacts
- **Conversion events**: Trigger: email opened → clicked → signed up → paid (attribute each step)
- **ROI dashboard**: Track invite volume, conversion rate, customer LTV, and incentive cost per acquisition

Use UTM parameters or API integrations (Stripe, HubSpot, Intercom) to close the loop.

### 8. Plan Launch: Soft-Launch to Scale
Structure your rollout:
- **Phase 1 (Week 1–2): Soft launch** → Invite top 20–30 advocates (identified in step 2) to test, gather feedback
- **Phase 2 (Week 3–4): Expand** → Open to all existing customers; monitor K-factor and adjust incentives if needed
- **Phase 3 (Month 2–3): Optimize** → Double down on top-performing referrer segments and incentive tiers
- **Phase 4 (Month 4+): Scale** → Integrate with marketing (campaigns, partnerships, affiliate expansion)

Measure: Weekly referral volume, conversion rate, payback period, and NPS impact. Iterate incentives based on data.

## Output Format

Deliver these artifacts:

1. **Advocacy Signal Report**: Tally of organic advocacy mentions from transcripts by customer segment, call context, and frequency
2. **Referrer Persona Cards**: 2–3 one-pagers per persona (who, when, why, frequency, incentive fit)
3. **Incentive Design Matrix**: Comparison of 2–3 incentive models with psychology, cost, and predicted appeal to each persona
4. **Viral Loop Blueprint**: K-factor calculation, payback period, 90-day targets, and scaling assumptions
5. **Sharing Mechanics Spec**: Channels, friction points, and technical requirements (email, link, in-app, social)
6. **Referral Landing Page Outline**: Headline, value prop, customer proof point, referee incentive, social proof, CTA
7. **Launch Plan**: Timeline, phases, success metrics, iteration triggers, and handoff checklist

## Example: BrightPath (Online Tutoring)

**Advocacy Signal Report:**
- Analyzed 40 CS calls over 6 weeks; 18 mentions of organic advocacy ("I told three other moms at school")
- Top advocates: Parents with 2–3 kids (higher stakes, wider networks), tenure > 6 months (trust built), NPS 9–10
- Seasonal spike: Referrals cluster around end of semester and college prep cycles

**Referrer Persona: Sarah (Parent Advocate)**
- Who: Mom of 2, college-prepping teen, uses BrightPath for Math + Science, NPS 10
- When: Month 3 of tenure (after first win) and October–December (college prep season)
- Why: Solves real problem (college readiness), earns peer credibility, helps friends
- Frequency: Refers 2–3 families per year

**Incentive Design:**
- Option A: Referrer gets $25 account credit; referee gets first lesson free (simple, low-cost)
- Option B: Referrer gets 10% off subscription, month-to-month; referee gets first lesson free + tutor match guarantee
- **Chosen**: Option A (aligns with persona psychology: direct value, low friction, social validation)

**Viral Loop Blueprint:**
- Current state: ~5% referral rate, ~2 invites per active user per quarter = K of 0.1 (unsustainable)
- Target: 20 invites/user/quarter × 25% conversion × $100 LTV per referee = K of 0.5
- Payback: $25 referrer credit paid back in first 2 months of referee LTV
- 90-day goal: Increase referral source to 15% of new trial signups

**Sharing Mechanics:**
- In-app "Refer Sarah" widget: One-click email or link copy; pre-fill friend's name
- Email template: "Sarah recommends [Tutor]—your teen gets first lesson free"
- Social: LinkedIn post template for parent communities and education groups
- Tracking: UTM `?ref=sarah_[unique_id]` on landing page; CRM sync via Zapier

**Launch Plan:**
- Week 1: Soft launch to 25 top advocates (NPS 9–10, tenure >6m); gather feedback
- Week 2–3: Expand to all active users; monitor conversion rate and incentive cost
- Week 4+: A/B test messaging variants (peer testimonials vs. product benefits); optimize for seasonal peaks

## Edge Cases and Mitigation

1. **B2B with long sales cycles**: Referral ≠ instant conversion. Attribute referral across multiple touchpoints over months. Offer referrer incentives for pipeline creation or deal closed (staggered rewards).

2. **Fraud prevention**: Self-referrals skew metrics. Mitigate with email domain blocking, IP checks, velocity limits (max referrals/day per user), and manual review of high-volume referrers.

3. **International and compliance**: GDPR, CCPA, and local laws restrict sharing contact lists. Use double opt-in for referred customers; offer non-invasive sharing (link-only, no email blasts). Consult legal before launch.

4. **Low advocacy signal volume**: If transcripts show few referral mentions, your advocacy may be hidden (NPS surveys often capture intent better). Combine with NPS data, feature request overlap, and customer success team interviews. Consider that product-market fit (not program) may be the constraint.

5. **Referral cannibalization**: Discount-focused referral programs convert customers who would have paid full price. Mitigate with tiered incentives (credit > cash), targeting high-equity segments, and A/B testing referral impact on payback period and customer LTV.
