# Craft Internal Narrative

## Overview

Leaders talk about strategy in board meetings, exec syncs, and 1:1s — but those scattered conversations rarely translate into a coherent story for employees. The result: mixed signals, rumor mills, and engagement surveys that say "I don't understand where we're heading."

**Craft Internal Narrative** synthesizes leadership discussions into one consistent internal narrative with talking points, manager FAQ, and a messaging cascade plan. It prevents confusion by turning fragmented strategy discussions into aligned, repeatable communication that reaches every team at every level.

## Use Cases

- **Pre-all-hands or town hall**: Translate leadership alignment into employee-friendly narrative
- **Strategic shift or pivot**: Make the why and what-now clear across the company
- **Announcing org changes**: Reorg, layoffs, or new leadership requires careful, consistent messaging
- **Quarterly company updates**: Board updates need to cascade as coherent strategy, not mixed signals
- **Post-board-meeting communication**: Board decision made; employees need honest context in their own language
- **After M&A or major partnership**: New acquisition or partnership announcement needs unified company voice
- **When employee surveys flag confusion**: Engagement drops because strategy isn't clear — time to clarify and communicate

## What You Get

1. **Core Narrative** (3-5 sentences) — The story of where we are, where we're going, and why it matters
2. **All-Hands Talking Points** — Opening context, 3 key messages with evidence, what's changing, what's not, what's next
3. **Manager FAQ** (10-15 Q&As) — Common questions employees will ask, with honest answers and framing suggestions
4. **Messaging Cascade Timeline** — Who communicates what, to whom, in what order (leadership → managers → teams → feedback loop)
5. **Feedback Mechanism** — How employees ask questions, raise concerns, and close the loop

## How It Works

1. **Mine leadership conversations** — Pull transcripts or summaries from recent board meetings, exec syncs, and strategy discussions
2. **Identify contradictions** — Map where different leaders frame the same topic differently (growth vs. efficiency, speed vs. quality)
3. **Reconcile** — Propose a unified framing that's honest and addresses all perspectives
4. **Draft core narrative** — 3-5 sentences that answer: Where now? What changed? Where next? Why does it matter?
5. **Build talking points** — All-hands outline with evidence, wins, and clear messaging
6. **Write manager FAQ** — Answer the questions employees will actually ask (layoffs? job changes? timeline?)
7. **Design cascade** — Timeline for who communicates when, in what order, with what tools
8. **Add feedback loop** — Slack channel, survey, office hours, or check-in so people can ask questions and you can correct misunderstandings

## Example

A 200-person analytics company decided to shift from growth-at-all-costs to profitable growth. The CEO emphasized efficiency in a board meeting. The CRO talked about "bigger deals" in a sales sync. The VP Engineering was still aggressively hiring. Result: sales thought we were cutting product; engineering thought growth was still priority; everyone was confused.

The skill reconciled these signals into one narrative: "We're shifting to profitable growth by focusing on our best customers and deepest capabilities. We'll make more money per customer, invest in what matters most, and build a company we can sustain long-term."

Then it turned that narrative into all-hands talking points, a manager FAQ addressing "Are there layoffs?" and "Does this stop product development?" and a messaging cascade so every team heard the same story from their leader.

## Key Features

- **Reconciles contradictions** — Finds the coherent story when different leaders are saying different things
- **Honest, not sanitized** — Acknowledges trade-offs and real changes, not empty platitudes
- **Actionable for managers** — FAQ and talking points they can use in their own team meetings
- **Prevents rumor mills** — Clear communication and a feedback loop short-circuit speculation
- **Role-based** — Different talking points for all-hands, board, customers, and teams
- **Cascades consistently** — Timeline ensures leadership → managers → teams → feedback in the right order

## Best Practices

- **Start with leadership alignment** — If board and execs don't agree on narrative, escalate before you draft
- **Be honest about what's changing** — Employees know when you're spin-doctoring. Acknowledge real shifts and trade-offs
- **Include managers in drafting** — They'll spot questions employees will actually ask
- **Communicate early and often** — Don't wait for perfect. Draft, gather feedback, iterate
- **Add a feedback loop immediately** — Survey, Slack channel, or office hours so you can correct misunderstandings and close the loop
- **Update as facts change** — Narrative isn't set-and-forget. Update as new information emerges or decisions shift
