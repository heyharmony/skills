# Design Growth Loops

> Stop paying for every customer. Build loops that bring users to you.

## What It Does

Growth loops are self-reinforcing cycles where user actions attract new users, creating compounding growth without constant ad spend. This skill maps your product's viral mechanics, content engines, paid loop structures, and data-driven feedback cycles. It identifies which user behaviors fuel growth, measures loop efficiency (viral coefficient, cycle time, reinvestment rate), and designs experiments to strengthen the loops that already exist or unlock new ones.

## Who It's For

- **Product Managers**: Need to shift from linear funnel thinking to loop-based growth. Pain point: CAC keeps rising even with more ads.
- **Growth Leaders**: Building sustainable acquisition channels. Pain point: Team is adding loop ideas without measurement or priority.
- **Founders**: Scaling from initial users to sustainable growth. Pain point: Unsure which behaviors to optimize or feature to build next.
- **Marketing Strategists**: Designing campaigns that recruit, not just convert. Pain point: One-time campaigns don't create lasting growth momentum.
- **Data Analysts**: Measuring loop health and velocity. Pain point: Lacking clarity on which metrics actually predict loop compounding.
- **Design/UX Leads**: Creating friction-free moments for viral actions. Pain point: Unsure which user moments are high-leverage for growth.

## Use Cases

- **Your acquisition is slowing** → Diagnose which loops exist and which are leaking users at each stage

- **You're building a referral feature** → Design the viral mechanics: what action invites, what the invite shows, how new users activate

- **A competitor is growing faster** → Reverse-engineer their loop structure, identify which type (viral, content, data, paid) drives them

- **You need to prove product-led growth works** → Show how loop-driven growth rate compares to sales-driven, make case for investment

- **You're about to launch a premium tier** → Design a paid loop: revenue from power users funds acquisition of next segment

- **Your team disagrees on growth priorities** → Use loop mapping to align on which atomic action matters most and deserves focus

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with:

- **Slack**: Pull team conversations about growth strategy, experiments, and loop discussions from meeting transcripts to identify which loops your team is already thinking about

- **Google Analytics / Mixpanel**: Measure loop velocity—conversion rates between each stage, viral coefficient, cycle time, leakage points with precision

- **Salesforce**: Segment users by growth loop participation type to show revenue impact of activated loops on enterprise expansion and segment retention

- **HubSpot**: Track viral loop outcomes—email opens, clicks, signup rates from viral invitations vs. paid campaigns to measure loop efficiency against paid CAC

## Setup

No setup required—this skill works with your product, market, and user behavior data. For richer analysis, connect your analytics platform to measure loop metrics at each stage (input → action → output → new user → repeat). Have growth team meeting notes, user research, or product roadmap docs handy for fastest insights. It helps to have 3-6 months of user behavior data or analytics, but you can design loops from first principles with market understanding and user interviews alone.

## What You Get

- A clear map of existing growth loops (viral, content, paid, data) and which ones are dormant or underdeveloped

- Metrics framework: viral coefficient, cycle time, reinvestment rate, and leakage points for each loop stage

- Specific experiments ranked by priority to strengthen your fastest loops, with hypotheses and success thresholds

- Competitive analysis: reverse-engineered loop structures from 2-3 competitors to understand what's working in your market

- Team alignment: clear loop ownership, targets, and review cadence to keep the organization focused

- Multi-loop strategy: which loops reinforce each other and where to invest next for maximum compounding effect

## Key Insight

The difference between a $1M and $100M company is rarely the product—it's often the loop structure. Linear funnels require constant acquisition fuel. Growth loops compound: each cycle produces more leverage for the next. Companies like Slack, Dropbox, and Pinterest didn't win through advertising—they won by making product use inherently viral.

The magic happens when your core product behavior naturally creates reasons for new users to sign up. Identify your atomic action, measure loop efficiency, and experiment relentlessly. The loop that compounds is the one that scales—focus there, and growth becomes inevitable.
