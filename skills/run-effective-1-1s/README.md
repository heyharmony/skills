# Run Effective 1:1s

> Transform one-on-ones from status updates into meaningful coaching conversations that develop people, build trust, and clarify career direction.

## What It Does

This skill guides you through structured, coaching-focused one-on-one meetings. Instead of management theater where people recite bullet points, it helps you ask powerful questions, understand wellbeing, track career growth, and build real relationships with direct reports.

The skill surfaces relevant context from your calendar and communication tools, prepares you with talking points, and captures key insights after each meeting for follow-up and accountability.

## Who It's For

- Managers conducting 1:1s with direct reports at any level
- Leaders mentoring peers or senior individual contributors (ICs)
- First-time managers who want to establish effective 1:1 practice from the start
- Experienced managers looking to deepen their coaching skills
- Anyone who wants to move from status updates to meaningful development conversations
- Teams struggling with engagement, retention, or trust
- Remote-first or distributed teams building connection at scale

## Use Cases

- Preparing for a regular 1:1 with structured talking points and coaching questions
- Navigating career conversations about growth, transitions, or development areas
- Detecting and addressing stress, disengagement, or burnout signals early
- Building career development plans with clear milestones and concrete actions
- Creating accountability for both manager and direct report through shared notes
- Preparing for difficult conversations (feedback, performance, role changes)
- Supporting a team member through personal crisis or major life change
- Tracking growth and progress over quarters to inform compensation and promotion decisions

## Connectors

- **Google Calendar** – Syncs 1:1 meeting context and attendee data
- **Slack** – Provides recent team messages and interpersonal dynamics
- **Notion** – Stores running 1:1 notes, career plans, and action items

## Setup

1. Connect your calendar, Slack, and Notion (optional but recommended for full context)
2. Add the skill to your workflow before regular 1:1 meetings
3. Customize the pre-meeting structure to match your team's needs
4. After each 1:1, capture notes in Notion for continuity across conversations

## What You Get

- Pre-meeting preparation with talking points and coaching questions tailored to each person's development area
- Suggested time allocation for wellbeing, status, coaching, and career discussion to balance your limited meeting time
- Post-meeting recap template to document decisions, action items, and growth insights with clear ownership
- A running record of career conversations, aspirations, and progress over time to inform development and promotions
- Signals for when deeper conversations are needed (anxiety patterns, burnout, role misalignment, retention risk)
- Coaching frameworks (powerful questions, SBI feedback model) to help people solve problems instead of waiting for answers
- Wellbeing tracking to catch stress early and adjust workload or support before crisis hits
- Documentation that protects both of you in sensitive situations (performance concerns, role transitions)
