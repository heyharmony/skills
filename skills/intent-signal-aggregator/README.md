# Intent Signal Aggregator

> Stop guessing which leads are ready to buy. Listen to their own words — budget, timeline, champion, ROI — and get a real-time intent score that predicts deal momentum.

## What It Does

Scans conversation transcripts to identify buying signals — budget mentions, timeline pressure, champions stepping forward, competitors evaluated, ROI discussions — and synthesizes them into a single 0–100 intent score. The output shows exactly which signals are present, which are missing, what's contradictory, and what your team should do next to strengthen the deal. Works from Harmony call transcripts when available; also works from call notes, emails, and summaries.

## Who It's For

- **Sales reps** who need to know after a call whether this deal is heating up or going cold
- **Sales managers** prioritizing pipeline — which deals should get attention this week
- **SDRs** deciding when to pass a prospect to an AE for deep selling
- **VP Sales** reviewing deals in pipeline reviews and flagging stalled momentum
- **Sales coaches** teaching reps what to listen for and what questions unlock signals

## Use Cases

- After every discovery call, immediately score intent → Know if the prospect is serious before next call
- Weekly pipeline review, scan all active deals → Identify which 3 deals should get pushed hard this week
- Sales rep says "they're interested but not sure" → Analyze the transcript, find what signals are missing (likely: budget or timeline)
- Prospect went quiet for 10 days after a good meeting → Pull transcript, see if signals were weak or external factors changed
- SDR wants to know if they should hand off to AE → Score the conversation. If 70+, hand off. If 40–60, one more qualification call.

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with these integrations:

- **HubSpot / Salesforce** — Automatically syncs intent scores to deal records so sales manager can sort pipeline by real intent, not optimism
- **Gmail** — Surfaces follow-up emails from buyers (budget approval, RFP request, timeline confirmation) as signals that reinforce conversation data
- **Google Calendar** — Maps meeting frequency and attendee escalation to predict timeline acceleration
- **Slack** — Captures team discussions about deals ("Hey, they have budget approved") that confirm or refute conversation signals

## Setup

Install and go. If you're using Harmony to record calls with Zoom or Google Meet, the skill pulls transcripts automatically. If you're working from call notes or email summaries, paste them in and the skill extracts signals from whatever you have. Connect HubSpot or Salesforce to sync scores directly to deal records for full pipeline visibility.

## What You Get

An intent score card breaking down 6 signal categories (budget, timeline, champion emergence, competitive context, ROI language, approval process) with individual strength ratings and evidence. The output shows which signals are strongest, which are missing, deal readiness status, a timeline of how signals shifted across conversations, raw quotes from the buyer, and specific next-action recommendations for your next call. Includes risk flags if momentum is stalling or signals are contradictory.

## Why This Matters

Sales teams spend too much time debating "are they really serious?" This skill removes the guesswork. By grounding deal assessment in actual buyer language, managers get real visibility into which opportunities will close, which need more work, and which are stalling. The intent score becomes the source of truth for pipeline health — no more disagreements about deal stage because the score is objective, based on evidence from the actual conversation.

## Real-World Impact

Instead of "they seem interested" (vague), you get "They have $150K budget available, timeline is Q2 (non-negotiable), VP Ops is the champion, and they're comparing us to 1 competitor. Missing: CFO approval clarity." This turns hope into actionable intelligence. Sales managers can see exactly what to coach reps on for the next call.
