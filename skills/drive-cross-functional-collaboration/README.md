# Drive Cross-Functional Collaboration

> Transform siloed teams into coordinated units by building real relationships, writing clear expectations, and practicing genuine partnership. Collaboration is how good products become great ones.

## What It Does

This skill teaches you to move teams from polite handoffs and unspoken resentments to genuine partnership. You'll learn to build relationships outside of meetings, write working agreements that prevent misalignment, include other functions in discovery so surprises happen early, and practice "yes and" thinking so disagreement becomes creative rather than defensive.

It covers everything from building the product trio (product, design, engineering as peers) to handling the specific tensions that arise between functions: product's perceived scope creep, engineering's missed timelines, design feeling rushed. The focus is on building the systems and behaviors that make collaboration feel natural rather than forced.

## Who It's For

Anyone working across functional boundaries to ship something that matters:

- Product managers collaborating with engineering and design
- Engineering leads partnering with product on roadmaps
- Design leaders ensuring design is part of decisions, not an afterthought
- Managers building cross-functional teams or projects
- Individual contributors on cross-functional initiatives
- Leaders scaling teams and needing clear collaboration models

## Use Cases

- **Launching major features:** Align product, design, and engineering early so scope is clear and surprises don't happen at 80% done
- **Improving delivery velocity:** Fix the communication breakdown that makes handoffs slow and rework-heavy
- **Building product trios:** Establish peer decision-making between product, design, and engineering
- **Onboarding new team members:** Clarify collaboration expectations so newcomers know how their function participates
- **Resolving team friction:** Address the function-specific tensions that create misalignment
- **Cross-functional retrospectives:** Learn from shipped work together and improve collaboration patterns
- **Scaling product teams:** Document collaboration patterns so they work even as you grow

## Connectors

- **Slack:** Monitor team communication and identify collaboration friction
- **Linear:** Track cross-team dependencies and work visibility
- **Jira:** Map collaboration workflows and dependencies
- **Google Calendar:** Coordinate synchronous collaboration
- **GitHub:** Review code review patterns and engineer engagement
- **Notion:** Share documentation and working agreements

## Setup

1. Identify your cross-functional partners and schedule 1:1 conversations to build real relationships
2. Facilitate a working agreement session covering communication, decisions, and how you'll handle disagreement
3. Include other functions in discovery conversations, not just delivery handoffs
4. Create shared artifacts (prototypes, documentation) that bridge functional languages
5. Establish weekly collaboration rhythms and retros
6. Practice "yes and" thinking when disagreements arise
7. Celebrate wins in ways that highlight cross-functional coordination

## What You Get

- A documented working agreement that prevents invisible expectations
- Reduced surprises and rework because misalignment is caught early
- Teams that move faster because collaboration feels natural rather than forced
- Genuine buy-in on shared decisions because people actually participated
- Better products because diverse perspectives are genuinely integrated early
- Happier team members who feel heard and respected across functions
- Clear ownership models so decisions don't get blocked by unclear authority
- A shared vocabulary for how your teams talk about problems and solutions
