# Analyze Financial Data

Turn scattered financial discussions from leadership meetings into structured analysis.

## Overview

Financial conversations happen everywhere—board meetings, investor calls, quarterly reviews, 1:1s with founders. CFOs explain variance, sales leaders discuss deal economics, and finance teams project runway. But the numbers stay buried in meeting transcripts, spreadsheets, and Slack threads.

This skill synthesizes financial context across conversations into a coherent narrative: P&L reviews, cash flow analysis, unit economics, and budget variance—all grounded in what leadership actually said about why results happened and what they're planning.

## What It Does

- **Mines meeting transcripts** for financial commentary: revenue drivers, cost explanations, variance context, forward guidance
- **Structures financial analysis** into P&L, cash flow, unit economics, and budget variance with narrative explanations
- **Connects numbers to narrative** so a 15% revenue miss becomes "two enterprise deals slipped with signed LOIs" instead of a mystery
- **Packages for audience** — board summaries (1 page), management deep dives (3–5 pages), investor narratives (2–3 pages)

## When to Use

- Monthly or quarterly financial reviews
- Board meeting prep (financial section)
- Investor update drafting
- Budget variance investigation
- Cash runway planning
- Unit economics analysis for pricing or growth decisions

## How It Works

The skill guides you through eight steps:

1. Gather financial context from recent meetings
2. Structure a P&L review by revenue segment, COGS, gross margin, and OpEx
3. Analyze cash flow and runway
4. Calculate unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback period)
5. Run budget variance analysis with narrative context
6. Identify risks and opportunities from leadership discussions
7. Build 3-month and 12-month forward projections
8. Package results for board, management, and investor audiences

Output includes a financial summary dashboard, variance analysis with narrative, cash flow statement, unit economics table, risk register, and forward projections.

## Example Use Case

**Trellis (Series A SaaS, $4.2M ARR)**

Q4 revenue hit 92% of plan—but not a miss. Two enterprise deals ($380K ARR) slipped to Q1 with signed LOIs. Expansion revenue is up 40% YoY. New SDR investment raised OpEx, but CAC is 6.3:1 LTV:CAC and trending healthy. Burn is $280K/month; runway is 14 months with a path to cash flow breakeven by year-end.

Result: Board sees the story, not just the numbers.

## Connectors

Works with:
- **Stripe** — Revenue, payment processing, customer data
- **HubSpot** — Pipeline, deal stage, customer lifetime value
- **Salesforce** — Enterprise deals, forecasts, account health
- **Google Drive** — P&L spreadsheets, financial models, board decks
- **Notion** — Meeting notes, financial tracking, strategic initiatives
- **Slack** — Casual financial commentary, quick context from leadership

## Edge Cases

- **Pre-revenue startups** — Focus on burn, runway, and milestones
- **International operations** — Consolidate multi-currency data
- **Missing financial data** — Supplement transcripts with P&L spreadsheets
- **Sensitive discussions** — Flag compensation and equity for restricted access
- **Rapid growth** — Use cohort analysis to identify unit economics trends

## Output Examples

- **Financial Summary Dashboard** — Key metrics (ARR, burn, runway, CAC, LTV:CAC) with trends
- **P&L with Variance** — Actual vs. plan with leadership commentary on major deltas
- **Cash Flow Statement** — Operating, investing, financing activities and runway projection
- **Unit Economics** — CAC, LTV, payback period, segmented by channel or cohort
- **Risk/Opportunity Register** — Headwinds and tailwinds with financial impact
- **Forward Projections** — 3-month and 12-month scenarios with key assumptions

## Questions?
