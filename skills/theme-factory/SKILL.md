## Purpose
Starting with a blank canvas can be paralyzing. This skill solves that by offering 10 professionally designed themes—each with a carefully chosen color palette, font pairing, and visual identity. Pick a theme that matches your industry and tone, apply it to your artifact (presentation, document, report, dashboard), and you instantly have cohesive, polished styling. For teams without a formal brand guide, themes are an immediate way to elevate visual quality. For established brands, themes offer inspiration or quick options for one-off projects.

## When to Use
- Creating a presentation or deck when you don't have brand guidelines
- Styling a one-off report, proposal, or document that needs to look professional
- Building a template or recurring artifact and needing a consistent visual foundation
- Pitching to investors or new customers and wanting a polished, trustworthy look
- Designing a dashboard or data visualization with color-coded status/categories
- Creating an internal communication that stands out and feels intentional
- Choosing a theme for a team or department that operates semi-independently
- Exploring design inspiration if you're defining brand guidelines from scratch

## Instructions

### 1. Browse the 10 Themes and Choose One
Review the themes below. Consider your industry, audience, and the message you want to convey:
- **Formal/Trustworthy:** Ocean Depths, Arctic Frost, Modern Minimalist
- **Warm/Creative:** Sunset Boulevard, Golden Hour, Desert Rose, Botanical Garden
- **Technical/Innovative:** Tech Innovation, Midnight Galaxy
- **Natural/Balanced:** Forest Canopy, Botanical Garden

Which theme resonates? Read the description and visual identity to pick.

### 2. Understand Your Theme's DNA
Each theme includes:
- **Primary Color (hex code):** Use for main CTAs, headers, brand emphasis
- **Secondary Color (hex code):** Use for supporting elements, section dividers
- **Accent Color (hex code):** Use sparingly for alerts, highlights, data emphasis
- **Neutral Light (hex code):** Background, light text areas
- **Neutral Dark (hex code):** Body text, strong contrast
- **Supporting Palette (2-3 hex codes):** Additional colors for data, charts, variety
- **Heading Font:** Font name, family (serif/sans), best weights
- **Body Font:** Font name, family, standard weights
- **Visual Identity:** Design style, imagery approach, overall vibe

### 3. Prep Your Artifact
Choose your format:
- **Presentation/Deck:** Slides, title slide layout, section breaks
- **Document/Report:** Multi-page layout, headers, footers, sections
- **Dashboard:** Cards, data visualizations, metric displays
- **Single-Page:** Proposal, flyer, or one-pager

Gather your content (text, data, images) before styling. You'll apply the theme as you go.

### 4. Apply Colors Consistently
Start with structure, then layer in colors:
- **Background:** Use Neutral Light as your base
- **Headers (H1, H2, H3):** Primary Color for H1/H2, Secondary Color for H3
- **Body Text:** Neutral Dark
- **CTAs/Buttons:** Primary Color background with Neutral Light text
- **Dividers/Accents:** Secondary Color (0.5-1px rules between sections)
- **Data/Charts:** Use Supporting Palette colors; assign consistent colors to categories
- **Callout Boxes:** Accent Color border or light Accent Color background with dark text

Avoid color chaos: stick to the 5-7 colors in your theme. Don't introduce new colors.

### 5. Apply Typography
Create clear hierarchy:
- **H1 (Title/Main headline):** Heading Font, Bold weight, Primary Color, [Size: 36-48pt for decks, 28-36pt for docs]
- **H2 (Section header):** Heading Font, SemiBold, Primary Color, [Size: 24-32pt for decks, 18-24pt for docs]
- **H3 (Sub-header):** Heading Font, Medium, Secondary Color or Neutral Dark, [Size: 18-24pt for decks, 14-18pt for docs]
- **Body Text:** Body Font, Regular, Neutral Dark, [Size: 12-14pt for docs, 18-20pt for slides, readable at distance]
- **Labels/Captions:** Body Font, Regular, Secondary Color or light Neutral, [Size: 10-12pt]

Test readability: print or view at actual size. Can you read the text comfortably?

### 6. Use Theme Imagery and Style
Apply the theme's visual language:
- **Photography:** Choose images that match the theme's style (e.g., Ocean Depths = seascapes, water imagery; Tech Innovation = modern, minimalist tech imagery)
- **Illustrations:** If using icons or illustrations, keep style consistent (e.g., line art, filled shapes, minimalist)
- **Filters/Tone:** If theme specifies a mood (e.g., Golden Hour = warm, sunset tones), apply consistent filters or adjust image saturation/temperature
- **Patterns:** Some themes use subtle background patterns or textures; apply sparingly to avoid clutter

### 7. Lock Down Spacing and Layout
Apply consistent spacing:
- **Margins:** 1 inch (docs/reports), 0.5 inch (slides), or [theme-specific]
- **Section spacing:** 32-48px (slides), 24-36px (docs)
- **Element spacing:** 12-16px between bullets, list items, cards
- **Card/box padding:** 16-20px inside boxes or callouts
- **Grid:** Use an 8px or 12px base grid so all spacing is a multiple

Aim for plenty of white space; don't cram content.

### 8. Review and Iterate
Before finalizing:
- [ ] All colors are from the theme palette (no new colors introduced)
- [ ] Typography hierarchy is clear (H1 > H2 > H3 > Body)
- [ ] Spacing is consistent throughout
- [ ] Text contrast meets WCAG AA standards (test with contrast checker)
- [ ] Artifact matches the theme's visual identity and tone
- [ ] Imagery and style align with theme description
- [ ] No design elements feel out of place or inconsistent

Make adjustments. If something feels off, it likely is.

### 9. Export and Share
- Save in your final format (PDF, .pptx, Google Slides, .docx, etc.)
- Share with stakeholders
- If this becomes a recurring template, save a blank version as a template for future use
- Tag it with the theme name (e.g., "Ocean Depths Presentation Template")

### 10. Now What?
After applying a theme to your artifact:
- **For presentations:** Share the file, use in a pitch or all-hands, or save as a reusable template
- **For documents/reports:** Publish to your wiki, email to stakeholders, or add to your document library
- **For dashboards:** Deploy to your monitoring/analytics tool, share the Figma or design file, or embed in reports
- **For templates:** Share with your team so others can create consistent work using the same theme

## Edge Cases

- **Theme colors don't work for your data/charts:** You can substitute one color while keeping the others. For example, if Arctic Frost's cyan doesn't contrast enough with your background, swap it for another Supporting color from the palette. Document the change.

- **You want to mix two themes:** Choose a primary theme and one supporting color from a secondary theme. For example, use Sunset Boulevard as primary with one accent from Desert Rose. Keep it to 2 themes max; more creates chaos.

- **Printed vs. digital:** Themes are designed for digital. If printing, verify colors print accurately (some bright digital colors print dull). Test on your printer before finalizing.

- **Accessibility/contrast concerns:** Some themes (e.g., Sunset Boulevard with orange on warm white) may have contrast issues. Use a contrast checker tool (e.g., WCAG checker) and adjust text color or background as needed.

- **Your artifact has very specific color needs (e.g., status indicators, data visualization):** Keep the theme's primary 5 colors and add 2-3 technical colors for your needs (e.g., green for success, red for failure). Document these as "extended palette."

- **Theme feels too bold or too muted:** Adjust saturation or brightness of colors slightly. Most design tools let you lighten/darken hex codes. A darker or lighter variant of the theme is fine; keep the overall palette intact.
