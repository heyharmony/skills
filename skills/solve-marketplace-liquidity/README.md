# Solve Marketplace Liquidity

> The chicken-and-egg problem is only a problem until you decide which egg to hatch first.

## What It Does

This skill diagnoses which side of your two-sided marketplace is actually constraining growth—supply or demand—and executes a targeted sprint to hit critical liquidity mass. It walks you through measuring your liquidity ratio (successful matches ÷ total requests), identifying quality bottlenecks, testing single-player use cases, and building a 90-day sprint playbook. Includes templates for mapping supplier and demander churn, calculating your liquidity threshold, and deciding whether to subsidize or pivot based on real economics.

## Who It's For

- **Product leaders** at early-stage marketplaces drowning in fundraising pressure to show growth without clarity on whether supply or demand is the real bottleneck

- **Founders** with flatlined marketplaces who've been subsidizing both sides unprofitably and need permission to focus on one side first

- **Growth leads** trying to communicate to the board why they should constrain geography and category before attempting national scaling

- **Operations and marketplace managers** who need to monitor liquidity metrics weekly and catch supply/demand imbalances before they become churn spirals

- **CFOs and finance leads** evaluating whether unit economics make sense at actual pricing (not subsidized rates) and how much burn they can sustain

## Use Cases

- **"Our marketplace is growing but matches are taking 3+ days."** → Diagnose whether supply is too thin or matching algorithm is broken, then execute supply recruitment or algorithm improvement sprint.

- **"Suppliers signed up but 80% never list a service."** → Identify onboarding friction vs. market fit, build single-player features to unlock supplier engagement independent of demand.

- **"We're burning $10K/month subsidizing both sides."** → Map unit economics at actual pricing, decide which subsidy to cut first, and measure churn to confirm product/market fit exists.

- **"We launched in 12 cities simultaneously and we're mediocre everywhere."** → Refocus on one city, hit 70%+ match rate and word-of-mouth density, then expand methodically to adjacent cities.

- **"Supplier churn spiked after we increased take rates."** → Analyze whether suppliers actually have margin at new rates, and whether your liquidity is strong enough to support higher pricing without losing key partners.

- **"One supplier is doing 40% of volume and they're threatening to leave."** → Recognize you have a quality or recruitment problem, not just a liquidity problem; understand why that supplier wins, then clone their success patterns.

## Connectors

Works standalone with direct user research and basic metrics, but richer with:

- **HubSpot** (track supplier funnel, conversion, and churn stages)
- **Salesforce** (monitor performance by supplier segment and predict churn)
- **Google Sheets** (real-time liquidity ratio dashboard updated weekly)
- **Slack** (automated alerts when match rate drops below threshold or top supplier churn risk)

## Setup

Review your last 30 days of marketplace activity: count active suppliers, active demanders, total requests, and successful matches. Interview 5-10 active users and 5-10 churned users separately—their pain points will reveal which side is truly constrained. You'll have your diagnosis in 2-3 hours of focused work.

## What You Get

- **Liquidity ratio calculation** (successful matches ÷ total requests) and benchmark against your industry standard rates

- **Constraint diagnosis** clarifying whether supply, demand, or both need to move toward critical mass

- **Targeted 90-day sprint playbook** focusing recruitment and retention efforts on the easiest-to-move side

- **Supply and demand quality audit** identifying which segments are churning fastest and why they leave

- **Single-player mode prototype recommendation** to validate product value independent of marketplace network effects

- **Unit economics review** at actual pricing, showing whether subsidies are masking real product/market fit problems

## Key Insight

Most founders delay the supply vs. demand decision, hoping both will grow together organically. They won't. Pick the side that's easier to recruit (almost always supply), constrain geography ruthlessly (be the best in one neighborhood before expanding), and solve locally before scaling nationally. Once you hit 70%+ match rates and word-of-mouth momentum in one area, expansion becomes predictable and capital-efficient. Before that, it's expensive brute force and luck. Marketplace liquidity is not a growth problem—it's a product problem.
