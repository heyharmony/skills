# LinkedIn Sales Navigator Alt

> Research any account in 10 minutes — map the decision-makers, find the warm intro path through your network, spot the recent signals that make them ready to talk.

## What It Does

Builds a structured account research brief from public data and your Harmony network. You get: company overview with recent funding and hiring signals, the decision-makers who actually control budget in your target department, warm introduction paths through your existing contacts, specific credibility hooks you can use in outreach, and a pre-call research checklist. No manual LinkedIn scraping needed — the skill works from public information and your existing relationship data in Harmony to find doors you didn't know you had.

## Who It's For

- **AEs** who need to quickly understand a target account before cold outreach or customer meeting
- **SDRs** building outbound lists and qualifying whether accounts are worth researching
- **Sales leaders** planning ABM accounts — need full organizational mapping before campaign launch
- **Sales development reps** creating prospecting lists and wanting to find warm intro paths
- **New team members** ramping on key accounts and needing structure before first call

## Use Cases

- "I want to target Velocity Logistics. Who's the VP Ops? Do we know anyone there?" → Full account brief with decision-makers, warm intro through your colleague, and credibility hooks for the email
- "This prospect just got hired to a new role at their company. Who else should I know about there?" → Full account structure with reporting lines and other stakeholders
- "We're planning an ABM campaign on 10 accounts. Research them all." → Get standardized briefs on each with decision-makers and signals
- "Prospect mentioned they just got funding / hired a new CEO / expanding to Europe" → Research reveals all the implications and which departments have new budget/pain

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with these integrations:

- **HubSpot / Salesforce** — De-dupes your prospect research against existing CRM records; flag existing relationships you might have forgotten
- **Gmail** — Finds email addresses of prospects; shows past email history with their company
- **Slack** — Searches team conversations to see if anyone has mentioned the company or knows someone there
- **Google Calendar** — Identifies people on your team who've already met with the target company through meeting attendees
- **LinkedIn** — If connected, provides real-time hiring, funding, and profile data to enrich research

## Setup

Install and go. Works best when you have Harmony data (past contacts, meetings, emails) so the skill can find warm intro paths through your network. Pure public research still works but lacks the "door opener" dimension. Connect HubSpot or Gmail to auto-detect existing relationships and avoid duplicate outreach.

## What You Get

A structured brief with company snapshot (size, funding, recent news), growth signals that indicate current pain or budget, a map of target decision-makers by department with their pain points, all possible warm introduction paths through your network with relationship strength and intro talking points, a recommended first call target with timing rationale, a pre-call research checklist, and 2–3 credibility hooks you can reference in your outreach to show you've done homework.

## Why This Matters

Cold outreach fails when you don't know who to call or why they should care. This skill solves both problems. It maps the full decision-making structure (not just finding a name in LinkedIn) and surfaces warm paths through your own network that most teams didn't know existed. Instead of a generic cold email, you have credibility hooks and a warm introduction path, which dramatically improves response rates.

## Real-World Impact

Your SDR spends 2 hours researching a company manually on LinkedIn, finding one name, and guessing if they're the right person. With this skill: 10 minutes, you have 3 decision-makers mapped, 2 warm intro paths identified, company growth signals documented, and a pre-call research checklist. Response rate increases 3–5x because the cold email isn't cold anymore — it has context and a warm path.
