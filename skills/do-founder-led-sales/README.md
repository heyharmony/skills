# Do Founder-Led Sales

> Close your first 10-20 customers through warm networks and discovery-based selling — turn objections into a playbook before you hire.

This skill guides founders through closing initial customers using warm introductions and iterative discovery conversations, building a repeatable motion from day one. Rather than pitching features, you listen to understand their pain, quantify the problem, and position your product as a solution to a specific, measurable problem that matters to their P&L. You document every conversation—objections, pricing reactions, questions asked—so patterns emerge that become your sales playbook for future hires. This approach also creates the defensibility you'll need: a playbook that actually works, not a guess about what the market wants. When you can show investors (or customers, or employees) a documented path to revenue based on real conversations, not assumptions, everything changes.

## Who It's For

- **Founders (all roles)** — Need to close first customers yourself; avoid hiring a sales person before you understand your own sales motion
- **Early-stage CEOs** — Bootstrapped or seed stage; revenue validates the business better than any investor pitch
- **Product Founders** — Want to learn from early customers instead of optimizing prematurely; need to stay close to market feedback
- **Technical Cofounders** — Uncomfortable with sales but willing to do customer discovery calls; leverage founder advantage (can commit to roadmap items)
- **First Sales Hires** — Using founder conversations to build and learn from the documented playbook
- **Fundraisers** — Need revenue traction to derisk Series A; founder-led sales proves repeatable motion faster than hiring
- **Lean Operations Teams** — Limited budget; founder-led sales with strong process beats hiring sales rep and discovering motion is wrong

## Use Cases

- **Closing first 10 customers** → Use warm introductions from your network, run structured discovery calls, document objections, and build a repeatable process before hiring
- **Validating pricing** → Learn what customers will actually pay (not what surveys say); adjust pricing based on real willingness-to-pay data across segments
- **Building your objection playbook** → Capture every objection raised, categorize them by theme, and practice responses so your future sales team inherits working answers
- **Deciding whether to hire sales** → Close 15+ customers personally; document the process; when you can articulate the repeatable motion, you're ready to hire
- **Understanding your ideal customer** → After 10-15 calls, patterns emerge (company size, industry, maturity, pain points); use this to filter future prospects and focus energy
- **Staying close to market feedback** — Even after hiring a sales team, founder involvement in early calls catches market shifts that pure sales metrics miss
- **Building credibility with early customers** → Founder involvement signals commitment and flexibility; use it to lock in expansion and referrals early

## Connectors

- **Gmail** — Keep all customer discovery threads in one place; never lose a conversation thread or objection that came up via email; searchable history
- **HubSpot** — Log every conversation with time stamps, objections raised, and pricing reactions; track patterns across customers automatically; run reports on common objections
- **Google Calendar** — Schedule discovery calls with reminders to prep; keep talk tracks and customer context attached to calendar events; prep checklists
- **Notion** — Build your objection playbook collaboratively; document discovery framework and customer profiles as they emerge; shared across co-founders
- **Airtable** — Create a customer tracker with company size, industry, pain points, objections, pricing reactions, and status; filter and sort to see patterns fast
- **Slack** — Share wins and learnings with your team; celebrate first customer, keep momentum visible

## Setup

Start by preparing your discovery framework (5-8 questions that surface pain, not features) and audit your warm network list for qualified prospects. Aim for 2-3 discovery calls per week maximum (quality over quantity). After each call, spend 15 minutes documenting objections raised, pricing reactions, and what you learned. Create a simple spreadsheet to track patterns (company size, industry, pain, objection, price reaction). After 10 calls, you'll see patterns forming; your ICP will emerge. By 20 calls, you'll have a documented playbook ready to hand off to your first sales hire, complete with objection responses they can actually use.

## What You Get

- **Discovery Framework** — Five questions that uncover real pain without pitching; prevents feature-focused conversations and builds trust early
- **Objection Log** — Every objection documented with your response and what you learned; becomes training material and sales playbook for future hires
- **Pricing Intelligence** — Real willingness-to-pay data across different customer segments and company sizes; actual price elasticity curves from conversations
- **Repeatable Sales Motion** — After 15-20 customers, patterns emerge (ideal customer profile, top objections, success criteria); this is your scalable playbook
- **Customer Playbook Template** — Document for your first sales hire so they don't reinvent the wheel; includes talk tracks, closing frameworks, and battle cards
- **Founder Advantage Checklist** — What makes you win (roadmap commitment, flexibility, decision-making speed) vs. what a sales hire can't do; defensible edge
- **Ideal Customer Profile** — Company size, industry, maturity, and pain points that convert fastest; filter prospects and focus energy efficiently
- **Market Validation** — Documented proof that your product solves real, measurable problems customers will pay for; defensible for fundraising conversations

## First Steps

1. Write your discovery framework (5-8 questions). Test it on your co-founders first; iterate until it feels natural and conversational.
2. List 20 warm leads from your network (people who know you, could benefit from your product, will take a call from you).
3. Schedule 2-3 calls this week. Spend 10 minutes prepping (review their company, industry, likely pain points based on what you know).
4. After each call, spend 15 minutes documenting: What pain did they describe? What objection came up? How did they react to pricing? What did you learn?
5. After 5 calls, you'll see your first pattern. After 10 calls, you'll have a framework worth testing. After 20, you'll have a documented playbook ready to hand off to your first sales hire.

## Key Insight

Most founders hire a sales person too early (before 5 customers, before patterns are clear). The founder should close the first 10-20 customers because you learn faster than anyone you could hire, you can commit to roadmap items competitors can't, and you build the repeatable motion from actual data, not theory.

The second insight: every objection is gold. Objections aren't rejections—they're data. They tell you what's preventing customers from saying yes. When you collect and categorize objections, you're building the training manual for your first sales hire. A sales hire without this playbook will spend months reinventing answers to questions you already solved.

Once you've documented the pattern across 15+ customers, you have something to teach. Until then, a sales hire will invent the process from scratch (usually wrong) or will chase shiny prospects that don't fit your ideal customer. Sell personally now so you can scale predictably later. Your founder-led sales isn't a temporary stage—it's the foundation of everything that comes next.
