# Onboard New Hires

> Structure first 90 days for belonging and clarity. Listen first, assign work second.

## What It Does

Structure the first 90 days to establish belonging, build relationships, deliver visible wins, and clarify success criteria. Prevents the isolation and unclear expectations that lead to early-hire attrition. Combines pre-onboarding context sharing, immediate pairing to prevent day-one drowning, listening tours to understand the company, and structured 1:1s that focus on relationships as much as productivity.

## Who It's For

- **Managers onboarding first hires** — want to set up new employees for belonging and fast ramp, not just task completion
- **Startup founders & leaders** — creating intentional onboarding culture (not accidental chaos) as you scale
- **People & HR leaders** — standardizing onboarding quality so every new hire gets the same care
- **Technical leaders** — onboarding engineers and want them productive fast while preventing burnout
- **Scaling teams** — every 10 hires, onboarding matters more; inconsistency breaks culture
- **Retention-focused companies** — most early-hire departures come from day-one isolation; invest in first 90 days

## Use Cases

**Before new hire's first day** → Send philosophy docs, company context, and mental prep so they ramp faster → walk in informed, not drowning

**First day isolation prevention** → Assign onboarding buddy, structure pairing, plan non-work lunch with team → immediately belong instead of sitting alone

**Ramping technical hires** → Pair on actual systems, show architecture, explain decision-making patterns → productive much faster than reading docs alone

**Understanding company perspective** → Conduct listening tour where new hire interviews 8-12 people → synthesize into state-of-union they present to leadership → fresh eyes surface blind spots

**Early win visibility** → Identify achievable project in first 30 days that shows value → present to team/leadership → feel like contributor, not learner

**Clarifying role expectations** → Define 90-day, 1-year, and 2-year success visions together → prevent month-6 surprise misalignment

**Building genuine relationships** → Create "relationship design" conversation early → ask how they work, what they need → relationships become explicit, not accidental

## Connectors

Works standalone with clear documentation and manager discipline. Richer with:
- **Google Calendar** — structure meeting schedule, block pairing time, schedule listening tour
- **Slack** — async welcome, team introductions, daily standups, quick questions during ramp
- **Notion** — onboarding checklist, company philosophy library, listening tour notes
- **GitHub** — first coding assignments, architecture overview, key codebases (technical roles)
- **Google Drive** — onboarding docs, company context, strategy, values reference

## Setup

1. **Prepare pre-arrival materials** — company philosophy, key docs, Slack norms, team member context
2. **Assign onboarding buddy** — peer-level person they'll work with, coach them on role
3. **Schedule first week pairing** — don't let day one be reading docs alone
4. **Plan listening tour** — identify 8-12 key people to interview in first 30 days
5. **Define 90-day success** — align on what "ramped" looks like before they start
6. **Protect 1:1 time** — weekly for first 90 days, no cancellations

## What You Get

- **Pre-arrival documentation** — company context that lets them hit the ground informed
- **First-week structure** — daily pairing, golden ritual teaching, relationship focus (not drowning)
- **Listening tour framework** — 8-12 interviews that surface company blind spots and new hire perspective
- **90-day success vision** — clear, specific outcomes so both sides know what "ramp" means
- **Early win identification** — achievable project in first 30 days that shows contribution
- **Relationship design template** — explicit conversation about working style, communication preferences
- **Monthly 1:1 framework** — first month (listening), second month (work review), third month (ownership)
- **90-day review rubric** — formal check-in that calibrates next 9 months
- **Faster productivity** — new hires who feel they belong ramp faster and stay longer

## Key Insights

**Belonging happens day one or not at all.** New hires who are isolated on day one often never recover. Pairing and immediate connection are non-negotiable.

**Listen before assigning.** Your job first 30 days is to understand them, not load them with work. Listening tour shows respect and surfaces blind spots.

**Early wins beat task completion.** One small shipped project visible to the team matters more than ten tasks completed silently. Visibility creates belonging.

**Golden rituals teach culture.** How you run standups, make decisions, and communicate says more than any handbook. Show them by doing them together.

**90-day clarity prevents month-six surprises.** Aligned expectations about what "ramped" looks like prevents the month-six conversation where expectations don't match reality.
