# Manage Media Relations

> Media relations is built long before you need it. Know the journalists, know what they care about, and pitch them stories—not product announcements.

## What It Does

This skill teaches you to identify journalists by beat, pitch story angles instead of product launches, prepare executives for media interviews, and respond to crises with transparency and speed. It includes a journalist database template, pitch strategies (personalized vs. blast, exclusive vs. open), media training for CEOs (key messages, tough questions, quotable language), and a crisis communication playbook with timelines and tone guidance. Earned media (real journalism) is worth 3-5x paid media because it's credible, lasting, and carries third-party validation that money can't buy.

## Who It's For

- **PR and Communications leads** building and managing media relationships, pitching story angles, and amplifying company narrative effectively

- **Founders and CEOs** preparing for media interviews, thinking through key messages, and handling crisis communications under pressure

- **Marketing leaders** coordinating product launches with PR strategy and influencing earned media coverage and tone

- **Operations leaders** preparing for potential crises and wanting to understand clear communication protocols and timelines

- **Investor relations teams** building credibility with financial journalists and industry-specific press covering your market

- **Startup founders** building thought leadership and positioning as industry experts independent of product announcements

## Use Cases

- **"We're launching a product and want PR coverage beyond just the launch announcement."** → Identify the trend your product is part of, pitch journalists who cover that beat with story angles (not product news), and seed early adopters to build momentum.

- **"Our CEO hates media interviews and doesn't know what to say."** → Build key messages (2-3 things to always convey), prepare for tough questions with honest answers, and do a 30-min mock interview to build confidence.

- **"A negative story is about to break and we don't know how to respond."** → Have a crisis playbook ready: issue a response within 4 hours, be transparent and action-oriented, provide weekly updates until completely resolved.

- **"We're getting no press coverage despite a solid product."** → Stop blasting generic press releases. Build a journalist database, read what each person covers, and pitch them stories they actually care about (trends, insights, data—not features).

- **"We don't have relationships with journalists in our space."** → Start with thought leadership (CEO byline, speaking engagements, original research) and availability for comments. Journalists remember people who respond quickly with useful insight.

- **"Product Hunt and Hacker News aren't applicable for our B2B product."** → Build relationships with industry-specific journalists and analysts instead. They influence enterprise buyer decisions far more than startup communities do.

## Connectors

Works standalone with direct journalist outreach and personal research, but richer with:

- **Gmail** (track pitches and develop ongoing email relationships)
- **Slack** (broadcast coverage wins and coordinate crisis responses in real-time)
- **Google Sheets** (maintain journalist database with beat coverage and relationship history)
- **Notion** (document crisis communication plans and media talking points centrally)

## Setup

Spend 3 hours researching journalists who cover your space: identify 3-5 target publications and 2-3 journalists per publication. Read 5-10 recent articles each to understand their beat and editorial angle. Document in a spreadsheet: journalist name, publication, email, beat, recent articles, and editorial angle. This database is your most valuable asset and worth updating quarterly.

## What You Get

- **Journalist database by beat** with editorial angles and recent coverage patterns (your roadmap for smart pitching)

- **Story angle pitches** (not product announcements) that journalists actually want to cover and pursue professionally

- **Media training for executives** with key messages, tough questions, and quotable language for interviews and statements

- **Crisis communication playbook** with timelines, tone guidelines, and what to say (or not say) in tense, high-stakes moments

- **Pitch templates** (personalized trend pitches, exclusive offers, responses to journalist inquiries and requests)

- **Quarterly media review** to measure coverage quality, relationship strength, and which angles actually worked in practice

## Key Insight

Most PR fails because it's volume-based (blast 100 journalists with the same release, hope 2 respond) instead of relationship-based (understand what 10 key journalists care about and pitch them stories they want to cover). Journalists get 200+ pitches weekly—yours will be deleted unless it's personalized, backed by insight and data, and aligned with what they actually write about. Start building relationships 12 months before you need them, and coverage becomes predictable and reliably strong instead of random and reactive.
