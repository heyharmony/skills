# Plan Team Offsites

> Offsites aren't just team bonding—they're concentrated bursts where your distributed team solves real problems and creates shared memory that lasts.

## What It Does

An offsite done wrong is exhausting: packed agendas, icebreaker games, no real strategy work. An offsite done right solves problems your team can't solve async, builds genuine trust, and results in decisions everyone commits to. This skill helps you design offsites (or quarterly "bursts" for distributed teams) that tackle your actual business problem—whether that's conflict resolution, roadmap alignment, or trust-building—while balancing deep work with connection. It ensures remote people have equal voice, keeps the agenda focused on what matters, and turns the offsite into action.

## Who It's For

- **Engineering and Product Leaders** scaling teams and needing to align on strategy, resolve conflict, or build trust quickly.
- **Distributed Team Leaders** who use quarterly offsites to maintain velocity and connection across time zones.
- **Operations and People Leaders** designing offsite logistics and agenda to maximize impact.
- **New Managers or VPs** joining a team and needing to understand dynamics and build alignment in-person.
- **Scrum Masters and Agile Coaches** running effective sprints or planning ceremonies with co-located and remote teams.
- **Founders** using offsites to navigate strategic pivots, scale challenges, or team transitions.

### Pain Points They Face

- Offsites feel like a waste of time and money: packed agendas, no real outcomes, or just soft skills training that doesn't address underlying issues.
- Remote team members feel excluded or unheard, even when present on Zoom.
- Conflict is avoided rather than addressed; people leave with unresolved tension.
- No clear decisions or action items result; it's fun but not strategic.
- Distributed teams lack shared memory and trust because offsites happen too rarely or don't build connection.
- Time zone and travel logistics make offsites hard to coordinate or unfair to some people.

## Use Cases

- **Quarterly burst cycles** → Remote teams do quarterly 1–3 day offsites to concentrate on hard problems and rebuild connection.
- **Strategic decision point** → Roadmap change, platform decision, or major pivot needs synchronous debate and alignment.
- **After merger or scaling** → New team or many new hires haven't built trust; offsite accelerates relationship-building and alignment.
- **Conflict or misalignment** → Teams debating strategy in async channels for weeks get nowhere; resolve it in-person in 2 hours with structured debate.
- **New leadership arrives** → New exec or manager joins and needs to understand team dynamics and decide on direction fast.
- **Trust repair** → After a failed project or tension, use an offsite to reset norms and rebuild psychological safety.

## Connectors

Works standalone with async pre-work and planning, but richer with:

- **Google Calendar**: Maps team availability and identifies optimal offsite timing.
- **Slack**: Surfaces team tensions, collaboration gaps, and what people are thinking (anonymous feedback forms).
- **Notion**: Stores pre-work, agendas, and post-offsite action items and decisions.
- **Linear / Asana**: Tracks action items and ownership that result from the offsite.

## Setup

1. **Define purpose** (most critical): What problem are you solving? Conflict, alignment, trust-building, strategy decision? One sentence.
2. **Assess context**: Is there unresolved conflict? Remote/co-located inequality? Budget and time constraints?
3. **Send pre-work** (1–2 weeks before): Required reading, anonymous feedback form on concerns.
4. **Design agenda** with three parts: Warm-up (connection), Deep Work (solve the problem), Closing (decisions and celebration).
5. **Make it inclusive**: Everyone on video/laptops together; shared boards so remote people see in real time; async voting for equal voice.
6. **Time-box ruthlessly**: Deep work sessions run long; keep time boundaries so energy carries through.
7. **Capture decisions** clearly: Who owns what? By when?
8. **Post-offsite actions**: Summary email, action items in tracking system, 1:1s with quiet/remote people, feedback survey.

## What You Get

- **Clear decisions** from synchronous debate—things that take weeks async happen in hours.
- **Shared memory and trust** in the team, especially for distributed people.
- **Action items with owners and deadlines**, not vague notes.
- **Conflict addressed** rather than avoided; people feel heard even when they disagree.
- **Remote equality**: Distributed team members have equal voice and don't feel like second-class participants.
- **Quarterly rhythm** (for distributed teams): Predictable offsites every 12 weeks maintain connection and momentum between async sprints.
