# Design Team Rituals

> Replace vague recurring meetings with named, templated rituals that everyone knows by their first Friday—and anyone can facilitate.

## What It Does

Most teams have meetings; great teams have rituals. The difference: rituals have a memorable name, a clear template, a consistent purpose, and every new hire learns them in their first week. This skill transforms your recurring meetings (standups, retrospectives, planning sessions, all-hands) into repeatable ceremonies with documented flow, time allocations, and facilitator guides. It then embeds those rituals into onboarding so they become part of your team's DNA.

## Who It's For

- **Team Leads and Engineering Managers** who want to eliminate meeting bloat and create structure that survives their absence or transition.
- **Operations Leaders** designing or refreshing team cadence and ensuring rituals scale with growth.
- **Distributed Team Leaders** who need to ritualize what would otherwise happen organically in co-located spaces.
- **Founders and Early-Stage Leaders** building team culture through intentional, repeatable practices.
- **People Ops and Onboarding Owners** who want new hires to absorb team rhythm and norms from day one.
- **Scrum Masters and Agile Coaches** designing or improving ceremony effectiveness (standups, retros, planning).

### Pain Points They Face

- Too many recurring meetings (10+); half feel purposeless. People dread calendar.
- Meetings lack structure; each facilitator runs them differently. No consistency.
- New hires don't know how the team works (standup, retro, planning cadence) until month two or three.
- Remote and co-located people experience rituals differently—some feel left out.
- When a facilitator or manager leaves, rituals disappear or change significantly.
- Meetings run long, wander, or produce no clear output or action.

## Use Cases

- **Building a new team** → Design rituals from day one so they're embedded in culture, not added later.
- **Scaling quickly** → Rituals make it possible for new hires to learn team rhythm fast; culture becomes repeatable, not osmosis-dependent.
- **Meeting fatigue** → Audit recurring meetings, cut the ones without purpose, and elevate the valuable ones into memorable rituals.
- **Distributed team struggles** → Async and remote teams need ritualized communication (demos, retros, planning) because hallway conversations don't happen.
- **Leadership transition** → Document rituals so the team's rhythm survives a manager or leader change.
- **Failed previous ceremonies** → You tried standup and it died; now redesign it with facilitator guide, clear output, and adoption plan.

## Connectors

Works standalone with calendar audit and team input, but richer with:

- **Google Calendar**: Maps existing meeting cadence; identifies candidates for ritualization or removal.
- **Slack**: Surfaces which conversations could be templated, moved async, or wrapped into a ceremony.
- **Notion**: Stores ritual templates, facilitator guides, and decision logs.
- **GitHub / Linear**: Tracks technical rituals (sprint planning, code review cadence, retrospectives).

## Setup

1. List all recurring meetings (weekly, biweekly, monthly); ask "What outcome would disappear if we killed this?"
2. Keep only meetings where absence would break communication, delay decisions, or lose feedback.
3. For each keeper, design a ritual: name it, write a one-sentence purpose, create a template with time-boxed flow.
4. Pilot one ritual for 2–3 cycles; refine based on feedback.
5. Create a one-page ritual calendar; share with the team.
6. Embed rituals into onboarding: new hire learns them week one, participates in full cycle, manager confirms understanding.
7. Rotate facilitators so the ritual doesn't depend on one person.
8. Review quarterly; retire rituals that no longer serve, adjust templates based on team feedback.

## What You Get

- **Ritual inventory**: A small, focused set of named ceremonies (usually 3–6 per team).
- **Templates for every ritual**: Documented flow, time allocations, facilitator notes, and output format so anyone can run it.
- **Onboarding clarity**: New hires learn the team's rhythm in week one; they can articulate why each ritual exists by month one.
- **Facilitator redundancy**: Because rituals are templated, anyone can facilitate; rituals survive leadership changes.
- **Distributed team equity**: Rituals have async versions or remote-first design so remote people aren't second-class participants.
- **Meeting culture reset**: Fewer, more intentional meetings; higher engagement because rituals have clear purpose and bounded time.
