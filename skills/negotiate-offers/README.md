# Negotiate Offers

> Money is what you get. Success factors are what you need. Negotiate success first.

## What It Does

This skill helps you negotiate compensation, role scope, and working conditions with a collaborative mindset and strategic approach. Instead of focusing only on salary, you'll identify the success factors that actually determine whether you'll thrive — role clarity, team quality, manager support, learning opportunities, and autonomy.

You'll research actual market value, understand your leverage, and frame negotiations as problem-solving conversations rather than adversarial demands. The skill walks you through negotiating in rounds, handling counteroffers, and knowing when to walk away.

## Who It's For

- **Candidates with job offers** who want to ensure they're getting market value and setting themselves up for success
- **People asking for raises** who need data and framing to back up requests
- **Career switchers** joining new companies or industries who worry about getting lowballed
- **Startup employees** evaluating equity offers and understanding what's realistic
- **Freelancers and contractors** negotiating rates and terms for the first time
- **People in recession environments** where salary is frozen but other levers exist (title, scope, flexibility, sign-on bonuses)
- **Internal mobility candidates** moving to a new team or role within the same company
- **Return offers** or boomerang candidates coming back after leaving

## Use Cases

- **You have a job offer and you're not sure if it's fair** → Use market data and success factors to negotiate confidently
- **You're transitioning careers** → Leverage your unique strengths (different perspective, transferable skills); negotiation shows confidence
- **You're in a bidding war** → Competing offers are your strongest leverage; use them strategically
- **Salary is frozen but you want better terms** → Negotiate for start date, title, scope, equity vesting, remote flexibility, anything except base
- **You're underpaid and want a raise** → Build the case, not the emotion; show impact, reference market data, frame as problem-solving
- **You're joining a startup with equity** → Understand what equity is worth (usually nothing); prioritize fair cash salary over promising equity
- **You're moving from contract to full-time** → Ensure benefits, equity vesting, and clarity around commitment are negotiated clearly
- **You received a counteroffer from your current employer** → Evaluate it against your original reason for leaving, not just money
- **You're joining a smaller company after big tech** → Adjust expectations for equity value but not your cash compensation

## Setup

No setup required. You'll use public market data (Levels.fyi, Blind, Glassdoor) and your own research. If you connect data sources, the skill can help you document and iterate on negotiation strategy.

When Harmony conversation data exists — recorded interviews, hiring manager calls, recruiter conversations — the skill extracts signals about what the company values, what flexibility exists, and what concerns they raised. This gives you leverage most candidates don't have: you negotiate from what they actually said, not what you assume.

## What You Get

- A framework for identifying success factors that matter more than salary alone (role clarity, team, manager quality, learning path, autonomy)
- Market research showing what your role is actually worth (by level, company stage, geography, skill set)
- Leverage assessment (where does your power come from? Competing offers, specific skills, market timing?)
- A negotiation strategy broken into rounds (success factors first, then comp, then edge cases)
- Templates for collaborative framing (questions instead of demands, how to keep tone professional)
- Counteroffer evaluation framework (is it good enough? What's the real reason you wanted to leave?)
- Red flags and walk-away conditions (when to say no, even if offer is good)
- Decision framework showing how to evaluate final offers and commit confidently

## Key Insight

87% of the time when you ask for more, you get it. The risk of asking is almost always lower than the cost of not asking. But the real unlock isn't salary — it's negotiating for the success factors (headcount, authority, resources, reporting structure) that determine whether you'll actually thrive in the role. Win the conditions, and the compensation follows.
