# Define Brand Voice

**Tagline:** Extract your brand's authentic voice from real customer conversations and codify it into a tone guide your entire team can follow.

## What It Does

Instead of hiring an agency to write aspirational brand voice guidelines, Define Brand Voice mines your actual customer conversations in Harmony to discover how your team *really* talks to customers, prospects, and each other. It then codifies those patterns into a complete tone playbook: voice attributes, tone spectrum, vocabulary rules, sample copy across channels, and a one-page voice card every writer can reference.

The result is a brand voice guide grounded in evidence, not guesswork—authentic, consistent, and actually used by your team.

## Who It's For

- **Marketing leaders** building a brand book or refreshing tired guidelines
- **Content teams** struggling with inconsistent voice across channels
- **Sales leaders** onboarding new reps and wanting them to sound like your best performers
- **Customer success teams** looking to standardize tone in support interactions
- **Hiring managers** needing to train new customer-facing hires fast
- **Rebranding teams** post-acquisition or after major pivots

## Use Cases

- **Launching a new brand** — Start with conversation data, not a blank worksheet
- **Consolidating voice after a merger** — Let call transcripts show you which voice is stronger
- **Onboarding writers and marketers** — Give them real examples, not vague tone words
- **Fixing inconsistent messaging** — Diagnose whether the problem is process, training, or unclear guidelines
- **Entering a new market** — Analyze calls with your new audience to see if your voice needs to shift
- **Writing a company style guide** — Use this as your tone foundation; layer in visual brand elements separately

## Connectors

- **Harmony** (required) — Pull conversation transcripts and call data
- **Slack** (optional) — Share voice card and get team feedback in real-time
- **Notion** (optional) — Build your voice guide collaboratively, embed sample copy
- **Google Drive** (optional) — Export voice card as a Google Doc for easy sharing and printing
- **HubSpot** (optional) — Map voice attributes to sales and customer lifecycle stages

## Setup

1. **Pull 10–20 recent conversation transcripts** from Harmony covering sales, support, and team discussions
2. **Identify your best performers** — whose calls feel most authentic and close the most deals?
3. **Note conversation patterns** — formal vs. casual, technical vs. plain, direct vs. diplomatic
4. **Run Define Brand Voice** — provide transcript excerpts or let the skill analyze exported calls
5. **Review and refine** — your voice attributes, tone spectrum, and vocabulary rules
6. **Export and share** — voice card goes on Slack, in Notion, printed for the team

## What You Get

- **Voice Attributes Matrix** — 3–4 core attributes with do/don't examples rooted in real calls
- **Tone Spectrum** — how your voice shifts across sales, support, marketing, product, and internal channels
- **Vocabulary Guide** — preferred terms, banned jargon, and customer-friendly alternatives
- **Sample Copy** — realistic examples across five channels showing voice in action
- **Voice Card** — one-page printable reference for your team's desk or Slack
- **Conversation Audit Trail** — which calls were analyzed and what patterns emerged
