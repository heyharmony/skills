# Lookalike Customer Finder

> Stop hunting blind. Find 1,000 prospects that match your best customers — replicate what's working instead of experimenting with every segment.

## What It Does

Analyzes your best customers — their company attributes, how they found you, what they were solving, what convinced them to buy — and reverse-engineers the exact profile that predicts success. Then it identifies all prospects in the market that match that profile. Instead of "let's sell to SaaS companies" (vague, low conversion), you get "let's sell to Series B SaaS, 50–200 people, in vertical markets with an ops-first culture who just hired" (specific, high conversion). The output includes the winning profile, disqualification criteria, where to find lookalikes, and outreach hooks that reference your best customers' wins.

## Who It's For

- **Sales leaders** who want to build SDR targeting strategy around replicable wins, not random guessing
- **Growth/go-to-market teams** planning outbound campaigns and needing to nail the target account list
- **SDRs** who want to focus on high-probability prospects instead of spray-and-pray outreach
- **Product marketers** who need to understand which customer segments drive highest LTV and retention
- **Founders** scaling sales and wanting to replicate early wins systematically

## Use Cases

- You have 5 amazing customers (high retention, high NPS, high expansion) and 10 mediocre ones → Find 500 more like the amazing ones, ignore the rest
- Your SDR says "I'm building an outbound list, what companies should I target?" → Provide the exact profile (size, industry, hiring signals, growth stage) based on data
- You just closed a $50K deal that's expanding fast → Analyze why they won, find 100 companies with the same profile, prioritize them
- Sales review comes up and manager asks "where should we focus next quarter?" → Show which customer segments drive highest LTV and build a target list around them
- You have deal data but no conversation context → Use the CRM data to identify patterns, then pull conversations to confirm

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with these integrations:

- **HubSpot / Salesforce** — Pulls complete deal history and customer attributes so you can identify which customers are actually your "best" by revenue, retention, and expansion metrics
- **Slack** — Captures team discussions about which customers are most successful ("we love working with them") and surfaces tribal knowledge
- **Google Calendar** — Shows customer engagement patterns (call frequency, who's involved) that correlate with success
- **Gmail** — Surfaces customer email threads that reveal pain points and buying triggers from the sales process

## Setup

Install and go. Works best when you have 5+ high-value customers whose conversation data exists in Harmony (so you can understand their buying journey). If you only have CRM data, the skill still works but with less depth. Pure CRM analysis: "they're all 50–200 people, in SaaS, Series B." With conversations: "they're all 50–200 people, in SaaS, Series B, had an ops-first culture, all found us by referral, all had a champion at VP level, and saw ROI in <30 days." Connect HubSpot or Salesforce to sync the winning profile back to your CRM for ongoing matching.

## What You Get

A detailed profile of your winning customer type: their company size range, industries, growth stage, and revenue. Behavioral patterns that predict success: how they find you, how fast they buy, who the champion is, what triggers them to search. A before-and-after understanding of their problem and solution. Disqualification criteria so you know who NOT to chase. A list of where to find lookalikes — specific LinkedIn search filters, industry databases, communities, and referral sources. An estimated count of qualified prospects in the market. And specific outreach hooks that reference your best customers' wins without naming them.

## Why This Matters

Most teams spray-and-pray outreach, targeting "anyone in SaaS with 50+ people." This skill cuts that down to the highest-probability segment. By reverse-engineering why your best customers won, you can predict which prospects will convert fastest, churn slowest, and expand most. Your SDRs focus on high-probability targets instead of random lists, conversion rates go up, and CAC goes down.

## Real-World Impact

Your team closes 10 amazing customers and 40 mediocre ones. Without this skill: you build a generic ICP and continue closing 10 amazing and 40 mediocre. With this skill: you identify exactly what the 10 have in common, find 500 more like them, focus SDR outreach there, and close 30+ amazing customers with almost zero mediocre ones. Your pipeline quality transforms.
