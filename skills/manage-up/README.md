# Manage Up

> Communicate with leadership like a peer. Bring solutions, not problems. Position yourself as an ally who reduces their burden.

## What It Does

This skill teaches you to intentionally manage your relationship with your manager and leadership. It's about understanding their priorities, communicating with precision, bringing recommendations instead of just problems, and positioning yourself as someone who makes their life easier.

Includes structured communication templates (HPM updates, recommendation briefs), meeting prep guides, and frameworks for understanding what executives care about and how they make decisions.

## Who It's For

- Anyone who wants to influence their manager and leadership effectively
- Individual contributors and leaders stepping into more influence and visibility
- People who feel misaligned with their manager or confused about company direction
- Teams wanting to communicate more effectively with executives
- People preparing for promotions or bigger roles who need to get on leadership's radar
- High-performers who feel their work goes unrecognized at the executive level
- Anyone stepping into a skip-level relationship or cross-functional partnership

## Use Cases

- Sending weekly or bi-weekly updates that land with your manager (and get remembered)
- Getting buy-in on a big recommendation or proposal with clear business case
- Navigating a skip-level meeting with executives confidently
- Bringing a problem with a clear recommendation (not just venting or asking for help)
- Understanding and influencing company strategy through your manager
- Building credibility with leadership through consistent, clear communication
- Preparing for a career conversation or promotion discussion
- Getting unblocked faster by understanding what your manager actually needs from you

## Connectors

- **Slack** – Enables quick async updates and unblocking from leadership
- **Google Calendar** – Syncs meetings with leadership and time blocks for prep
- **Notion** – Stores running updates, recommendations, and communication history
- **Google Drive** – Pulls company strategy documents and executive priorities

## Setup

1. Connect Slack for sending quick updates and getting unblocked quickly
2. Link Google Calendar to understand your manager's schedule, priorities, and patterns
3. Create a Notion database for tracking recommendations, decisions, and outcomes over time
4. Before big presentations or decisions, use the skill to structure your communication

## What You Get

- HPM update template (Highlights-Priorities-Metrics) for regular async communication that saves time
- Recommendation brief structure that brings options and data, not just problems
- Meeting prep guide to understand what executives care about and how to position your needs
- "Chapter 1 first" approach for executive communication (headline before details, respecting their time)
- Decision log to track outcomes and reference decisions over time for credibility
- Frameworks for managing up with different manager styles (hands-on, delegating, anxious, growth-minded, etc.)
- Communication calendar to know when to communicate, how often, and through what channel
- Escalation framework to know when to loop your manager in and how to present escalations
