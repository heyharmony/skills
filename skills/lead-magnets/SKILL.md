## Purpose
Create and optimize gated content (lead magnets) that attract qualified prospects, capture emails, and move them toward a buying decision. This skill helps you choose the right format, gate level, and distribution channel for your audience stage.

## When to Use
- You need to build an email list or grow a segment
- You're planning to launch a product, feature, or campaign
- You want to repurpose existing content into a revenue driver
- Your sales team reports a knowledge gap in prospects (indicating a content opportunity)
- You're trying to reduce lead acquisition cost (CPL)
- You need to validate product-market fit through demand generation
- You're entering a new market and don't yet have buyer data

## Instructions

**Step 1: Audit Your Buyer Data**
Pull conversation data from customer calls, sales calls, and support tickets. Note the exact questions prospects ask at each stage (awareness/consideration/decision). These are your magnet topics. If you lack conversation data, start with your three biggest objections from sales calls and your three most common support tickets.

**Step 2: Match Format to Buyer Stage**
- Awareness: Checklist, cheat sheet, assessment quiz, webinar recording
- Consideration: Template, mini-course, comparison guide, case study collection
- Decision: ROI calculator, implementation checklist, vendor evaluation matrix

Avoid gating highly educational 101 content (it should be free). Gate content that solves a specific problem or reveals proprietary insight.

**Step 3: Choose Gating Strategy**
- Full gate: Entire asset behind form (highest friction, lowest volume, highest quality)
- Partial gate: Free preview + email for full download (balanced conversion and lead quality)
- Content upgrade: Free post/article + gated bonus material (lowest friction, drives email growth)

Decide based on your CPL target, list size goal, and lead quality threshold.

**Step 4: Design Landing Page and Form**
Landing page should: headline that mirrors prospect pain point or promise, 2-3 benefit bullets, form below fold (name/email minimum; avoid over-collecting until you have volume), no navigation menu, urgency element (limited seats for webinar, limited availability for guide), and trust signal (customer logo, stat, certification).

Form friction rule: If gating to awareness-stage content, 2-field form (name, email). If gating decision-stage content, 3-4 fields (company size, buying timeline). Never exceed 7 fields.

**Step 5: Plan Delivery Method**
Choose based on lead magnet format and audience:
- Email: Immediate PDF delivery, drip sequence option
- Hosted portal: For multi-asset resources (checklist + template + guide)
- Slack/community: For engaged existing audiences
- Direct download: Simple PDF, low friction
- Webinar platform: If format is live or recorded session

**Step 6: Build Distribution Plan**
Layer 3-5 channels: owned (email, blog CTA, popup), earned (social share, community mention), paid (LinkedIn, Google Ads targeting keywords), and partnerships (co-marketing, guest posts, affiliate). Allocate budget to the two highest-intent channels first.

**Step 7: Set Up Measurement**
Track: landing page conversion rate (target: 25-40% for gated content), cost per lead (CPL), lead-to-customer rate (%, to validate relevance), and time to first follow-up touch. Establish A/B test plan—start with headline variation on landing page, then test form fields and CTA copy.

## Example

**Company: Mosaic HR** (B2B HR tech for mid-market)
*Goal: Build email list of HR directors and generate sales meetings for their new "Diversity & Inclusion" platform module*

---

**Lead Magnet Recommendation**
- Format: Interactive assessment quiz (8-10 questions)
- Gating level: Full gate + email delivery
- Target buyer stage: Awareness (HR directors don't yet know they need D&I tooling)
- Why: Quizzes are engaging, position Mosaic as expert, and self-qualify (quiz score indicates readiness). HR directors have no friction taking a quiz, and the email triggers a 5-day nurture sequence.

**Content Outline**
1. Quiz intro: "How Inclusive Is Your Hiring Process? Take This 2-Minute Assessment"
2. Question logic scoring: low (basic practices), medium (some structure), high (mature program)
3. Score-based results page showing: current state, industry benchmark, top 3 gaps, and a 1-page gap-closure guide (instantly downloadable, no second gate)
4. Results email: personalizes based on score tier, includes next steps (watch 4-minute video, book office hours with Mosaic consultant)

**Gating & Landing Page**
- Form fields: First name, email, company size (dropdown), current D&I tooling (yes/no)
- Landing page headline: "Is Your D&I Hiring Process Mature? Find Out in 2 Minutes"
- Above-the-fold: "Trusted by 200+ mid-market companies" + logo carousel (5 customer logos)
- Urgency signal: "Complete by March 31 to get personalized benchmark report"

**Delivery & Distribution Plan**
- Primary: Email to existing customer base (HR users) + existing prospect list (1,000 warm leads)
- Secondary: LinkedIn organic post from Mosaic CEO (share quiz link, offer 5-min consultation for top scorers); LinkedIn paid retargeting to HR director audience ($500/month, 2-week test); blog post: "5 Diversity Hiring Mistakes We See" with quiz CTA mid-post
- Promotion timeline: Soft launch to customer email base week 1 (baseline), paid push weeks 2-4, organic social promotion ongoing

**Measurement & Success Metrics**
- Landing page conversion rate: Target 35% (quiz is low friction)
- CPL: Target $15-25 (assuming 200-300 leads in month 1, $3k-5k spend across channels)
- Lead-to-customer validation: Track % of quiz takers who open nurture email, watch video, or accept office hours (sign of genuine interest); measure # who convert to paid trial in 90 days (Mosaic's top-of-funnel goal is 5% conversion to trial)
- A/B test: Week 2 test two headlines—"Is Your D&I Process Mature?" vs. "Your D&I Hiring Checklist (And 2-Min Self-Audit)"—measure which drives higher conversion and lead-to-trial rate

## Edge Cases

1. **No existing conversation data**: If you lack prospect questions or objections, start with your sales team's three biggest deal-loss reasons. Use those as magnet topics. Run a 2-week experiment: distribute one magnet, measure engagement and lead quality before scaling.

2. **Sensitive or proprietary content**: If your magnet reveals competitive advantage (e.g., pricing strategy, secret formula), use a partial gate or content upgrade to protect it while still building the list. Require both email and company verification before download.

3. **Long sales cycle (6+ months)**: Build a nurture sequence, not a one-off magnet. Create 3-4 magnets targeting different stages (awareness, consideration, decision) and route leads to the right sequence based on quiz response or landing page source.

4. **Existing content inventory**: Audit first. You likely have a blog post, case study, or template that can be repackaged as a checklist or guide in 2 hours. Repurposing is faster than creation and tests demand before you invest in original content.

5. **Low perceived value**: If your magnet feels generic (e.g., a generic "SaaS Best Practices" guide), it won't gate well. Make it specific: "How We Reduced Churn by 30% in 90 Days: The Exact Play-by-Play." Specificity = gateability.

6. **Multi-persona audiences**: Create separate magnets for different roles (e.g., HR director vs. recruiting manager). They have different pain points and buyer stages. One magnet rarely works across multiple personas.
