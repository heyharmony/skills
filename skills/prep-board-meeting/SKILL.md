## Purpose

Board meetings succeed or fail based on preparation. This skill mines the last 4-6 weeks of internal meetings — executive syncs, team updates, customer reviews, finance discussions — to surface the topics, risks, and wins that board members need to know. It structures everything into: consent agenda (approve without discussion), discussion items (need board input), and information items (FYI). It also tracks action items from the previous board meeting and flags which ones are complete, in progress, or stalled.

## When to Use

- **2 weeks before scheduled board meeting**: Start pull on relevant conversations and documents
- **When assembling board pre-read materials**: Synthesize conversations into digestible summaries for each topic
- **After previous board meeting (for action item tracking)**: Monitor progress on commitments made in prior meeting
- **Board agenda review with CEO**: Align on discussion priorities and time allocations
- **First board meeting with new board members**: Provide context and background on open issues
- **Emergency or special board session**: Quickly compile critical info for unscheduled meeting

## Instructions

1. **Pull action items from last board meeting minutes** — Check status against recent conversations. Note which have progressed, which are blocked, and which appear abandoned. Flag any surprises (items claimed complete that aren't, or new blockers).

2. **Mine last 4-6 weeks of executive meetings for board-relevant topics** — Scan executive syncs, finance reviews, customer council meetings, engineering standups. Classify each: consent (administrative, low risk), discussion (needs board input), or information (FYI, no decision required).

3. **Identify items needing board approval** — Budget changes, hiring decisions, strategic shifts, partnership moves, funding decisions. Flag source conversations and decision deadlines.

4. **Build agenda with time allocations** — Consent agenda (5 min total), discussion items (15-20 min each), CEO update (10 min), customer wins/risks (10 min), metrics review (15 min), close/action items (10 min). Adjust based on priority.

5. **Draft pre-read summaries for each discussion item** — One-page maximum per topic. Include: situation, options considered, recommendation, financial/strategic impact, timeline for decision, and next steps if approved.

6. **Anticipate board questions** — For each discussion item, list 3-5 likely questions board members will ask. Prepare answers grounded in data. Note which directors have raised similar concerns in past meetings.

7. **Compile metrics dashboard** — KPIs the board tracks: ARR, net revenue retention, magic number, CAC payback, runway, hiring plan status, customer concentration, pipeline coverage. Show trend lines (2-3 quarters) and commentary on variance.

8. **Note board member context** — Who's been vocal about what in past meetings. Who has specific expertise (sales, engineering, finance). New members who need onboarding on stalled issues. Any known positions or concerns on upcoming discussion topics.

9. **Generate follow-up template** — Action items from this meeting: owner, description, deadline. Use this post-meeting to track execution and build the prior-actions list for next meeting.

## Output Format

**Board Meeting Prep Package** includes:

- **Previous Action Item Tracker**: Items from last meeting, current status (complete/in progress/stalled), owner, and blocker if any
- **Agenda**: Consent items (list only), discussion items (with time, owner), information items (brief summary), time allocations
- **Pre-Read Summaries**: One per discussion item; situation, options, recommendation, impact, timeline
- **Anticipated Q&A**: For each discussion item, 3-5 questions with prepared answers
- **Metrics Dashboard**: KPIs with trend lines and commentary
- **Board Member Notes**: Director context, expertise, positions, new member background
- **Follow-Up Template**: Post-meeting action item tracker with owners and deadlines

## Example

**Meridian** (B2B logistics SaaS, Series A, 4-person board: CEO, 2 VCs, 1 customer board member)

**Q1 Board Meeting Prep (scheduled March 15)**

### Previous Action Items
- Q4: Hire VP Engineering → In Progress (3 candidates in final round, decision by March 10)
- Q4: Evaluate ERP system for finance → Complete (Implemented NetSuite Jan 15)
- Q4: Resolve customer concentration risk (top 3 = 45% ARR) → Stalled (sales pipeline thinner than expected)

### Agenda (90 minutes)
1. Consent: Approve Q1 financial statements (2 min)
2. Consent: Approve option pool refresh to 18% (2 min)
3. Discussion: International expansion proposal — UK market entry (15 min) [Product lead: Sarah]
4. Discussion: VP Engineering hire decision + onboarding plan (10 min) [CEO: James]
5. Information: Customer concentration risk update & mitigation plan (10 min)
6. Metrics review & Q2 outlook (15 min)
7. Close & action items (5 min)

### Pre-Read Summary: International Expansion (UK)
**Situation**: UK sales inbound has increased 40% YoY. Three enterprise customers requesting UK legal entity. Current strategy focuses on US; no dedicated UK effort yet.

**Options**: (1) No change — miss 12-18 month window; (2) Hire regional sales lead + localize product (3-4 month lift, $250K investment); (3) Partner with UK reseller (lower control, revenue share).

**Recommendation**: Option 2. UK market timing aligns with customer demand and Series B narrative. Hire regional sales lead Q2, launch localized version Q3. Expect 500K ARR from UK by end of year.

**Financial Impact**: $250K investment in Q2-Q3, payback ~12 months at conservative 400K ARR.

**Timeline**: Hire decision end of March, launch by Q3.

### Anticipated Q&A: International Expansion
- *VC1: "Why UK first vs. other markets?"* → Customer pull is strongest in UK, English-speaking reduces localization lift, proven SaaS market.
- *VC2: "What's the reseller option economics?"* → Resellers take 30% margin; we'd net 70% but lose control of customer relationship. For enterprise, direct is critical.
- *Customer Director: "Will this distract from US roadmap?"* → Dedicated hire means US motion unaffected. Sarah remains US product lead.
- *CEO: "What if expansion stalls?"* → We can pivot to partner model. Sunk cost is salary + localization; customer lock-in limits downside.

### Metrics Dashboard

|--------|--------|---------------|-------|------------|
| ARR | $2.1M | $2.35M | ↑ | +11% QoQ, customer concentration remains 45% |
| NRR | 118% | 115% | ↓ | Expected seasonal, one renewal at lower spend |

| Runway | 18 mo | 22 mo | ↑ | NetSuite ops saving 15K/mo |
| CAC Payback | 14 mo | 13 mo | ↑ | Shorter sales cycles in mid-market |

### Board Member Notes
- **VC1 (Acme Partners)**: Former SaaS COO, detail-oriented on ops and finance. Push back on UK expansion unless cash flow is clear. Expect 3-4 probing questions.
- **VC2 (Sage Ventures)**: Ex-founder, bullish on geographic expansion. Will likely ask about Series B narrative. Trust his judgment.
- **Customer Director (RetailTech Inc)**: First-time board member. Concerned about execution risk and roadmap focus. Wants assurance on feature parity.

## Edge Cases

**First board meeting ever (no prior actions)**: Skip the prior action tracker. Focus on context and narrative coherence. Board likely wants to understand strategy and market opportunity more than metrics. Prepare a "state of the company" summary instead.

**Board member who dominates discussion**: Design agenda to give that person early, defined space (lead a customer discussion, review their area of expertise). Set time boundaries on each discussion item so other directors get airtime.

**Sensitive topic (exec performance, pivot, founder transition)**: Prepare executive session agenda separately. Brief CEO on likely questions. Have data (performance metrics, market evidence) locked down. Consider 1:1 calls with each director before the meeting.

**Virtual board meeting logistics**: Include Zoom/Google Meet link, dial-in numbers, and IT support contact in pre-read. Test screen share and speaker audio 15 min before start. If presenting metrics or slides, send static PDF version in advance for reference.

**Board observer vs. voting member distinction**: Clarify in agenda notes. Observers typically attend discussion items but not executive session. If sensitive topics arise, note who should step out and when.
