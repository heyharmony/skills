# Plan Launch Marketing

> Launch is a moment, not a campaign—plan for what comes before, during, and after.

## What It Does

This skill builds a complete go-to-market plan with tiered launch strategies (Tier 1 for new products, Tier 2 for major features, Tier 3 for updates), internal alignment checkpoints, and pre-launch/launch-day/post-launch execution playbooks. It includes templates for early adopter seeding, cross-functional launch briefs, audience-specific messaging (investor, customer, press), and Week 1-4 post-launch measurement plans. Helps you focus on Day 7 activation and Day 30 retention instead of misleading Day 1 signup metrics and vanity numbers.

## Who It's For

- **Founders and product leaders** planning their first or next product launch who need a repeatable playbook coordinating across marketing, sales, support, and product teams

- **Marketing leaders** responsible for launch execution who need to ensure internal alignment before external announcement happens

- **Sales leaders** preparing teams for launch-driven demand surges with training, talking points, and competitive positioning

- **Communications and PR leads** seeding early adopters and managing media relations for launch announcements and follow-up coverage

- **Growth teams** trying to move beyond Day 1 noise and measure what actually matters to business success (Day 7 activation, Day 30 retention)

- **Operations and project managers** coordinating across functions with clear timelines, ownership, and accountability checkpoints

## Use Cases

- **"We're 4 weeks from shipping a new product and don't know where to start."** → Use the Tier 1 playbook to build a 6-week pre-launch timeline, identify what each function owns, and coordinate internal alignment meetings.

- **"Our launch day was chaotic—sales wasn't ready, support got blindsided, messaging was inconsistent."** → Build a single launch brief that everyone reads, schedule internal training 1 week before, and assign clear roles for launch day contingencies.

- **"We're launching on Product Hunt but don't know how to maximize visibility."** → Seed early adopters 2-3 days before, designate a founder as the maker, prepare for live engagement in comments, and treat it as part of a bigger launch strategy.

- **"Day 1 we got 500 signups but nobody activated the feature by Day 7."** → Shift focus from Day 1 metrics to Day 7 activation and Day 30 retention; build post-launch email sequences and product iteration sprints based on where users drop off.

- **"We're launching in multiple time zones and coordinating is a nightmare."** → Pick one launch time zone (accept that some regions will get the announcement while sleeping), stagger follow-up communication by region, and focus on 24-hour momentum.

- **"We don't know if we should go big on this launch or keep it minimal."** → Use the tiering framework: new product = Tier 1 (6-8 weeks, full team), major feature = Tier 2 (2-4 weeks, focused effort), minor update = Tier 3 (changelog, brief email).

## Connectors

Works standalone with direct team coordination and spreadsheets, but richer with:

- **Slack** (real-time launch day coordination and momentum tracking)
- **Google Calendar** (schedule pre-launch, launch day, and post-launch milestones)
- **Gmail** (manage pre-launch email sequences and waitlist campaigns)
- **Linear or Asana** (track launch tasks with clear ownership)
- **Notion** (centralize the launch brief, timelines, and post-launch retrospective)

## Setup

Spend 3 hours mapping your launch tier, key dates (pre-launch, launch day, post-launch Week 1-4), and who owns each phase of execution. Schedule internal alignment meetings 1 week before launch. Create a one-page launch brief that sales, support, product, and marketing all read by launch day minus 1.

## What You Get

- **Tiered launch strategy** (Tier 1, 2, or 3) that matches investment and team effort to actual impact and timeline

- **6-8 week pre-launch timeline** with teaser content, beta access, waitlist, and early adopter seeding strategy

- **Launch day playbook** with simultaneous channel coordination, team roles, and hourly checkpoints for tracking momentum

- **One-page launch brief** that everyone reads (source of truth for what's launching, why, when, and how)

- **Post-launch Week 1-4 plan** with Day 7 activation and Day 30 retention measurement, not misleading Day 1 signup metrics

- **Audience-specific messaging** (investor, customer, press narratives) so different teams can adapt the story appropriately

- **Early adopter seeding strategy** to build organic momentum without relying entirely on paid media spend

## Key Insight

Most launches fail at internal alignment, not external execution. If your sales team doesn't know what's launching before customers do, if support isn't prepared with documentation, if your CEO doesn't understand why it matters, launch day will feel chaotic and rushed. Build the launch brief first, align internally second, coordinate channels third. And stop measuring success by Day 1 signups—measure Day 7 activation and Day 30 retention instead. That's the metric that tells you if the product actually matters to customers.
