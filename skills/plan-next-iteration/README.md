# Plan Next Iteration

> Transform learning into momentum. 100 reps beats 100 hours of study.

## What It Does

Plan Next Iteration takes what you shipped (and learned) and turns it into a concrete 4–8 week plan. It structures your next iteration as a **quest** (specific objective) plus a **5-rep progression** — five successive ships that build incrementally toward the quest.

Each rep is shippable on its own; each one teaches you whether you're on the right track. By rep 5, you're approaching the quest target. The output includes success metrics, blocker mitigation, and a clear calendar.

## Who It's For

- **Product managers** deciding what to build after launch.
- **Engineering leads** planning feature roadmaps and validation loops.
- **Design teams** shipping iteratively on customer feedback.
- **Growth/Operations** amplifying what worked; pivoting what didn't.
- **Startups** shipping fast; learning faster.

Anyone who ships and needs to turn feedback into a focused next move.

## Use Cases

1. **"We just shipped an MVP. What do we build next?"**
   → Turns user feedback into a 6-week quest (e.g., "increase feature adoption from 20% to 60%") with weekly ships.

2. **"Our launch had mixed adoption. How do we iterate?"**
   → Analyzes why some users loved it, some ghosted. Designs reps to fix friction + enable power users.

3. **"We're ready to scale. What's the lever?"**
   → Identifies the bottleneck (onboarding? documentation? integrations?) and builds a reps playbook to unlock scale.

4. **"Feature landed. Customer feedback is contradictory."**
   → Structures experiments (via reps) to test conflicting hypotheses in parallel and learn fast.

## Setup

1. **Kick off the analysis:** Share what you shipped (timeline, launch results, team feedback).
2. **Provide context:** Link to retro notes, customer feedback, or usage analytics.
3. **Define success:** What's the target metric for the next 8 weeks?
4. **Get a plan:** Receive quest + rep progression + calendar + blocker mitigation.
5. **Execute:** Pick rep 1, ship this week, measure, iterate.

## What You Get

- **Quest definition** (clear 4–8 week objective with metrics).
- **Rep 1–5 progression table** (what ships each week, why, owner, success metric).
- **Before/after metrics** (baseline state, target state, measurement method).
- **Blocker mitigation** (dependencies called out with owners).
- **Team calendar** (kickoff, mid-cycle sync, ship dates, retro).
- **Next-cycle hypothesis** (what you'll learn; what quest comes after).

**Turnaround:** 1–2 hours to analyze + deliver plan. 8 weeks to execute.
