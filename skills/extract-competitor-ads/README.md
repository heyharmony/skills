# Extract Competitor Ads

> **Discover competitive positioning and creative strategies by analyzing ads from Meta, Google, and LinkedIn — surface messaging patterns, spend signals, and whitespace opportunities.**

## What It Does

This skill systematically pulls ads from public ad libraries (Meta Ad Library, Google Ads Transparency Center, LinkedIn Ads) and analyzes them for competitive intelligence. It catalogs messaging themes, creative strategies, call-to-action patterns, and estimated spend to identify competitive threats and positioning gaps.

Output: A structured competitive ad intelligence report with theme benchmarking, creative analysis, spend signals, and strategic recommendations for positioning, messaging, and campaign planning.

## Who It's For

- **Marketing leaders** planning brand positioning and messaging strategy
- **Product marketers** benchmarking competitive claims and differentiation
- **Sales enablement** teams arming reps with competitive intelligence
- **Growth teams** identifying creative tactics and budget allocation trends
- **Brand strategists** conducting quarterly competitive analysis

## Use Cases

- **Quarterly competitive benchmarking**: Track how competitor messaging evolves across Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4
- **Pre-launch positioning**: Before launching a new feature or campaign, research what competitors are claiming
- **Sales enablement**: Create one-pagers showing competitive positioning gaps for sales teams
- **Budget planning**: Use spend signals to estimate competitor investment in specific markets or campaigns
- **Creative ideation**: Identify emerging creative trends (video, animation, customer testimonials) used by competitors
- **Whitespace positioning**: Discover messaging themes competitors are NOT using — your differentiation opportunity

## Connectors

- **HubSpot**: Pull competitor mentions from deal notes, contact records, and sales activity to trigger research
- **Slack**: Surface competitive intel shared in marketing and sales channels; notify team of findings
- **Google Drive**: Store ad screenshots, analysis spreadsheets, and competitive benchmarking reports
- **Notion**: Update competitive intelligence wiki pages with latest ad findings and messaging themes

## Setup

Install and go. For best results, have 3–5 competitor names ready and decide on your research scope — which platforms (Meta, Google, LinkedIn), date range (30/60/90 days), and geographic regions to cover. The skill works from public ad library data, so no special access or API keys are needed. Set a monthly or quarterly cadence for ongoing competitive monitoring.

## What You Get

- **Executive summary** of key competitive themes and strategic implications
- **Competitor profiles** with ad volume, spend levels, messaging focus, and creative approaches
- **Messaging benchmark table** showing theme frequency across competitors
- **Creative strategy analysis** covering visual approaches, copy tone, and CTA strategies
- **Spend signal insights** on budget allocation patterns and market priorities
- **Positioning gaps & opportunities** identifying whitespace and differentiation chances
- **Actionable recommendations** for messaging, creative testing, and platform allocation
- **Shareable report** formatted for sales enablement, strategy workshops, and stakeholder reviews

## Common Workflows

- **Monthly competitive pulse**: Run on the first of each month to track messaging shifts and new campaigns across your top 3 competitors
- **Pre-campaign research**: Before launching your own ads, scan competitor creative to avoid overlap and find whitespace
- **Sales enablement updates**: Generate quarterly battlecard updates showing how competitors position themselves in paid media vs. their website claims
- **Board prep**: Include competitive ad spend estimates and positioning analysis in quarterly board materials
