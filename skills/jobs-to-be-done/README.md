# Jobs-to-be-Done Analyzer

> Stop selling features. Understand the job your customers are actually trying to do — and build products they can't live without.

## What It Does

Uncovers the functional, emotional, and social jobs customers are trying to accomplish — not just the product features they're buying. Uses customer conversations from Harmony to extract verbatim language about what they're trying to achieve, how they want to feel, and how they want to be perceived. Builds job stories and personas grounded in real customer motivation, so product, marketing, and sales teams align around what actually drives purchase and use. Works without conversation data by asking diagnostic questions about current solutions and friction points.

## Who It's For

- **Product managers** building features that customers actually want to use (not just install)
- **Marketing leaders** crafting messaging that resonates because it speaks to emotional and social jobs, not just features
- **Sales teams** winning deals by leading with the customer's real motivation, not a feature list
- **Strategic leaders** understanding why some customers adopt and others churn
- **UX/design teams** prioritizing improvements based on what actually enables the job

## Use Cases

- New product planning; need to understand the job before shipping → Functional + emotional + social job analysis from customer interviews
- Customer churn analysis; users say "great product" but don't use it → Likely solving the wrong job. JTBD analysis reveals the real blocker.
- Go-to-market repositioning; competitor wins even though feature parity is equal → Different job positioning. Rebuild messaging around jobs.
- Sales enablement; reps struggle even with great collateral → Reps are selling features; customers care about jobs. Rebuild sales training.

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with these integrations:

- **Salesforce / HubSpot** — Pulls win/loss notes, lost deal feedback, and opportunity notes to reveal which jobs drove purchase decisions (or prevented them)
- **Gmail** — Surfaces customer email threads discussing challenges, workarounds, and what matters to them
- **Slack** — Pulls support conversations and customer feedback to identify patterns in the jobs customers are trying to accomplish
- **Google Drive** — References interview transcripts and research documents that already contain job language

## Setup

Install and go. Works best when customer conversations live in Harmony — the skill pulls exact quotes showing the job, emotional context, and social motivation. Without Harmony data, provide customer interviews, support tickets, or sales call recordings, and the skill will extract jobs from those.

## What You Get

Complete JTBD analysis including: primary job (functional, emotional, social) expressed as job stories, job moments (before, during, after), JTBD-based personas (not demographic personas), competitive positioning from a jobs lens, opportunities for product/marketing/sales teams, and assumptions to validate before major decisions.
