# Campaign Debrief

> The difference between a good campaign and a great one isn't the numbers — it's what you learn from them.

## What It Does

Campaign Debrief transforms scattered post-campaign data into a structured, honest analysis that reveals what actually worked, what didn't, and exactly why. It goes beyond dashboards and metric reports to synthesize campaign objectives, actual results, qualitative feedback from customers and teams, and root-cause analysis into a clear, actionable narrative.

The skill pulls together:
- **Quantitative results**: Performance against targets by channel and metric
- **Qualitative signals**: Sales feedback, customer response, team observations, market context
- **Channel analysis**: Which channels drove engagement, conversions, and ROI—and which didn't
- **Root-cause insights**: Why metrics hit or missed (was it reach, resonance, or conversion?)
- **Repeat/Stop/Start framework**: What to double down on, what to kill, what to test next
- **Campaign health score**: An honest 1–5 rating backed by data and reasoning
- **Prioritized recommendations**: 3–5 specific, concrete steps for the next campaign iteration

The output is structured, markdown-formatted analysis ready to share with leadership, sales, product, or clients—or use as input for your next campaign planning.

## Who It's For

**Marketing managers and teams** who need to debrief campaigns quickly and extract learnings before the moment fades.

**Marketing directors and leaders** who want honest insights, not defensive narratives, to inform next-quarter strategy.

**Product and sales leaders** who need to understand what marketing campaigns actually delivered and why, to align roadmap and outreach.

**Agency partners and consultants** running campaigns for clients and needing to deliver professional, data-backed debrief reports.

**Growth leaders and founders** managing tightly-budgeted campaigns and needing to maximize ROI by learning fast and iterating.

## Use Cases

### Post-Campaign Analysis
You ran a 30-day product launch or promotional campaign. It's done. You have metrics, feedback from sales, some customer comments. Now what? Campaign Debrief turns that pile of data into a clear story: what worked, why, what didn't, and what to do next time. Especially useful when campaigns are multi-channel (email, paid ads, webinars, events, PR) and you need to compare performance across channels.

### Performance Troubleshooting
Your campaign missed targets. Sales says the leads were low quality. Marketing says the targeting was off. The CEO wants to know why the ROI sucked. Campaign Debrief helps isolate root causes: Was it a reach problem (too few people saw it)? A resonance problem (they saw it but didn't care)? A conversion problem (they engaged but didn't convert)? Or external factors (market timing, competition, budget constraints)? Understanding the *why* tells you what to fix.

### Multi-Campaign Comparison
You ran three campaigns in parallel targeting different segments or using different channels. Which one worked best, and why? Campaign Debrief structures the analysis so you can compare apples to apples and identify patterns—maybe video always outperforms text, or maybe one audience segment is more responsive. Use these learnings to allocate budget more smartly next time.

### Team Alignment and Retrospectives
Marketing, sales, product, and leadership often have different takes on what happened. ("Those leads were garbage." "No, your sales process was too slow." "Actually, we should've launched in March, not February.") Campaign Debrief synthesizes diverse perspectives into a single, data-backed narrative. It legitimizes feedback, surfaces disagreements, and builds consensus on next steps.

### Shareable Stakeholder Reports
Leadership wants to know ROI. Clients want to see what their budget did. Sales wants credit for their contribution. Campaign Debrief produces a professional, structured report with clear results, reasoning, and recommendations—ready to present to executives or clients without defensive explanations.

### Learning Loop for Next Campaign
The Repeat/Stop/Start framework and prioritized recommendations directly feed into your next campaign planning. No need to re-interview people or re-analyze data. You have a clear action list ready to go.

### Budget Justification
Marketing budget under pressure? Campaign Debrief shows which channels and tactics are actually working and worth investing in, and which are sinks. Helps you defend why you're reallocating spend or asking for more.

## Setup

No setup required. Campaign Debrief works with:
- Campaign metadata (name, dates, channels, budget, goals)
- Quantitative data from any source (Google Analytics, marketing platform, CRM, custom spreadsheets)
- Qualitative feedback (sales team input, customer feedback, team retrospective observations, market context)

If you have Harmony conversations with your sales team, team meetings, or customer feedback sessions during or after the campaign, Harmony can pull relevant signals from those conversations to enrich the debrief. But the skill works equally well with direct input—you don't need Harmony conversation data to use it.

**Best practices**:
- Run debrief within 1–2 weeks of campaign end, while details are fresh
- Gather sales and team input while feedback is still warm
- Have access to marketing platform data (traffic, conversions, spend) before starting
- If multi-channel, have channel-level data if possible (not just aggregate)

## What You Get

A structured, production-quality campaign debrief including:

1. **Results at a Glance**: Table of key metrics vs. targets, showing hit/miss status immediately

2. **Campaign Health Score**: 1–5 rating with clear rationale, so leadership knows instantly whether this was a win or needs investigation

3. **What Worked / What Didn't**: For each channel or tactic, specific evidence (metric, feedback, or observation), root-cause analysis (the *why*), and what it means for future campaigns

4. **Channel Performance Breakdown**: Comparative table showing reach, engagement rate, conversions, and cost-per-conversion for each channel—easy to see which outperformed

5. **Qualitative Signals**: Structured summary of what sales, customers, and teams actually said—bringing human context to the numbers

6. **Repeat / Stop / Start Framework**: Clear, prioritized guidance on what to double down, kill, and experiment with next

7. **Prioritized Recommendations**: 3–5 specific, concrete actions for the next campaign, with rationale and estimated impact/effort for each

8. **Data Gaps & Caveats**: Honest acknowledgment of what you don't know, so leadership doesn't over-interpret results

The output is markdown-formatted, ready to share as a document, email, or slide deck—and structured to feed directly into next-quarter campaign planning.
