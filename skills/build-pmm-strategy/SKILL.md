## Purpose

Product marketing managers live at the intersection of product, sales, and marketing — and the best ones know that their strategy must be grounded in customer and prospect voice, not internal assumptions. This skill mines Harmony transcripts (discovery calls, objection patterns, win/loss interviews, product feedback sessions) to extract the exact language customers use to describe their problems, your product, and competitive alternatives. From those insights, it builds a coherent positioning framework, messaging hierarchy, competitive narratives, and sales enablement materials that sales teams actually use and trust.

## When to Use

- **New product positioning**: Launching a new product or major feature and need to define how it fits in the market
- **Competitive response**: Responding to a new competitor or market entrant with differentiated messaging
- **Sales struggling with messaging**: Deal velocity is slow, and reps are confusing prospects with inconsistent value propositions
- **Analyst briefing prep**: Preparing to brief analysts or press and need a clear, defensible narrative
- **Quarterly messaging refresh**: Quarterly business reviews or planning cycles require updated positioning based on recent customer feedback
- **Product-market fit validation**: Validating that your core value proposition resonates by testing it against customer objections and win/loss data

## Instructions

**Step 1: Mine Customer Voice**
Review Harmony transcripts from discovery calls, product feedback sessions, and win/loss interviews. Extract:
- Exact language customers use to describe their problem (not your solution)
- How prospects describe competing solutions or status quo
- Objections and concerns that appear repeatedly
- Words and phrases that signal resonance (customers repeat them back unprompted)
- Proof points and success metrics customers mention

**Step 2: Build Positioning Canvas**
From customer voice, define:
- **Target Segment**: Who are we solving for? (job title, company size, use case, decision driver)
- **Category**: How do customers naturally bucket what you do?
- **Key Differentiator**: What is the one thing about us that matters most to this segment?
- **Proof Points**: Customer evidence (names, metrics, outcomes) that supports the differentiator

**Step 3: Create Messaging Hierarchy**
Build a three-tier structure:
- **Headline**: One sentence that captures the core value (customer problem → unique solution)
- **Value Pillars** (3–4): The main reasons to believe, each grounded in customer evidence
- **Proof Points & Handlers**: Stories, metrics, customer quotes, and objection handlers for each pillar

**Step 4: Map Competitive Narrative**
For each major competitor:
- How do customers and prospects describe them?
- What are they good at (and what do we cede)?
- What is our strongest angle against them?
- What messaging positions us as the clear alternative?

**Step 5: Design Sales Enablement Assets**
Create materials sales teams will actually use:
- **One-pagers**: Problem → solution → proof for each segment
- **Battlecards**: Competitor comparisons, objection handlers, win strategies
- **Email templates**: Discovery, objection response, follow-up hooks
- **Talk tracks**: How to open a call, handle discovery questions, close on next steps

**Step 6: Define Personas with Conversation Evidence**
For each persona (e.g., VP of Sales, Marketing Operations Manager):
- Job title, company size, key challenges
- How they talk about the problem
- What success looks like to them
- Most effective proof points and objections
- All grounded in Harmony transcripts, not guesswork

**Step 7: Build Analyst & Press Narrative**
Create a concise brief (500 words) for analysts and reporters:
- Market context and trend
- Why your solution matters now
- Your positioning and vision
- Key differentiators
- Customer validation

**Step 8: Set Measurement Framework**
Define how you'll know if the messaging works:
- Win rate by messaging version or segment
- Sales rep message pull-through (are they using your talking points?)
- Customer quote frequency in RFPs and reviews
- Time to first value conversation
- Competitive win/loss ratio by message theme

## Output Format

- **Positioning Canvas**: One-page summary of segment, category, differentiator, and proof
- **Messaging House**: Headline, value pillars, proof points, and objection handlers
- **Competitive Messaging Matrix**: Us vs. each competitor, messaging angles, key wins
- **Persona Cards**: Title, challenges, success metrics, key proof points (one per persona)
- **Sales Enablement Brief**: One-pagers, battlecards, email templates, talk tracks
- **Analyst/Press Narrative**: 500-word brief for external stakeholders
- **Measurement Framework**: KPIs, win rate tracking, message pull-through metrics

## Example: Driftwood (Customer Data Platform for E-Commerce)

### Transcript Excerpts (Harmony)

**Discovery Call with VP of Marketing, Fashion Retailer (Nov 2025)**
> "We're losing customers because we don't know who they are after the first purchase. We email everyone the same thing. Tools like Segment cost us $50K/year and we still have to hire someone to use them. We need to know: who's likely to buy again, who's churning, what discount brings them back?"

**Win/Loss Interview: Lost Deal to Competitor (Oct 2025)**
> "We looked at Driftwood, but [competitor] felt like it was built for us. They showed us how [competitor] did cohort analysis right in the platform. Driftwood felt like we'd need engineering for a month to set up."

**Product Feedback Session: Customer Success, D2C Brand (Dec 2025)**
> "Driftwood saved us 6 hours a week. Before, we manually exported CSV files and merged them in Excel. Now we just... click. The retention cohort view — that's gold. We've actually fired Tableau because of it."

### Positioning Canvas

| Element | Definition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Target Segment** | E-commerce brands ($2M–$100M ARR) who rely on repeat purchases and lack clear visibility into customer behavior without hiring a data analyst |
| **Category** | Customer data platform (CDP) for e-commerce, not a generic analytics tool |
| **Key Differentiator** | Pre-built e-commerce schemas + no-code cohort analysis → insights in minutes, not months |
| **Proof Points** | 47% increase in repeat purchase rate; 6 hours/week saved per analyst; 3-week onboarding vs. 12 weeks with competitors |

### Messaging House

**Headline**
"Know your repeat customers. Act on it in minutes."

**Value Pillar 1: Time to Insight (No Engineering Required)**
- Proof: "Built-in cohort analysis for e-commerce. No SQL, no data scientist."
- Objection Handler: "But we don't have a data team. Will we need to hire one?" → "No. Our templates handle 80% of e-commerce use cases out of the box."

**Value Pillar 2: Higher Repeat Purchase Rates**
- Proof: "Segment customers by behavior in real time. Brands see 40%+ lift in repeat rate."
- Objection Handler: "How do we know this will work for us?" → "See how [customer] went from 12% repeat rate to 18% in 6 weeks."

**Value Pillar 3: Cost Savings**
- Proof: "Replace Segment + manual analysis + Excel with one platform. Median savings: $45K/year."
- Objection Handler: "Isn't this expensive?" → "Driftwood is 40% cheaper than Segment + the analyst time you're already paying for."

### Competitive Messaging Matrix

| Competitor | Their Angle | Our Angle | Our Win |
|---|---|---|---|
| Segment | Generic CDP for any data | Built for e-commerce patterns | No engineering needed, faster insights |
| Shopify Analytics | Free, but limited | Deep cohort + behavior analysis | Own your data, no lock-in |
| Tableau | Powerful but slow to implement | Fast, pre-built for e-commerce | 3-week vs. 12-week setup |

### Persona Card: VP of Marketing (D2C Brand)

**Role**: VP of Marketing, $10M ARR e-commerce brand
**Top Challenges**: Low repeat purchase rate, high CAC, attribution across channels
**Success Metric**: 15%+ increase in repeat rate within 6 months
**Most Effective Proof Point**: "We went from 12% to 18% repeat rate in 6 weeks. Here's how." (Case study)
**Key Objection**: "Isn't this just a fancy analytics tool?" → "No, it's built so you can activate these insights in your email platform immediately."

## Edge Cases

1. **Pre-product-market-fit** (limited customer data): Use interviews with early users, founder calls with prospects, and feature requests from sign-ups. Positioning will be narrower; refresh it monthly as you gather more evidence.

2. **Crowded category with no clear differentiator**: Audit competitive positioning carefully. Focus on a specific use case or persona where you have a real advantage, even if narrow. "Best-in-class for e-commerce" beats "general CDP for everyone."

3. **Pivoting to new market**: Don't assume your old positioning works. Run 10 discovery calls in the new market. You may find customers describe their problem (and your solution) completely differently.

4. **Founder-driven messaging that needs professionalization**: Preserve the founder's core insight and voice; reframe it in customer language. Ask: "What does this mean to the customer?" Translate "AI-powered" to "gets insights 10x faster."

5. **International messaging adaptation**: Don't just translate. Run discovery calls in each market. A pain point in the US may not resonate in APAC. Competitive landscape differs. Refresh positioning per region.
