# Apply Marketing Psychology

## Overview

Understand why people buy and apply proven psychological principles to your marketing strategy. This skill maps buyer behavior from real conversations to cognitive frameworks—helping you diagnose conversion bottlenecks and design persuasive experiences.

## What This Skill Does

- **Diagnoses buyer hesitation**: Why do prospects stall, object to price, or choose competitors? Surface the underlying psychology.
- **Surfaces decision-making language**: Harmony extracts language patterns from calls, emails, and Slack to reveal which psychological triggers are active in your market.
- **Maps to proven frameworks**: Connects your bottleneck to specific biases and mental models (loss aversion, anchoring, scarcity, authority, network effects, etc.).
- **Designs targeted interventions**: Proposes minimal changes to messaging, pricing, flow, or social proof that leverage the identified psychology.
- **Measures impact**: Guides a/b testing and outcome tracking to validate that psychology change works in your context.

## When to Reach For This Skill

- Conversion stalls (prospects understand value, still hesitate)
- Price objections or "too expensive" pattern
- Trial signup or low-commitment conversion gaps
- Team debates about messaging—resolve with psychology + data
- Win/loss asymmetry (competitor wins despite worse product)
- Email engagement declining (open rates, clicks)
- Scaling a high-touch sale into self-serve

## How It Works

1. You describe the bottleneck (e.g., "8% trial-to-paid conversion").
2. The skill extracts decision-making language from your Harmony data (calls, emails, Slack).
3. It diagnoses the root psychology (e.g., loss aversion + switching-cost fear).
4. It proposes a targeted intervention grounded in a specific psychology framework.
5. It guides measurement and learning so you can validate and scale what works.

## Key Frameworks

- **Thinking models**: First Principles, JTBD (Jobs to Be Done), Theory of Constraints
- **Buyer psychology**: Confirmation Bias, Sunk Cost, Loss Aversion
- **Persuasion**: Reciprocity, Authority, Scarcity
- **Pricing psychology**: Charm Pricing, Anchoring, Price Relativity
- **Design/scaling**: Nudge Theory, Network Effects, Compounding

## Example Use Case

Bloomreach (e-commerce personalization) had strong trial signup but low trial-to-paid conversion. By analyzing call language, they discovered loss aversion (fear of complexity) was the real barrier—not price. They added expert setup availability, timing social proof, and price anchoring to benchmarks. Trial-to-paid conversion jumped from 8% to 11.2%.
