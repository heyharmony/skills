# Document Co-Author

> Turn meeting discussions into polished, audience-ready documents in three deliberate stages.

## What It Does

Document Co-Author guides you through structured collaboration to transform raw meeting notes, transcripts, and brainstorms into documents that land with your audience. It starts by understanding your document's purpose and audience, identifies gaps in context, then walks you through section-by-section drafting with iterative refinement—finishing with a comprehension test to catch clarity gaps before publish.

The skill pairs well with meeting transcripts and Harmony's conversation data, but works just as well with brainstorming sessions or existing notes. You end with a document that's clear, well-organized, and audience-appropriate.

## Who It's For

- **Product Managers:** Turning product discussions into proposals, roadmaps, and specs that secure buy-in from executives and engineering
- **Engineering Leads:** Creating architecture documents and technical RFCs from team discussions and design conversations
- **Internal Communicators:** Drafting status updates, newsletters, and announcements from leadership and team conversations
- **Sales Engineers:** Converting technical discussions into customer-ready case studies and solution documents
- **Project Managers:** Turning kickoff conversations into project charters, timelines, and stakeholder briefs
- **Operations Teams:** Creating process documentation and runbooks from operational discussions and lessons learned

## Use Cases

- **Product Proposal:** Turn a 45-minute executive brainstorm about a new feature into a compelling 3-page business case with budget, timeline, and success metrics.
- **Technical Deep Dive:** Synthesize three engineering discussions into a detailed architecture document that helps new team members onboard and future auditors understand design trade-offs.
- **Status Update:** Convert weekly leadership sync notes into a crisp "Progress/Plans/Problems" update that keeps stakeholders informed without requiring them to read raw notes.
- **Incident Postmortem:** Transform a chaotic incident call into a structured postmortem that identifies root causes, captures lessons, and drives action items.
- **Customer Case Study:** Build a narrative around a customer conversation and project success story, framed for external audience and sales enablement.
- **Strategy Document:** Consolidate multiple strategy discussions and perspectives into a cohesive vision statement and roadmap that aligns teams and prioritizes work.

## Setup

Install the skill. When you're ready to turn a discussion into a document:
1. Gather your source material (meeting transcripts, notes, or open a voice memo)
2. Trigger the skill with phrases like *"write a document about this"* or *"turn this into a proposal"*
3. Walk through the intake questions—the skill will guide you
4. Let it pull context from Harmony conversation data if available
5. Follow the section-by-section refinement and testing steps
6. End with a polished, ready-to-share document

## What You Get

A document that's clearly structured, audience-appropriate, and backed by real conversations and data. You'll have a full markdown template you can copy into your tool of choice, formatted with headers, FAQs, and next-step clarity. The skill also suggests what to do next with the document—whether that's sharing it via email, adding it to your wiki, syncing details into your CRM, or creating a Slack announcement.
