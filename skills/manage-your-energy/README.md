# Manage Your Energy

> Energy management is more fundamental than time management. You can't manage time, but you can manage what you do with the energy you have. Burnout isn't from working too much; it's from recovering too little.

## What It Does

This skill teaches you to audit your energy (where does it come from and where does it go?), understand the four sources of energy (physical, emotional, mental, spiritual), identify what activities drain you versus energize you, and restructure your work and life to recover more energy than you spend. You can feel exhausted at 4pm and restored by 6pm if you have the right recovery activity. The goal isn't more productivity—it's sustainable productivity where you feel capable and alive, not burned out and depleted. You'll learn that energy management is more powerful than time management: with proper recovery, you can accomplish more in 40 focused hours than in 60 fragmented ones, because your brain and body are actually resourced.

## Who It's For

- **Anyone experiencing burnout**: Exhaustion is the signal that your energy is depleted faster than it recovers. Time management won't fix it.
- **People who've optimized time but still feel tired**: You've cut meetings and protected deep work, but you're still running on fumes. Energy management is the next lever.
- **Leaders trying to sustain high performance**: Sprints are fine; long-term high performance requires energy recovery.
- **Managers watching their teams burn out**: Teach them energy management, not just time management. It changes how people see sustainable work.
- **People who want better work quality, not more output**: More energy per hour is more efficient than more hours. You'll ship better code, make better decisions, and be present with your team.
- **Anyone struggling with sleep, exercise, or personal relationships**: These are often symptoms of depleted energy, not laziness
- **High-achievers at risk of long-term depletion**: You've crushed sprint goals. Now learn how to maintain that intensity for years, not months

## Use Cases

- **You're exhausted by Thursday and non-functional by Friday** → Audit your red (draining) activities, add green (energizing) recovery, and protect sleep
- **Sleep is suffering and you can't exercise** → Start with sleep (it unlocks everything). Add movement as soon as sleep improves.
- **Certain people or meetings drain you faster than others** → Restructure: do those activities when you have energy buffer, follow them with recovery
- **You've lost sense of why your work matters** → Spiritual energy (alignment with values) is depleted. Reconnect with meaning or consider role change
- **Good meetings and bad relationships are tanking your emotional energy** → Resolve conflicts and clarify expectations. Then set boundaries to protect from future drains
- **You have energy for work but none left for relationships or personal life** → You're optimizing for the wrong things. Rebalance energy sources so work doesn't consume everything
- **Team burnout is rising even though workload seems reasonable** → Team members might be running on different energy deficits. Teach energy management, not just productivity

## Connectors

Works standalone, but richer with:
- **Google Calendar**: Map when you have green, yellow, and red activities. Restructure to do more green before red activities.
- **Notion**: Track your energy audit, recovery activities, and patterns. Document which work energizes vs drains you.
- **Apple Health**: Monitor sleep and movement data. Sleep is the foundation of all other energy sources.
- **Gmail**: Identify which relationships and communication patterns drain emotional energy. Set boundaries.
- **Linear or task management**: Track which types of work energize vs drain you, so you can design your role intentionally

## Setup

1. List all major activities (work, meetings, 1-on-1s, exercise, sleep, family, hobbies, commute). Don't forget work transitions and admin time.
2. Rate each: green (energizing), yellow (neutral), or red (draining). Be specific about which meetings drain you—that matters.
3. Count the proportion: what % of your time is green vs red? If red > green, you're in deficit. You will burn out.
4. Assess the four energy sources: which is most depleted? Physical? Emotional? Mental? Spiritual? This tells you where to focus recovery.
5. Identify one green recovery activity and schedule it daily. For most people, it's sleep, exercise, time with people they love, or alignment with mission.
6. Fix the foundation first: sleep, then exercise, then everything else follows. You can't recover emotional energy if you're physically depleted.

## What You Get

- **Sustainable high performance**: You can work hard, but you recover so you do it again tomorrow, not just this week. Your pace compounds instead of depletes.
- **Better work quality**: When you're rested and energized, your thinking is clearer and your decisions are better. Your output quality improves dramatically.
- **Less emotional reactivity**: Physical and emotional energy restored means you respond instead of react. You're more patient with your team and yourself.
- **Reclaimed joy in work**: When spiritual energy (alignment with values) is restored, work feels like purpose again, not just survival. You remember why you care.
- **Presence and connection**: When you're not running on empty, you're actually available to people you care about. Your relationships strengthen.
- **Immunity to burnout**: You know which activities drain you and which restore you, so you can prevent depletion instead of just recovering from it. Burnout becomes avoidable.
- **An energy audit scorecard**: Track your green/yellow/red activities and recovery patterns so you can see progress and course-correct as life shifts

## Key Insight

Burnout isn't a character flaw; it's an energy math problem. If you spend more energy than you recover, you will burn out, no matter how smart or disciplined you are. Start with sleep—it's the foundation of physical, emotional, and mental energy. Everything else builds from there. You can't exercise your way out of sleep deprivation, and you can't meditate your way to spiritual alignment when you're exhausted.

## Pro Tips

- Sleep is non-negotiable. It's not laziness; it's the fuel for everything else. Protect it like you protect your most important meeting.
- Emotional energy depletes fastest when relationships are broken. Resolve conflicts and clarify expectations. They're not distractions; they're energy emergencies.
- Spiritual energy (alignment with values) is where long-term sustainability lives. If your work doesn't align with your values, no amount of vacation will restore you.
