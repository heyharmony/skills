# Conversation Research Brief

> A question comes up in a meeting — get a structured briefing with findings, sources, and next steps before the conversation is even over.

## What It Does

Picks up topics, questions, and knowledge gaps from Harmony conversations and turns them into concise research briefings. Findings are tied back to the original conversation context — your team knows not just what was found, but why it matters to the discussion at hand.

## Who It's For

Product managers, strategists, and team leads who need fast answers to questions that come up mid-conversation — without derailing the meeting to "Google it."

## Use Cases

- Prospect mentions a competitor on a sales call → competitive brief with positioning, pricing, and differentiation delivered to the account team.
- CTO raises a technology question in a strategy meeting → research briefing with ecosystem status, adoption data, and a recommendation, linked to the original discussion.
- Compliance concern surfaces during a client onboarding call → quick regulatory overview with relevance to the specific situation discussed.

## Setup

Install and go. For richer results, ensure Harmony has access to your workspace Notes and previous conversation history so the skill can cross-reference prior research.

## What You Get

A Markdown research briefing with: background, numbered key findings with sources, a section connecting findings to your specific conversation context, and recommended next steps. Structured for Slack, Harmony Notes, or feeding into other skills.
