# Align Strategic Priorities

Detect strategic misalignment across teams by analyzing what teams actually discuss and commit to in their meetings against stated company priorities.

## Overview

Strategy execution happens at the seams between teams. When the CEO says "we're going upmarket" but marketing is still targeting startups, that's a gap. This skill mines team meetings across your organization to surface where execution diverges from strategy—before it shows up in quarterly results.

## Use Cases

- **Quarterly alignment reviews**: Validate that team execution matches strategic OKRs
- **Board meeting prep**: Verify your narrative about strategy adoption is grounded in reality
- **Strategic pivots**: Measure how quickly a new priority cascades through the org
- **Execution gaps**: Diagnose why teams are busy but results lag
- **Cross-functional planning**: Identify broken assumptions and dependency gaps

## What It Does

The skill:
1. Extracts stated priorities from OKRs, board decks, or leadership meetings
2. Analyzes meeting transcripts from across your org (Engineering, Sales, Marketing, Product, CS)
3. Maps team activity against stated priorities: Which teams allocate time and focus to each priority?
4. Detects drift signals: Topics consuming meeting time that aren't tied to strategy
5. Scores alignment per team and identifies cross-team dependency gaps
6. Recommends realignment (change behavior) or redefinition (adjust strategy)

## Inputs

- **Stated priorities**: OKRs, board deck, strategy document, or leadership meeting transcript
- **Team meetings**: Standups, planning sessions, retrospectives, syncs from past 2–4 weeks
- **Source**: Slack transcripts, Notion meeting notes, Linear updates, Jira roadmap, Google Drive strategy docs

## Outputs

- **Alignment heat map**: Priorities × Teams matrix showing alignment level and time spent
- **Team alignment scores**: Strong / Partial / Weak / Misaligned, with evidence
- **Drift signal report**: Off-strategy topics consuming time, with quotes from meetings
- **Dependency gap analysis**: Assumed handoffs that aren't happening
- **Recommendations**: Realign or redefine, with concrete next steps

## Best Practices

- Run quarterly, aligned with OKR review cycles
- Include meetings from all major functions (don't skip Sales or Marketing)
- Use 2–4 weeks of recent meetings for the freshest signal
- Share results in leadership sync; expect productive debate about whether misalignment reflects execution gaps or flawed strategy
- Follow up with team leads to validate findings and co-create action plans
