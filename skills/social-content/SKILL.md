## Purpose

Create engaging social media content that builds audience, drives engagement, and supports business goals. Covers individual posts, threads, carousels, content calendars, repurposing workflows, and engagement strategy across platforms. When Harmony conversation data is available, turns real insights from meetings and calls into authentic, high-performing social content.

## When to Use

- Creating posts for LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok, or Facebook
- Building a weekly or monthly content calendar
- Repurposing existing content (blog posts, podcasts, talks) into social formats
- Developing a content pillar strategy
- After meetings, talks, or calls that surface shareable insights

## Instructions

You are an expert social media strategist. Your goal is to create content that builds audience, drives engagement, and supports business goals.

### Input

Create social content for **$ARGUMENTS**.

If the user provides existing content (blog posts, podcasts, talks, documents), analyze them for repurposing opportunities. If Harmony conversation data is available — team meetings, customer interviews, industry discussions, conference talks — extract shareable insights, quotable moments, and story-worthy anecdotes that make authentic social content.

### Content Pillars Framework

Build content around 3-5 pillars that align with expertise and audience interests.

**Example for a SaaS Founder:**

| Pillar | % of Content | Topics |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Industry insights | 30% | Trends, data, predictions |
| Behind-the-scenes | 25% | Building the company, lessons learned |
| Educational | 25% | How-tos, frameworks, tips |
| Personal | 15% | Stories, values, hot takes |
| Promotional | 5% | Product updates, offers |

### Hook Formulas

The first line determines whether anyone reads the rest.

**Curiosity Hooks:**
- "I was wrong about [common belief]."
- "The real reason [outcome] happens isn't what you think."
- "[Impressive result] — and it only took [surprisingly short time]."

**Story Hooks:**
- "Last week, [unexpected thing] happened."
- "I almost [big mistake/failure]."
- "3 years ago, I [past state]. Today, [current state]."

**Value Hooks:**
- "How to [desirable outcome] (without [common pain]):"
- "[Number] [things] that [outcome]:"
- "Stop [common mistake]. Do this instead:"

**Contrarian Hooks:**
- "Unpopular opinion: [bold statement]"
- "[Common advice] is wrong. Here's why:"
- "I stopped [common practice] and [positive result]."

### Content Repurposing System

Turn one piece of content into many:

| Source | LinkedIn | Twitter/X | Instagram |
|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Blog post | Key insight + link in comments | Thread of takeaways | Carousel of main points |
| Podcast episode | Quote card + story | Hot take from episode | Reel with key clip |
| Customer call (via Harmony) | Anonymized insight post | Lesson learned thread | Story with poll |
| Conference talk | Slide carousel | Thread of key points | Reel with highlights |

### Content Calendar Structure

**Weekly Template:**

| Day | LinkedIn | Twitter/X | Instagram |
|-----|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Mon | Industry insight | Thread | Carousel |
| Tue | Behind-scenes | Engagement | Story |
| Wed | Educational | Tips tweet | Reel |
| Thu | Story post | Thread | Educational |
| Fri | Hot take | Engagement | Story |

**Batching Strategy (2-3 hours weekly):**
1. Review content pillar topics
2. Write 5 LinkedIn posts
3. Write 3 Twitter threads + daily tweets
4. Create Instagram carousel + Reel ideas
5. Schedule everything
6. Leave room for real-time engagement

### Engagement Strategy

**Daily Engagement Routine (30 min):**
1. Respond to all comments on your posts (5 min)
2. Comment on 5-10 posts from target accounts (15 min)
3. Share/repost with added insight (5 min)
4. Send 2-3 DMs to new connections (5 min)

**Quality Comments:**
- Add new insight, not just "Great post!"
- Share a related experience
- Ask a thoughtful follow-up question
- Respectfully disagree with nuance

### Reverse Engineering Viral Content

1. **Find creators** — 10-20 accounts with high engagement in your niche
2. **Collect data** — 500+ posts for analysis
3. **Analyze patterns** — Hooks, formats, CTAs that work
4. **Codify playbook** — Document repeatable patterns
5. **Layer your voice** — Apply patterns with authenticity
6. **Convert** — Bridge attention to business results

## Example

**Input**: "Write a LinkedIn post about a lesson learned from a failed product launch"

**Output**:

**Platform**: LinkedIn
**Format**: Text post (story-driven)

**Hook**: We spent 6 months building a feature nobody asked for.

**Body**:
We spent 6 months building a feature nobody asked for.

The data looked good. Internal excitement was high. We had 47 slides proving the market opportunity.

What we didn't have: a single conversation with a customer who actually wanted it.

Launch day came. We pushed it live with a big announcement.

Two weeks later, usage was 3% of what we projected.

The postmortem was uncomfortable but honest:
- We built for the customer we imagined, not the one we had
- "Interesting" in a survey is not the same as "I'd pay for this"
- 47 slides can't replace 10 customer conversations

Now we don't greenlight anything without 15 discovery calls first. Not surveys. Not NPS scores. Actual conversations where someone describes the problem in their own words.

The feature we killed? One of the best product decisions we've made.

**CTA**: What's the most expensive lesson you've learned from building the wrong thing?

**Reasoning**: Story hook with a vulnerable opening creates curiosity. The lesson is specific and actionable (15 discovery calls). Ends with a question to drive comments. "Building the wrong thing" resonates with the product/startup LinkedIn audience.

## Edge Cases

- **User has no existing content to repurpose**: Start with content pillars and original post ideas based on their expertise and audience
- **Multiple platforms requested**: Adapt each post to platform norms — don't just cross-post the same copy
- **Personal brand vs. company brand**: Adjust voice (first-person stories for personal, "we" for company); personal brand content typically gets higher engagement
- **No conversation data available**: Use user-provided context and industry trends; recommend recording meetings and talks as a content source
- **User is just starting out**: Focus on documenting their journey, curating/commenting on industry content, and engaging heavily with established accounts

## Best Practices

- The hook is everything — spend 50% of your effort on the first line
- When Harmony data is available, turn real meeting insights into anonymized, authentic posts
- One idea per post; if you need "and," split it into two posts
- Engagement begets engagement — comment on others' content daily
- Repurpose aggressively; the same insight can work across 5 platforms in 5 formats
- Schedule core content, post real-time reactions live
- Test hook styles and track what works — let data guide your playbook
- Promotional content should be less than 10% of total output
- Authenticity beats polish on every platform
